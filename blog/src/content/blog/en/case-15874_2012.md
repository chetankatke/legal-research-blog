## Summary
In this criminal appeal, the Supreme Court of India addressed the conviction of the appellants under Sections 302 read with 120-B of the Indian Penal Code (murder in furtherance of criminal conspiracy), for which they were sentenced to life imprisonment. The case arose from an incident on 6 September 1996, where the deceased Sant Kumar Sinha and the informant were stopped by six persons, including the appellants, who assaulted the deceased with a knife and shot him at point-blank range in connection with a dispute over an institute run by co-accused Binod Kumar. The High Court of Jharkhand had affirmed the trial court's conviction. The Supreme Court was called upon to examine the evidentiary basis for the conviction, particularly the role of each accused in the conspiracy and the murder. (
## Key Holdings
- The appellants were convicted under Sections 302 read with 120-B IPC and sentenced to life imprisonment for the murder committed pursuant to a criminal conspiracy.
- The High Court of Jharkhand at Ranchi affirmed the conviction and sentence passed by the 3rd Additional Sessions Judge, Dhanbad, in Sessions Trial No. 112 of 1997.
- An absconder co-accused, Yakub @ Ayub, was tried separately under S.T. No. 405 of 1998 after being apprehended later, while the other accused faced trial together.
- The prosecution established the offence through the testimony of the informant/complainant, Dr. Gopal Prasad Sinha (PW.7), who witnessed the incident at Sant Nirankari Chowk on 6.9.1996.
- The FIR was registered under Sections 302/120-B/379 IPC and Section 27 of the Arms Act, 1959, based on the Fardbeyan of the informant.
