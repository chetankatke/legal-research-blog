## Summary
**Summary:**

This case involves cross-appeals before the Supreme Court of India arising from a Rajasthan High Court judgment concerning the calculation of royalty payable by Hindustan Zinc Ltd. for the extraction of lead and zinc from leased lands in districts Bhilwara, Rajsamand, and Udaipur. The core legal question is the proper methodology for determining royalty on mineral extraction under the relevant mining lease and concession rules. The State of Rajasthan challenged the High Court's direction that the royalty be re-calculated, contending it would result in substantial revenue loss, while Hindustan Zinc Ltd. challenged the very direction requiring re-calculation. The High Court had previously quashed the additional royalty demands raised by the State through notices dated December 2001 and January 2002, and the order of the revisional authority upholding those notices. Both appeals were heard together, with the State's appeal being treated as the primary matter for determination.
