# 20801_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This appeal arose from a land acquisition dispute where the Kerala High Court had awarded compensation of Rs. 77,000/- per Are to the appellant, Ponnappan, for his acquired lands. The Supreme Court, considering the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, found this compensation inadequate and granted an enhancement of Rs. 1,48,000/- along with all other statutory benefits over and above the amount already awarded. The Court allowed the appeal and directed payment of the enhanced compensation to the appellant. Notably, the Court clarified that this order was fact-specific and shall not be treated as a precedent in any other case.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court enhanced the compensation awarded by the High Court by an additional sum of Rs. 1,48,000/- over and above the amount already granted, based on the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case.
- The appellant was held entitled to the enhanced compensation along with all other statutory benefits arising from the land acquisition.
- The judgment is specifically declared not to be treated as a precedent in any other case.
- The appeal was allowed on the ground that the compensation fixed by the High Court at Rs. 77,000/- per Are was inadequate in the facts of the case.

Rs.1,48,000/- required to be granted.  
4.
In view of the above, the appeal is allowed. We direct 
an enhancement of a total compensation in a sum of Rs.1,48,000/- 
with  all  other  statutory  benefits  to  the  appellant  over  and 
above the amount already awarded by the High Court.
