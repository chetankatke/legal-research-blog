## Summary
This is a brief procedural order of the Supreme Court of India in *Manohar Lal (D) through LRs v. Ugrasen (D) through LRs & Ors.*, dated January 14, 2013. The matter arose from I.A. Nos. 1 & 2 of 2012 filed in a Curative Petition (C) No. D 36053 of 2011, which itself was preferred against Civil Appeal No. 973 of 2007. The sole legal question before the Court was whether to grant permission to withdraw these interlocutory applications, which the applicants' counsel sought on instructions. A bench comprising Justices H.L. Dattu and Ranjan Gogoi allowed the withdrawal and disposed of the IAs as withdrawn, without adjudicating any substantive issue.
## Key Holdings
- Permission to withdraw interlocutory applications may be granted on the basis of instructions received from the applicant through counsel.
- Interlocutory applications that are withdrawn stand disposed of as withdrawn, without the court adjudicating their merits.
- The Court has the discretion to allow withdrawal of pending interlocutory applications filed in a curative petition.
- Withdrawal of interlocutory applications does not necessarily affect the main curative petition, which may continue independently.
- Procedural orders disposing of withdrawn applications do not constitute binding precedent on the substantive issues raised in the underlying matter.
