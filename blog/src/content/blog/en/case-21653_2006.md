## Summary
This batch of civil appeals by M/s. Bangalore Club arose from a common judgment of the Karnataka High Court, raising the question of whether the doctrine of mutuality exempts interest income earned by an unincorporated Association of Persons (AOP) on fixed deposits maintained with its corporate member banks from income tax. The Assessing Officer had rejected the claim on the ground that there was no identity between contributors and participators, but both the CIT(A) and the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal held in favour of the assessee, finding that the funds were lent to corporate members and the concept of mutuality applied. The Supreme Court was called upon to decide whether the Tribunal's view—that interest earned on deposits placed with member banks was protected by mutuality, while interest from non-member banks was properly taxable—was legally correct.
