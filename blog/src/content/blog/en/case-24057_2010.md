## Summary
**Summary:**

This case arises from a dispute concerning the seniority of Ashok Kumar Srivastava, a Lecturer in Ras Shastra at a government Ayurvedic college in Uttar Pradesh, who was promoted to the post of Reader. The U.P. Public Service Commission recommended his promotion in 2005 for a vacancy that had arisen on 31.7.2001 (upon the superannuation of Dr. Hari Shanker Pandey
## Key Holdings
- The respondent was appointed as a Lecturer in 1996 and the Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission recommended him for promotion to the post of Reader on 15.6.2005, placing him at serial No. 6.
- The State Government issued an office memorandum on 16.8.2005 promoting the respondent and granting him seniority only with effect from the date of the promotion order.
- The U.P. State Public Service Tribunal directed the respondent to submit a representation, which the State rejected on 2.1.2008, holding that seniority was to be reckoned from the date of the promotion order.
- The respondent contended before the High Court that he was entitled to retrospective seniority from the date the vacancy arose, namely after the superannuation of Dr. Hari Shanker Pandey on 31.7.2001.
- The High Court initially directed the Public Service Commission to expedite the promotion process within six months due to long-pending vacancies in the promotional post of Reader.
