# 13647_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** August 30, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court allowed the appeal filed by R. Jayanthi, who had been convicted by the Karnataka High Court under Sections 7 and 13(1)(d) read with Section 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, after being acquitted by the Trial Court. The legal question centered on the denial of proper legal representation, as the appellant's case papers were transferred to a new advocate at the last moment, and he failed to appear when the matter was taken up. The Court accepted the appellant's explanation regarding the lack of representation and set aside the High Court's judgment solely on this ground. The appeal was restored to the Karnataka High Court's file with a direction to dispose of it at the earliest.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's judgment because the appellant was denied proper legal representation when the case papers were transferred to another advocate at the last moment who failed to appear.
- The right to legal representation is a critical component of fair hearing, and its denial vitiates the proceedings before the appellate court.
- The criminal appeal was restored to the Karnataka High Court for fresh consideration and disposal at the earliest possible time.
- The Court accepted the appellant's explanation for non-representation, recognizing that such procedural lapses cannot be permitted to cause irreparable prejudice to a party's case.
- The acquittal by the Trial Court under Sections 7 and 13(1)(d) read with Section 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 was reversed by the High Court, and the matter was remanded for reconsideration.
