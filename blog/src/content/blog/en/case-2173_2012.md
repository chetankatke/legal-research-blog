## Summary
**Summary:**

This is a brief procedural order from the Supreme Court of India in Criminal Appeal No. 401 of 2012, involving the appellant Raghu @ Raghavendra against the State of Karnataka. The appellant's counsel, acting on instructions, sought permission to withdraw the criminal appeal, which the Court duly granted. The appeal was consequently dismissed as withdrawn, and the connected miscellaneous petition (CRL.M.P. No. 13868 of 2013) was disposed of accordingly. The order, dated July 18, 2013, was passed by Justices H.L. Dattu and Dipak Misra, and no substantive legal question was adjudicated due to the withdrawal.
## Key Holdings
- Permission to withdraw a criminal appeal may be granted on the appellant's instructions through learned counsel.
- A criminal appeal that is withdrawn with the court's permission stands dismissed as withdrawn.
- Connected miscellaneous petitions arising from a withdrawn criminal appeal are disposed of accordingly.
