## Summary
In this gift tax appeal, the Supreme Court addressed whether a revocable transfer of 6000 equity shares of Hero Cycles Ltd. (executed in 1982 and later revoked in 1988) could be treated as a valid gift, with the assessee arguing the ITAT was correct in quashing the gift-tax assessment. The legal question before the High Court was whether the ITAT erred in law in setting aside the gift-tax assessment, given that the deed allowed the assessee an 8-month window to revoke the transfer. The Gift Tax Officer had treated the transaction as a colorable device to reduce tax liability (relying on *McDowell & Co.*), and the High Court had set aside the ITAT's order to uphold the assessment. ## Key Holdings
- A revocable transfer of shares, where the donor retains a window period to revoke the gift, is not a valid gift and is liable to gift tax.
- Bonus shares allotted to the transferee during the subsistence of the revocable transfer do not revert to the donor upon revocation, as they fall outside the scope of the original gift deed.
- The deed of revocable transfer expressly excluded bonus and right shares from the gift, so only the original 6000 equity shares were covered by the transfer.
- Where a revocable transaction is entered into primarily to reduce tax liability, it cannot be accepted as a genuine gift for assessment purposes.
- The High Court rightly set aside the ITAT's order and upheld the gift tax assessment against the assessee.
