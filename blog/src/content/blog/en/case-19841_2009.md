## Summary
This case, *Suresh Kumar Koushal v. Naz Foundation* (2013), arose from appeals against the Delhi High Court's decision in a PIL filed by the Naz Foundation NGO, which had declared Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code unconstitutional insofar as it criminalized consensual sexual acts between adults in private, on the grounds that it violated Articles 14, 15, and 21 of the Constitution. The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the High Court erred in reading down Section 377 and whether the provision was constitutionally valid. The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's judgment, holding that the High Court overstepped its jurisdiction and that judicial review of a parliamentary enactment should be approached with great caution, particularly on matters involving the "wisdom" of legislating morality. The Court ruled that Section 377 did not suffer from any constitutional infirmity and restored the provision in its original form, leaving any amendment to the discretion of the legislature.
