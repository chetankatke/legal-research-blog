## Summary
**Summary:**

This case concerns whether the sole surviving partner of a two-partner firm can continue to prosecute a suit originally filed by the partnership after the firm's dissolution caused by the death of the other partner. The partnership deed provided that if a deceased partner's legal representative declined to join, the firm would stand dissolved and the surviving partner could continue the business as a proprietary concern. ## Key Holdings
- On the death of a partner during pendency of a suit, the partnership stands dissolved if the legal representative of the deceased partner fails to express interest in continuing as a partner within the stipulated period.
- The sole surviving partner of a dissolved partnership firm is entitled to continue the business of the firm as a proprietary concern, with all interests of the firm devolving upon him.
- The surviving partner is entitled to continue prosecuting the suit originally filed by the partnership firm, and the plaint must be amended accordingly to reflect the change in constitution.
- A clause in a partnership deed providing for dissolution upon failure of the legal representative of a deceased partner to join within the prescribed time is valid and enforceable.
- The trial court rightly allowed the surviving partner to continue the suit as a proprietary concern and to recall a witness for further examination to meet the ends of justice.
