## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal arose from a challenge by Awani Kumar Upadhyay, an Additional District and Sessions Judge, to severe strictures and observations made against him by the Allahabad High Court in a Second Appeal (U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad v. Lajja Ram), in which the High Court had also recommended that the Chief Justice consider initiating disciplinary proceedings against him. The central legal question was whether the High Court could justify passing adverse strictures against a judicial officer who had merely recorded evidence and submitted findings on remand from the first appellate court, without having rendered any decision as either the trial court judge or the first appellate judge. ## Key Holdings
- The appellant's challenge was confined to the strictures portion of the High Court's judgment, not the merits of the underlying second appeal.
- The appellant, a judicial officer, was not the original trial court or first appellate court judge but was merely directed by the first appellate court to record evidence on remanded issues.
- The High Court's strictures against the appellant were challenged on the ground that the Court did not furnish any independent finding on the issues determined by him or address his ultimate decision.
- Strictures passed by a High Court against a subordinate judicial officer, which may permanently affect reputation and service career, can be subjected to appellate scrutiny by the Supreme Court.
- The High Court had forwarded a copy of its judgment to the Chief Justice to consider whether disciplinary proceedings against the appellant were warranted.
