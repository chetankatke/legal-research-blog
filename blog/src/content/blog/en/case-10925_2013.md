# 10925_2013

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 10.01.2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This appeal by the State of Madhya Pradesh challenged the High Court's order granting anticipatory bail to Pradeep Sharma and others accused of murdering Rajesh Singh Thakur by allegedly serving him poisoned milk rabri (sweet dish) at a tea house in Chhindwara, in connection with an FIR registered under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC. The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's order, holding that the grant of anticipatory bail was unjustified given the gravity of the offence, the fact that the accused were absconding since the date of the incident, and the necessity of custodial interrogation as previously noted by the High Court itself in its earlier rejection of bail. The Court emphasized that in serious offences like murder, particularly where the accused have evaded arrest for over a year, anticipatory bail should not be granted as a matter of course. The appeals were accordingly allowed and the High Court's orders granting anticipatory bail were set aside.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court held that anticipatory bail should not be granted in serious offences like murder under Section 302 IPC where custodial interrogation is found necessary.
- The Court emphasized that the mere fact that an accused is absconding and arrest warrants have been issued against him is a relevant consideration against granting anticipatory bail.
- Granting anticipatory bail to accused persons involved in a premeditated conspiracy to commit murder by poisoning is unjustified and cannot be sustained in law.
- The High Court erred in granting anticipatory bail without properly considering the gravity of the offence, the nature of the conspiracy, and the fact that the accused were declared absconders during investigation.
- Custodial interrogation of an accused in a murder case is necessary for fair investigation and is a relevant ground to reject anticipatory bail.
