## Summary
This case, Sarah Mathew v. The Institute of Cardio Vascular Diseases, was placed before a Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court of India to resolve a conflict between two earlier decisions on the computation of the limitation period under Section 468 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The core legal question is whether the relevant date for computing limitation is the date of filing of the complaint/institution of criminal proceedings, or the date on which the Magistrate takes cognizance. The two-Judge Bench in Bharat Damodar Kale held that the relevant date is the date of filing the complaint, while a three-Judge Bench in Krishna Pillai (concerning Section 9 of the Child Marriage Restraint Act, 1929) appeared to take cognizance as the relevant date, creating an apparent conflict. The Constitution Bench was constituted to authoritatively resolve this conflict, framing the specific question of which date triggers the limitation period under Section 468 Cr.P.C.
