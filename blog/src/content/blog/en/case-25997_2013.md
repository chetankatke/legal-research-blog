## Summary
In this suo motu contempt proceeding against Advocate-on-Record (AOR) Rameshwar Prasad Goyal, the Supreme Court confronted the issue of AORs who enter appearance in large numbers of cases but refuse to appear before the Court, thereby lending their signatures without assuming any responsibility. The contempt arose when the AOR, having filed a restoration application in *Mohamed Israfil v. Raufunessa Bibi*, refused to come to Court despite being specifically called upon to explain the factual controversy, even though he had entered appearance in over 1,400 cases in each of the preceding years.
