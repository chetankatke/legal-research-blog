## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court case involves Criminal Appeals Nos. 14 and 15 of 2007 arising from convictions under Sections 498-A, 304-B, and 201/34 IPC (dowry harassment, dowry death, and causing disappearance of evidence) affirmed by the Punjab and Haryana High Court. The prosecution's case against appellant Rajinder Singh was that his wife Santosh Kaur was subjected to harassment and demands for additional dowry of Rs. 25,000 by her husband and in-laws shortly after her marriage in April 1992, leading to her death in January 1993.
