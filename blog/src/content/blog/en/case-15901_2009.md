# 15901_2009

## Citation

- **Date:** 13-11-1987

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court arose from a dispute concerning the entitlement of two retired employees of the Ara Municipal Corporation—Ramashish Prasad (retired 31.08.1996) and Vishwanath Ram (retired 31.03.1999)—to pension under the Bihar Municipal Officers and Servants Pension Rules, 1987, which were notified on 13.11.1987 but adopted by the Municipal Corporation only on 19.06.200
## Key Holdings
- The Bihar Municipal Officers and Servants Pension Rules, 1987 came into effect on 13 November 1987 when notified in the Gazette, making them applicable to all permanent employees of Municipalities and Notified Area Committees from that date.
- The right of an employee to pension under the Rules is dependent upon the exercise of an option for pension as required by Rule 4 of the Rules.
- Employees who superannuated after 13 November 1987 but failed to exercise their option for pension under Rule 4 are not entitled to claim pensionary benefits under the Rules.
- The Ara Municipal Corporation's failure to implement the Rules until 19 June 2004 did not override the statutory requirement for employees to exercise their option for pension within the prescribed manner.
