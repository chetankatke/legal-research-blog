# 23160_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
In this case, the Supreme Court addressed matrimonial and property disputes arising from an alleged marriage between two minors, where the respondent sought restraint on remarriage, a share in the appellant's property, and restitution of conjugal rights under Section 9 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955. The High Court had held the marriage void under Section 5(iii) of the HMA due to the parties' minority and made observations suggesting the respondent could initiate criminal prosecution against the appellant under Section 376 IPC. The Supreme Court criticized the High Court's approach, holding that it was unjustified to make such criminal prosecution observations when only maintenance and restitution of conjugal rights were sought. The Court emphasized that in matrimonial disputes, courts should encourage reconciliation through conciliation or mediation, and that even an illegitimate child is entitled to maintenance, rather than pushing the parties into further conflict.
## Key Holdings
- A marriage solemnized in contravention of Section 5(iii) of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, where a party is a minor, is void.
- Suits filed by a minor are not maintainable and the court has no jurisdiction to decide them on merits.
- An illegitimate child is entitled to maintenance.
- The High Court was not justified in making observations about initiation of criminal proceedings under Section 376 IPC when the relief sought was only restitution of conjugal rights and maintenance.
- In matrimonial disputes, courts should encourage reconciliation and refer the parties to conciliation or mediation.
