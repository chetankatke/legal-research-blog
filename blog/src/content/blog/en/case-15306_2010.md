# 15306_2010

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court arose from the termination of an employee, Brijesh Vipin Chandra Shah, who was appointed on compassionate grounds as a Junior Clerk in 1998 but failed to clear the mandatory post-training examination for Class III posts in all three chances (plus one additional chance granted by the High Court). The principal legal question was whether any relaxation could be granted in the mandatory qualifications prescribed under the Gujarat Non-Secretariat Clerks (Direct Recruitment Procedure) Rules, 1990 for recruitment to a Class III post. The Supreme Court held that no relaxation could be granted in the mandatory qualifications for the Class III post, and accordingly dismissed the appeal, while noting the appellant's alternative prayer for being accommodated in a Class IV post on compassionate grounds (the final outcome of which is not discernible from the excerpt).
