## Summary
This appeal arises from a murder case in Tamil Nadu where six accused (A1–A6) attacked the deceased Babu with a sickle on 18.04.2001, following enmity rooted in a love triangle and prior alter
## Key Holdings
- The High Court rightly acquitted A4 to A6 due to lack of evidence connecting them to the offence while confirming the conviction of A1 to A3.
- The conviction of A1, A2 and A3 under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC was sustained as they collectively inflicted indiscriminate cuts on the deceased inside the shed.
- Mere presence or association with the accused is insufficient to convict when there is no evidence of participation in the commission of the offence.
- The prosecution must establish each accused's individual role beyond reasonable doubt, even where a common intention under Section 34 IPC is alleged.
- The prior enmity between the accused and the deceased, coupled with the pre-mediated attack, established the requisite mens rea for the offence of murder.
