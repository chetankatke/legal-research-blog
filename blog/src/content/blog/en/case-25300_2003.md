## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a dispute over the transfer of 3,530 equity shares of Peerless General Finance Company, which Bhagwati Developers had agreed to acquire from Tuhin Kanti Ghose as repayment of a Rs. 38,83,000 loan advanced in 1986 for purchasing those shares. The central legal question concerns the rights of the transferee to bonus shares subsequently issued by the company when the original transfer deeds were not properly executed and the transferor retained the bonus entitlements. The parties had previously settled their dispute through a compromise agreement dated 21 November 1994, and the matter was before the Supreme Court challenging the concurrent findings of the Company Law Board and the Company Judge confirming that settlement. The judgment excerpt is incomplete, so the final holding cannot be determined from the provided text, though the case appears to turn on enforcement of the compromise agreement and the proper share transfer mechanism under company law.
## Key Holdings
- Where transfer deeds are not properly filled in and executed, the transferor remains the registered shareholder and retains legal title to the shares.
- Bonus shares allotted to a registered shareholder in the absence of a specific agreement to the contrary belong to that registered shareholder and not the intended transferee.
- A loan advanced on the understanding that shares would be transferred as repayment constitutes valid consideration, but the transferee must ensure proper execution of transfer deeds to perfect title.
- Once shares are transferred by a registered shareholder to another, the transferee bears the responsibility of obtaining duly executed transfer deeds to complete the transfer.
- A settlement agreement between parties acknowledging a prior sale of shares can resolve disputes regarding share ownership and related bonus entitlements.
