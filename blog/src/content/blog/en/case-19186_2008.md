# 19186_2008

## Summary
This Supreme Court criminal appeal concerns the conviction of Sahib Hussain for the murder of five members of a family (Seema, three children, and Munna) on 27.10.2006, following a prior scuffle with Seema on Eid. The trial court convicted him under Section 302 IPC and imposed the death sentence based on circumstantial evidence, but the High Court commuted the death sentence to life imprisonment while maintaining the conviction. The Supreme Court was called upon to determine whether the circumstantial evidence was sufficient to sustain the conviction and whether the death sentence warranted commutation. (Note: The provided excerpt is incomplete, so the final holding of the Supreme Court is not visible in the text.)
## Key Holdings
- The High Court commuted the death sentence awarded by the trial court to imprisonment for life in the case of multiple murders under Section 302 IPC.
- The conviction was sustained based on circumstantial evidence establishing the appellant's guilt for killing five persons.
- The court took into account the prior scuffle between the appellant and the deceased on the day of Eid as relevant to the sequence of events leading to the murders.
- The death reference under Section 366 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 was considered alongside the criminal appeals challenging the conviction and sentence.
