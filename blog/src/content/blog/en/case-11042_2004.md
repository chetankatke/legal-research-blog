# 11042_2004

## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from a dispute regarding higher pay scales for English and Hindi typists in the South Eastern Railway, which had been granted by the Central Administrative Tribunal and affirmed by the Calcutta High Court. Relying squarely on its earlier judgment in *Union of India v. Arun Jyoti Kundu* (2007) 7 SCC 472, the Court held that the Tribunal had exceeded its jurisdiction by issuing such a direction, since the Fifth Pay Commission had not recommended the higher scale for these typists. The Court reasoned that
## Key Holdings
- The Central Administrative Tribunal exceeded its jurisdiction by issuing directions regarding higher pay scales to typists that the Fifth Pay Commission had not recommended.
- It is for the Government to act on the report of the Pay Commission, including deciding whether to accept or reject its recommendations and determining the effective date of implementation.
- Granting a higher pay scale to English and Hindi typists with effect from 31.1.2000 based on the Anomalies Committee's recommendations did not constitute discrimination or arbitrary action beyond the Government's power.
- The High Court was in error in failing to set aside the Tribunal's directions that exceeded its jurisdiction, and accordingly the appeal was allowed and the impugned High Court order was set aside.
