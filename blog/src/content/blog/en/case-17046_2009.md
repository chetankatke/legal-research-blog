# 17046_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 01.04.2008
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court heard an appeal against the Madhya Pradesh High Court's judgment, which had reduced the appellant's sentence for an offense under Section 376 IPC (rape) from 10 years to 7 years rigorous imprisonment. The legal question before the Court was whether further reduction of sentence was warranted, given that the appellant had already undergone the 7-year sentence awarded by the High Court. The Court directed the appellant's immediate release, clarifying that if he had not yet completed 7 years, the sentence would be reduced to the period already undergone, and expressly stated that this order shall not be treated as a precedent in any other case.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court reduced the appellant's sentence under Section 376 IPC to the period already undergone, since the appellant had already served the 7-year sentence imposed by the High Court.
- The Court directed that the appellant be released forthwith if not required in any other case.
- The Court held that delay in filing the appeal stood condoned and leave to appeal was granted.
- The Court clarified that this order shall not be treated as a precedent in any other case.
