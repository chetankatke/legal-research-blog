## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court directed the Charity Commissioner to adjudicate on all questions pertaining to the merger of trust and other pending disputes as expeditiously as possible.
- Change Reports filed under the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 are the proper mechanism to record changes affecting a registered public trust, including mergers arising from the unification of churches.
- A registered religious society under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, when also registered as a public trust, has its property vested with its Property Committee, and changes to such vesting must be duly reflected in Change Reports.
- The legal consequences of the unification of churches (such as the formation of the Church of North India from six predecessor churches) must be determined by the competent authority under the applicable trust and charity laws.
- The factual background regarding the unification process commencing from 1929 and the relevant trust and society registrations is integral to adjudicating disputes over trust property post-merger.
