# 18546_2006

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 24.11.2005
- **Judges:** H.L, RANJAN GOGOI
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court dealt with two connected criminal appeals arising from the High Court of Rajasthan's judgment in D.B. Criminal Appeal No. 119 of 2004 involving respondent Nathi Singh, who had been convicted of offences punishable under Sections 365 (kidnapping) and 384 (extortion) of the IPC. In Criminal Appeal No. 644/2008 filed by the State of Rajasthan, the Court held that neither the High Court nor the trial court committed any error warranting interference, and accordingly dismissed the State's appeal. In the connected Criminal Appeal No. 645/2008 filed by Nathi Singh, the Court noted that since the accused had failed to secure bail and must have already served out his sentence, the appeal had become infructuous and was disposed of on that ground.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court declined to interfere with the concurrent findings of the High Court and trial court, finding no error warranting appellate intervention.
- An appeal becomes infructuous and is liable to be dismissed when the appellant has already served out the sentence imposed by the lower courts during the pendency of the appeal.
- Where the accused was denied bail and consequently must have served the sentence imposed for offences under Sections 365 and 384 of the IPC, the appeal challenging the conviction survives nothing for consideration.
- The appellate court's power of interference with concurrent findings of fact recorded by the trial court and the High Court is limited absent any legal or factual error.
