## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal (Krishnan v. State of Haryana) concerns the constitutional validity and application of Section 32-A of the NDPS Act, 1985, which prohibits any suspension, remission, or commutation of sentences awarded under the Act. The High Court had upheld the denial of remission to the appellants, holding that Section 32-A has overriding effect over the Cr.P.C. and the Punjab Jail Manual, and that remission provisions are governed by statute rather than Article 161 of the Constitution. ## Key Holdings
- Section 32-A of the NDPS Act prohibits suspension, remission, or commutation of any sentence awarded under the Act, notwithstanding anything in the CrPC or any other law in force.
- The validity of Section 32-A of the NDPS Act was challenged as being arbitrary, discriminatory, and violative of Articles 14 and 21 of the Constitution by creating an unreasonable distinction between NDPS convicts and other prisoners.
- The appellants contended that Section 32-A negates the statutory provisions of Sections 389, 432, and 433 of the CrPC by taking away the judicial power of courts to suspend or remit sentences.
- The legislature cannot, by statutory prohibition, take away the judicial function of the appellate court in deciding whether a sentence under the NDPS Act can be suspended, otherwise rendering the substantive right of appeal illusory.
- The restriction imposed under Section 32-A upon the executive is asserted to be for a reasonable purpose and object, thereby justifying the overriding effect of the provision over general remission rules.
