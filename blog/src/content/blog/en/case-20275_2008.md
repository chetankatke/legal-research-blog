## Summary
This is a brief disposal order in Criminal Appeal No. 1840 of 2013 before the Supreme Court of India, involving Ashok Kumar Aggarwal as the appellant and CBI as a respondent. The bench of Justices Dr. B.S. Chauhan and S.A. Bobde held that the appeal need not be dealt with separately, as it involved the same parties as Criminal Appeal No. 1837 of 2013. Accordingly, the appeal was disposed of in terms of the judgment rendered in that connected case, without independent adjudication on the merits. The judgment was delivered on November 22, 2013, and was classified as non-reportable.
## Key Holdings
- The appeal was disposed of in terms of the judgment delivered in the connected Criminal Appeal No. 1837 of 2013 between the same parties.
- The court deemed it unnecessary to deal separately with the appeal where a connected appeal between the same parties had already been decided.
- The judgment was rendered by a bench comprising Dr. B.S. Chauhan, J. and S.A. Bobde, J. under the Supreme Court's Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction.
- The matter was adjudicated as a non-reportable judgment, indicating it did not lay down any new precedent of general application.
- The appeal was decided on November 22, 2013, at New Delhi, with the bench formally signing the order on the date of pronouncement.
