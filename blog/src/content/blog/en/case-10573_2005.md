# The Sessions Court mainly relied on the decision of this Court in Kamta Tiwari vs. State of Madhya Pradesh

## Citation

- **Date:** 23.12.2002
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In *State of Rajasthan v. Jamil Khan*, the Supreme Court addressed whether the High Court erred in commuting a death sentence to life imprisonment in a case involving the brutal rape and murder of a five-year-old girl, where the Sessions Court had relied on the precedent of *Kamta Tiwari v. State of Madhya Pradesh*. The State appealed contending that the case warranted the death penalty, raising key legal questions including the penological approach in particularly shocking murders, the scope of a High Court's consideration of a Death Reference under Chapter XXVIII of the CrPC, and any restrictions on remission or commutation powers under Sections 432 and 433 CrPC. The High Court had declined to confirm the death sentence without articulating aggravating/mitigating circumstances or special reasons
## Key Holdings
- The Sessions Court relied on Kamta Tiwari vs. State of Madhya Pradesh, which held that rape and murder of a child warrants the death sentence.
- Certain murders shock the conscience of the court and community and require a distinct penological approach, potentially warranting the death penalty.
- When considering a Death Reference, the High Court must refer to the aggravating and mitigating circumstances and record special reasons before declining to confirm a death sentence.
- A case falls within the rarest of rare category warranting death sentence based on the brutal nature of the crime and the aggravating circumstances present.
