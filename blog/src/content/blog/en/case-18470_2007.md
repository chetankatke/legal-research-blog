## Summary
In *Kulwant Singh & Ors. v. State of Punjab*, the Supreme Court addressed whether the conviction of the husband, his father, and his mother for offences under Sections 304-B (dowry death) and 498-A (cruelty) of the IPC should be sustained, in connection with the suspicious death of Rachhpal Kaur on 14 October 1988. The deceased had been continuously harassed and maltreated for bringing insufficient dowry despite having given sufficient dowry at the time of her marriage in 1984, and Panchayat intervention had failed to resolve the matter. Post-mortem and chemical examination revealed fatal aluminium phosphide poisoning, with the deceased also carrying a 26-week fetus at the time of death. The Court held that there was sufficient evidence on record to sustain the conviction, affirming the appellants' guilt under both statutory provisions.
