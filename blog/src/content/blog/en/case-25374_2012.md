# 25374_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 22.4.2008
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This appeal by the State of Jammu & Kashmir arises from a High Court order directing the appointing authority to examine the claim of Sat Pal, a Scheduled Caste candidate on the select/waiting list for Junior Engineer (Civil) Grade-II posts, for appointment against vacancies arising because higher-ranked candidates (including Trilok Nath) failed to join despite being offered appointment. The central legal question concerns whether a candidate lower in the merit/select list is entitled to appointment when candidates ranked above decline to join, given the rule that the waiting list remains valid for one year. Notably, the State Government failed to file objections before the High Court despite being given the opportunity, leading the High Court to accept the respondent's assertions as truthful. The Supreme Court, having granted leave, was called upon to determine the legality of the High Court's direction in light of the unchallenged factual record and the applicable waiting list rules.
## Key Holdings
- The merit list of candidates in continuation of those offered appointment constitutes a valid waiting list for a period of one year, during which unfilled vacancies must be offered to the next eligible candidates.
- When a candidate higher in the merit/select list declines or fails to join the offered post, the immediate next candidate on the waiting list is entitled to be considered for appointment against the resulting vacancy.
- A party's failure to file objections despite notice from the court results in closure of its right to object, warranting the inference that the opposing party's assertions are truthful and accepted for determination.
