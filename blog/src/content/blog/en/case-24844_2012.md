## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Rajamani v. State of Kerala*, the Supreme Court addressed the limited question of quantum of sentence imposed on a truck driver convicted under Section 55(a) of the Kerala Abkari Act for transporting 218 plastic cans containing approximately 7,194 litres of spirit. While the Trial Court had sentenced him to 7 years' imprisonment with a Rs. 1 lakh fine, and the High Court reduced the term to 5 years while enhancing the fine to Rs. 2 lakhs, the Supreme Court observed that the appellant was merely a driver by profession with no ownership or financial interest in the contraband
## Key Holdings
- The large quantity of contraband carried by the accused is a relevant factor in determining the quantum of sentence under Section 55(a) of the Kerala Abkari Act.
- Whether the accused was the owner of the contraband or had any financial interest in its possession or transportation is an equally important consideration for sentencing.
- The mere fact that the appellant was a driver transporting the contraband, without any ownership or financial interest, warranted reduction of sentence from seven years to five years despite the large quantity involved.
- The Investigating Officer must make genuine efforts to identify the real culprits behind the purchase and transport of contraband, including the consignor and consignee, rather than confining the prosecution to the driver of the vehicle.
