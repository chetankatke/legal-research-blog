## Summary
This criminal appeal from the Supreme Court of India arose from a 1998 incident in Kapurthala, Punjab, in which two appellants—Manjit Singh and his father-in-law Paramjit Singh—were tried along with three co-accused for offences under Sections 302 and 307 read with Section 34 IPC, arising from an election-related attack on two brothers that resulted in the death of Jagmohan Singh and injuries to Amarjot Singh. The prosecution's case, supported by eyewitness testimony and forensic evidence, established that Manjit Singh fired a .315 bore rifle shot that struck Jagmohan Singh on the right cheek, killing him, while Paramjit Singh and another accused also fired at the victims.
