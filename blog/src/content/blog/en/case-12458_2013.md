# 12458_2013

## Citation

## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court addressed whether the High Court was justified in directing the Electricity Department to restore electricity connection to M/s Sri Kanakadurga Castings on payment of a paltry sum of Rs. 18,89,244/-, which was only the amount admitted by the industry. The Court noted that the assessed electrical charges outstanding against the respondent-industry were a substantial Rs. 28,54,63,320/-, and given the vast disparity between the admitted amount and the assessed liability, directing restoration on such minimal payment was not "just and proper." The Court set aside the impugned interim and contempt orders of the High Court and requested the High Court to dispose of the writ appeal on merits within six months.
