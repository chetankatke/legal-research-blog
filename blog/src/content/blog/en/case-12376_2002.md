## Summary
This case involves a batch of civil appeals filed by four cement manufacturing companies challenging a common judgment of the Madras High Court concerning the levy of royalty and dead rent on mining leases over patta lands in Tamil Nadu. The central legal question is whether the government was justified in discontinuing the long-standing convention of charging only 50% of the statutory royalty and dead rent rates for mining operations on patta lands, and instead collecting the full rates prescribed under the Second and Third Schedules of the Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957. The dispute arose from a 1982 Government letter directing collectors to stop sharing 50% of royalty and dead rent with patta landholders, following an audit objection by the Senior Deputy Accountant General
