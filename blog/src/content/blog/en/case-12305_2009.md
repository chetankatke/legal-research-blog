## Summary
**Summary:**

In this appeal arising from a dispute over a forged will, the Supreme Court (per R.M. Lodha, J.) addressed whether the High Court was justified in staying a civil suit pending the conclusion of a related criminal case. The appellant had filed both an FIR against respondents for forgery and fabrication of a will, and a civil suit seeking declaration of title, possession, and annulment of the sale deed, in which the trial court had refused to stay proceedings under Section 10, CPC, but the High Court reversed that order and granted the stay. Relying on the Constitution Bench decision in *M.S. Sheriff v. State of Madras*, which governs the simultaneous prosecution of civil and criminal proceedings, the Court held
## Key Holdings
- Civil and criminal proceedings are independent of each other, and the pendency of a criminal case does not automatically warrant a stay of a related civil suit.
- Section 10 of the CPC permits a stay of a civil suit during the pendency of a criminal case only when the issues in both proceedings are directly and substantially the same.
- Stay of civil proceedings under Section 10 CPC is not mechanical and must be exercised judicially based on the facts and circumstances of each case.
- The mere fact that allegations of forgery arise in both proceedings is not sufficient to stay the civil suit when the civil and criminal cases involve distinct issues for determination.
