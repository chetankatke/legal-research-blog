## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from a dispute over the unauthorized occupation of a government quarter by S.D. Bandi, a driver in the Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation, who was transferred from Mysore to Mangalore in 1992 but continued to occupy the Mysore quarter despite joining his new posting. After eviction proceedings were upheld by the District Judge under the Karnataka Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1974, the High Court initially allowed the employee's writ petition but later granted him time to vacate by 30.04.2004, prompting the present appeal. The core legal question concerned the prolonged unauthorized retention of government accommodation by members of the State's three branches beyond the prescribed allotment period, and the inefficiency of the existing statutory eviction framework in curbing such retention through successive appeals and writ proceedings. government quarters especially in metropolitan cities where
