# 13470_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 22.02.2011
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed whether the High Court could reverse a trial court's decree in a partition suit without affording an opportunity of hearing to the parties. The Court held that the principles of natural justice required the High Court to grant both parties an opportunity to be heard before reversing the trial court's decree in favor of the petitioners/plaintiffs. Consequently, the Supreme Court set aside the High Court's judgment and remanded the matter for fresh disposal after providing a hearing to both sides, while keeping all contentions on merits open and expressing no opinion on the substantive issues.
## Key Holdings
- A court must afford an opportunity of hearing to the parties before reversing or setting aside a lower court's decree.
- Failure to grant an opportunity of hearing to either party renders the judgment unsustainable and liable to be set aside.
- The matter was remanded to the High Court for fresh disposal in accordance with law, after affording both parties an opportunity of being heard.
- All contentions of both parties were kept open and the Court clarified that no opinion was expressed on the merits or demerits of the case.
- The High Court was directed to dispose of the remanded matter as expeditiously as possible, given its long pendency.
