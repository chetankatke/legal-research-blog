## Summary
In *State of M.P. v. Mohan & Others*, the Supreme Court (Justice K.S. Radhakrishnan) heard the State's appeal against the Madhya Pradesh High Court's order reducing the trial court's sentence of three years' rigorous imprisonment (with a Rs. 1,000 fine) imposed on the accused for offences under Sections 294 and 307 read with Section 34 IPC, to merely the period already undergone. The case arose from a night incident on 11.6
## Key Holdings
- Common intention under Section 34 IPC was established where multiple accused fired gunshots in concert at the complainants to kill them, even though only one accused actually fired.
- The High Court's reduction of sentence to the period already undergone was challenged by the State as being unduly lenient for an offence of attempt to murder committed with common intention.
- Medical evidence of lacerated wounds with blackening around the injuries corroborated the prosecution's case that the injuries were caused by use of firearms.
- The trial court properly inferred the common intention to kill from the circumstances of the accused arriving armed at night and firing multiple shots without provocation at the informant side.
- Concurrent convictions under Sections 294 and 307 read with Section 34 IPC were based on findings that the accused acted pursuant to a shared common intention to cause death of the complainants.
