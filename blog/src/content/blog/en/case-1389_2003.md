## Summary
**Summary:**

This batch of 23 consolidated civil appeals before the Supreme Court arose from disputes between contractors and the Government of Karnataka (and its agencies/instrumentalities) regarding the appointment of arbitrators under contract clauses. Seventeen appeals were filed by contractors challenging the Karnataka High Court's rejection of their prayer for appointment of an Arbitrator to settle disputes arising from works contracts, while five appeals were filed by Karnataka Neeravari Nigam Limited and Krishna Bhagya Jala Nigam Limited challenging orders directing their Chief Engineers to act as Arbitrators. The lead case (Civil Appeal No. 1586 of 2004) involved M/s. P. Dasaratharama Reddy Complex, which had been awarded a 1996 contract for constructing a bridge between Yethabadi-Buyyanadoddi across the Shimsha river in Malavalli, where the contractor alleged non-cooperation by the Chief Engineer and claimed unpaid dues.
