## Summary
In *Lokesh Kumar Jain v. State of Rajasthan*, the Supreme Court heard an appeal against the Rajasthan High Court's refusal to quash FIR No. 10/2000, which had been registered against the appellant under Section 409 IPC (criminal breach of trust) based on an Auditor General's report alleging embezzlement of Rs. 4,39,617 during his tenure as Lower Division Clerk-cum-Cashier in the Office of the District Literacy Education Officer, Dausa, in 1996-97. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court refused to quash FIR No.10/2000 registered against the appellant under Section 409 IPC for alleged financial irregularities committed while serving as Lower Division Clerk-cum-Cashier.
- The FIR was lodged based on the report of the Auditor General disclosing an alleged embezzlement of Rs.4,39,617/- during the appellant's tenure from November 1996 to November 1997.
- The High Court disposed of the Section 482 Cr.PC petition by permitting the petitioner to file representations/documents, directing the Investigating Officer to investigate on the basis thereof and file a progress report before the concerned court.
- The Supreme Court granted leave to appeal against the High Court's order declining to quash the FIR and took up the matter for examining the rival contentions of the parties on the factual background.
