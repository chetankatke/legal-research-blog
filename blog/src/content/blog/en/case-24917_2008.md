## Summary
In *Rajendra Sharma v. State of West Bengal*, the Supreme Court considered a criminal appeal challenging the conviction and 10-year sentence imposed under Sections 395/397 IPC, the Arms Act, and the Explosive Substances Act for a 1998 dacoity at gold jewellery workshops in Cossipore. The appellant's central argument was that, in the absence of any overt act during the dacoity—his role being limited to keeping a taxi nearby while accomplices committed the robbery—the maximum sentence of 10 years was disproportionate. He further contended that, having already served seven years, a sentence reduction was warranted given his limited participation.
