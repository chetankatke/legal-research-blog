## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from a motor vehicle accident where a 19-year-old appellant suffered grievous pelvic and urethral injuries (including total urethral rupture and permanent erectile dysfunction) when respondent No. 1 negligently reversed a Tipper truck into him. ## Key Holdings
- This appeal arises from the recurring problem of abysmally inadequate or tragically disproportionate compensation awarded by Motor Accident Claims Tribunals and High Courts to accident victims.
- A 19-year-old assisting his father in agricultural operations sustained grievous pelvic and urethral injuries (total urethral rupture) causing permanent erectile dysfunction, rendering him unfit for marital life.
- A claimant's loss of earning capacity can be established by showing he was a student who simultaneously contributed to family income through agricultural and dairy activities, even in the absence of formal employment.
- Compensation in motor accident cases must be assessed under multiple heads including medical expenses, loss of earnings, loss of amenities of life, loss of expectation of life, and future medication costs.
- The appellant was entitled to seek enhanced compensation given the permanent nature of his injuries and the disproportionate award given by the lower tribunals.
