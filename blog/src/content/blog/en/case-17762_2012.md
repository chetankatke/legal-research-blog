# 17762_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 7.8.1969

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arose from a challenge to the Calcutta High Court's refusal to interfere under Article 227 with a District Judge's order condoning the delay in filing a revision petition by the State of West Bengal. The core legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the District Judge was justified in condoning the delay in filing the revision, which challenged the executability of a 1969 decree in a suit originating from 1967, where the State had failed to appeal the original decree and the dismissal of its Section 47 CPC objection. The decree-holder had been pursuing litigation for over four decades, and the executing court had dismissed the State's objection as a futile attempt to delay execution. The Supreme Court (per Justice M.Y. Eqbal) was called upon to determine whether such a belated attempt to reopen a long-finalized decree warranted interference with the condonation of delay.
## Key Holdings
- Courts have always exercised discretion in favour of the person seeking condonation of delay in filing an appeal or revision.
