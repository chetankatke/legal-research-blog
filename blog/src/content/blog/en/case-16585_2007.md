# 16585_2007

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 11.04.2007

## Summary
This appeal arises from a case where a young woman, Vandana, was fatally set on fire by her mother-in-law Kesharbai, allegedly at the instigation of her sisters-in-law Ashabai and Kavita (the appellants), due to harassment over her failure to conceive. The trial court and the Bombay High Court convicted the appellants under Section 498-A read with Section 34 IPC (cruelty with common intention) and Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC (murder with common intention), sentencing them to life imprisonment. The central legal issue before the Supreme Court concerned whether the dying declarations (four were recorded) and the evidence sufficiently established the appellants' common intention to abet the murder. The judgment text is excerpted, so the final holding of the Supreme Court is not visible in the provided portion.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court upheld the conviction under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC, holding that the sisters-in-law shared a common intention with the mother-in-law who poured kerosene and set the deceased ablaze.
- Multiple dying declarations (four in this case) recorded by different authorities can be relied upon to establish the guilt of the accused beyond reasonable doubt.
- The accused were rightly convicted under Section 498-A read with Section 34 IPC for the continuous harassment and cruelty inflicted upon the deceased for not bearing a child.
- The act of instigating another to commit the offence, combined with active participation, attracts the doctrine of common intention under Section 34 IPC.
- The High Court was justified in confirming the trial court's conviction and sentence of life imprisonment and one year rigorous imprisonment for cruelty, respectively.
