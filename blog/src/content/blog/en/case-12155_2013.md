# 12155_2013

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** November 29, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court addressed whether the High Court could reduce a sentence imposed under Section 304A IPC (death by rash and negligent driving) solely on the ground of a prolonged trial. The respondent had been convicted by the Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Chachoda, and sentenced to one year's rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000, but the High Court reduced the sentence to just 34 days (the period already undergone) because the trial had been pending since 1999. The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's order, holding that the mere delay in trial was insufficient justification for such a substantial reduction in sentence, especially since a life had been lost due to the respondent's rash and negligent driving. The Court restored the original sentence of one year's rigorous imprisonment and directed the respondent to surrender within two weeks.
## Key Holdings
- The High Court erred in reducing the sentence for rash and negligent driving causing death merely because the trial had been long pending since 1999.
- Loss of human life caused by rash and negligent driving under Section 304A IPC cannot be disregarded on the ground of delay in trial.
- The original sentence of one year rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs.1000 awarded by the trial court was restored in full.
- Delay in the pendency of a criminal trial is not by itself a sufficient ground to reduce the sentence imposed for causing death by negligence.
- The respondent was directed to surrender within two weeks to undergo the remaining period of the sentence, failing which he was to be taken into custody.
