## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal by Veer Singh and four other accused challenges the High Court of Allahabad's 2007 judgment in remand proceedings, which had commuted their death sentence to life imprisonment while upholding their conviction under Sections 302 read with 149, 307 read with 149, 452, and 148/147 of the IPC. The original Sessions Court had convicted all five accused (and acquitted three co-accused) for offences arising from a violent incident in Muzaffarnagar, initially imposing the death sentence. The legal question before the Supreme Court centers on whether the High Court, after remand, was justified in maintaining the convictions and substituting the death penalty with life imprisonment, and whether the appellants are entitled to further relief.
