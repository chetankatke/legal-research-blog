## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court of India concerned whether an employee's ad-hoc service as Assistant Engineer (from 1999 to 2005) could be counted towards the 8-year "regular service" requirement for promotion to Executive Engineer under the applicable recruitment rules. The respondent sought to have his ad-hoc period treated as regular service so he could be considered for promotion, which led him to challenge the UPSC's decision to fill the Executive Engineer post through direct recruitment. The Central Administrative Tribunal dismissed his application, holding that ad-hoc appointments cannot be equated with regular service for the purpose of meeting eligibility criteria for promotion. ## Key Holdings
- Ad-hoc service rendered by an employee cannot be treated as regular service for the purpose of computing the qualifying period required under recruitment rules for promotion to a higher post.
- Where no departmental candidate fulfills the prescribed eligibility criteria of regular service for promotion, the employer is justified in filling the post through direct recruitment.
- Only regular service as expressly defined under the recruitment rules qualifies an employee to be considered eligible for promotion, and periods of ad-hoc appointment do not count towards such qualifying service.
- The Tribunal rightly rejected the respondent's claim to count his ad-hoc period as Assistant Engineer towards the eight-year regular service requirement for promotion to Executive Engineer.
