## Summary
In this criminal appeal, the Supreme Court considered whether the appellant's conviction under Sections 419 (cheating by personation) and 467 (forgery of valuable security) of the Indian Penal Code was sustainable, where he was accused of impersonating Kewal Krishan Loomba and executing two sale deeds in favor of Gurdarshan Singh in respect of the complainant's land. The trial court, Additional Sessions Judge, and the Punjab and Haryana High Court had all upheld the conviction and sentence (one year RI under Section 419 and three years RI under Section 467), with sentences running concurrently. The key issue before the Supreme Court was whether the prosecution had established beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant had personated the real owner and forged the sale deeds, particularly where the complainant discovered the impersonation only when Gurdarshan Singh revealed it in a civil court written statement.
