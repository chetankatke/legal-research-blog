## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal arose from a dispute between the Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC), Baroda, and Biotor Industries Ltd., a castor oil manufacturer, regarding the levy of market fees on castor seeds purchased by the company within the APMC's market area. The legal question centered on whether market fees were leviable on agricultural produce brought from outside the market area into the market area for use by an industrial concern situated therein, in light of the exemption under sub-rule (2) of Rule 48 of the Gujarat Agricultural Produce Market Rules, 1965. ## Key Holdings
- Agricultural produce brought from outside the market area into the market area for use therein by an industrial concern situated in the market area is not liable for market fees under sub-rule (2) of Rule 48 of the Gujarat Agricultural Produce Market Rules, 1965.
- The respondent-Company, manufacturing castor oil from castor seeds purchased and brought into the market area of APMC, Baroda, falls within the exemption provided under Rule 48(2) of the Rules, and no market fee/cess is leviable on such transactions.
- The State Government under Section 48 of the Gujarat Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1963 has the revisional authority to set aside the order of the APMC levying market fees on agricultural produce brought into the market area by an industrial concern for its own use.
- The High Court correctly allowed the Letters Patent Appeal filed by the respondent-Company, holding that castor seeds brought within the market area for industrial processing by the Company are not subject to market fees.
- The APMC's market area jurisdiction extends to trading activities carried out within the notified area, but does not extend to levy fees on agricultural produce imported for industrial consumption by a manufacturing unit located within the market area.
