## Summary
This appeal concerned whether a member of the Armed Forces, accused of murdering a civilian under Section 302 IPC, should be tried by a criminal court or a Court Martial. The appellant relied on the Supreme Court's judgment in *General Officer Commanding, Rashtriya Rifles v. CBI* (2012), particularly paragraph 95.2, which held that the Armed Forces authorities must decide whether the trial proceeds by court martial or criminal court. The High Court had refused to quash the criminal proceedings, holding that the murder was committed while the appellant was not on active service.
