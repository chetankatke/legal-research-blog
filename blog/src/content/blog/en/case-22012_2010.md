## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether the Maharashtra government could withdraw the linguistic minority status previously granted to the Dayanand Anglo Vedic (DAV) College Trust and Management Society under Article 30 of the Constitution. The appellant, founded by Hindi-speaking Arya Samaj followers, had been recognized as a linguistic minority for academic years 2004-05, 2005-06, and 2006-07, but the Competent Authority withdrew this status in 2009, claiming the earlier grant was based on a mistaken belief that the trustees resided in Maharashtra. The Bombay High Court had refused to interfere with the withdrawal order, prompting the appeal before the Supreme Court. The legal question centered on the validity of withdrawing an existing minority status determination and the procedural requirements under the new 2008 government resolution governing minority recognition.
