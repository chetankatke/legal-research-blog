# 18070_2013

## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal concerns whether the University Grants Commission (UGC) has the authority to prescribe aggregate qualifying marks as a final eligibility criterion for the National Eligibility Test (NET) 2012, after candidates have already secured the minimum marks prescribed in the notification for each paper. The Bombay High Court (Nagpur Bench) and a Kerala High Court single judge had held that UGC lacked the competence to impose such an aggregate cutoff, prompting UGC's appeal. The Court examined the scope of the UGC Act, 1956—enacted under Entry 66, List I of the Seventh Schedule—and Sections 12 and 26, which vest the Commission with powers to coordinate higher education, determine standards, and frame regulations defining qualifications. The case ultimately turns on interpreting whether fixing aggregate qualifying criteria falls within UGC's regulatory and standard-setting functions
## Key Holdings
- The UGC has the power to fix the final qualifying criteria for candidates who have obtained minimum marks in all papers of the NET Examination before declaration of results.
- The UGC Act, 1956 was validly enacted by Parliament under Entry 66 of List I of the Seventh Schedule, covering coordination and determination of standards in higher education institutions.
- Section 12 of the UGC Act casts a general duty on the Commission to take steps for promotion, coordination of university education, and determination and maintenance of standards of teaching, examination, and research.
- Section 12(j) and Section 26 of the UGC Act empower the Commission to make regulations and perform functions incidental or conducive to advancing higher education in India.
- The Division Bench of the Bombay High Court erroneously held that UGC lacked competence to prescribe aggregate marks as a final qualifying criterion for the NET Examination.
