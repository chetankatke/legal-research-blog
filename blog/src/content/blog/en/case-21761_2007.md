## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether an insurance company's liability to pay compensation to a workman/employee injured in a motor accident during the course of employment could be limited to the amount under the Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, or whether full compensation under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 was payable. The appellant/claimant, who was a cleaner injured in a road accident due to the driver's negligence, challenged the Karnataka High Court's order restricting the insurer's liability to the Workmen's Compensation Act amount. The court relied on its earlier decision in *Suresh Chandra vs. State of U.P. & Anr.* (1996 ACJ 1), which held that when a claim is made under the Motor Vehicles Act, the compensation cannot be limited to the amount admissible under the Workmen's Compensation Act.
