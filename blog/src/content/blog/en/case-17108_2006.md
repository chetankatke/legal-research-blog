# 17108_2006

## Summary
This appeal before the Supreme Court concerned the conviction of appellant Nos. 2 and 4 under Sections 304B (dowry death) and 498A (cruelty) of the Indian Penal Code for the death of Madhuri, a newly married woman who committed suicide by hanging twelve days after her wedding, allegedly due to dowry demands (a colour TV, Rs. 50,000 in cash, and a Hero Honda motorcycle). The central legal question was whether the appellants, who contended they were living separately from the deceased and her husband, could still be held jointly liable for cruelty and dowry harassment. The Court upheld the concurrent findings of the trial court and the High Court, holding that the appellants shared joint and direct responsibility for the cruelty inflicted on the bride, and that mental agony caused by the failure to resolve the dowry demands was sufficient to establish their liability under Sections 304B and 498A. The conviction of appellant Nos. 2 and 4 was therefore maintained.
## Key Holdings
- The appellants were convicted under Sections 304B and 498A of the Indian Penal Code for dowry death and cruelty towards a newly married bride.
