## Summary
In *Nagendrappa Natikar v. Neelamma*, the Supreme Court addressed whether a compromise under Order XXIII Rule 3 CPC in a Section 125 CrPC proceeding—where the wife agreed to accept Rs. 8,000 as permanent alimony and relinquish future maintenance claims—would bar her from subsequently seeking maintenance under Section 18 of the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956. The wife had filed a Section 125 CrPC maintenance application, and the parties settled it via a consent letter (in Kannada) stating she suffered from pre-existing ailments (backache, paralysis, epilepsy) before marriage, had voluntarily withdrawn from marital life, and would not claim future maintenance. The court was tasked with determining the binding effect of this compromise on her rights under the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, an issue of significant importance regarding the interplay between summary criminal maintenance proceedings and substantive civil maintenance claims.
## Key Holdings
- The Court considered whether a compromise decree under Order XXIII Rule 3 CPC in a Section 125 CrPC proceeding, where the wife agreed to receive a consolidated amount as permanent alimony and relinquished future maintenance claims, bars a subsequent suit for maintenance under Section 18 of the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956.
- The Court examined the validity of a consent letter executed by the wife recording her voluntary withdrawal from marital life due to pre-existing health conditions and her agreement to forfeit future claims of maintenance in consideration of Rs. 8,000/-.
- The Court considered the distinction between maintenance proceedings under Section 125 CrPC and a substantive suit for maintenance under Section 18 of the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956, and whether acceptance of permanent alimony in one forum operates as res judicata or estoppel in the other.
- The Court took note of the factual context that the wife was suffering from ailments including backache, paralysis stroke, and epilepsy prior to the marriage, which formed the basis of the compromise.
- The Court was required to determine whether a wife who has voluntarily relinquished her right to future maintenance in a court-recorded compromise can later be permitted to claim maintenance under the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act.
