## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from the conviction of the appellants under Section 15 of the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, for possessing 410 kg of poppy husk (10 bags of 41 kg each), which was affirmed by the High Court of Punjab and Haryana, carrying a sentence of 10 years' rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1 lakh each. The facts reveal that on 5.9.1996, police chased a suspicious jeep whose occupants attempted to flee and crash into a house wall, after which the appellants were apprehended and a search conducted in the presence of the Deputy Superintendent of Police (as they opted for a Gazetted officer). ## Key Holdings
- Conviction under Section 15 of the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 was upheld for possession of 410 kg of poppy husk recovered from the appellants' vehicle.
- The search conducted in the presence of the Deputy Superintendent of Police, a Gazetted officer chosen by the accused, satisfied the statutory procedural requirements under the NDPS Act.
- The positive Forensic Science Laboratory report corroborating the presence of poppy husk in the recovered bags was sufficient to sustain the conviction.
- The concurrent findings of the trial court and the High Court were affirmed as the prosecution had established the offence beyond reasonable doubt.
