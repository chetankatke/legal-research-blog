## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal by BSNL arises from a dispute over a commuted pension amount of Rs. 87,400/- allegedly owed to the respondent, who retired in 1990 but filed his claim before the Central Administrative Tribunal only in 2007—after a delay of 17 years. The central legal question was whether an adverse inference of non-payment could be drawn against BSNL merely because it could not produce proof that the cheque had been received by the respondent, given the extreme delay and the destruction of bank records beyond ten years. The Court observed that the claimant cannot be expected to prove a negative (non-receipt of payment), and that the inordinate 17-year delay and destruction of State Bank of India records meant no adverse presumption of law should be cast against the appellants. ## Key Holdings
- A claimant cannot be expected to prove a negative, such as non-receipt of a cheque or demand draft, especially after a long lapse of time.
- Inordinate delay of 17 years in raising a grievance regarding non-receipt of commuted pension amount disentitles the claimant to a presumption of non-payment against the employer.
- Destruction of bank records beyond the statutory retention period of ten years is a relevant factor that complicates adjudication of payment disputes.
- When payment is alleged to have been made by cheque or demand draft dispatched to the claimant, the passage of considerable time weighs against drawing an adverse inference of non-receipt against the paying party.
- The Tribunal erred in directing the employer to furnish proof of receipt of the cheque within a short timeframe without adequately considering the effect of the claimant's prolonged delay in pursuing the remedy.
