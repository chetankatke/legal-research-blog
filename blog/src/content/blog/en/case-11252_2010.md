## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Rohtash Kumar v. State of Haryana* (Criminal Appeal No. 896 of 2011), the Supreme Court considered an appeal challenging the appellant's conviction under Sections 302 and 404 of the Indian Penal Code for the murder of his estranged wife, Sonia, and consequent life sentence affirmed by the Punjab and Haryana High Court. The prosecution's case established that the couple had entered into an inter-caste marriage disapproved of by Sonia's family, that they had filed a mutual consent divorce petition under Section 13-B of the Hindu Marriage Act, and that Sonia was found dead by strangulation in the gallery of her hostel in Faridabad shortly after the appellant had visited her on 2 September 2004.
