# 2576_2012

## Summary
This is a criminal appeal before the Supreme Court arising from a kidnapping case in Bihar, where the Sessions Court and the Patna High Court had convicted the appellant and other accused under Sections 364A, 368, 149, and 120B of the IPC. The case involves the disappearance of several young children (aged 3-8 years) who had gone out to play after returning from a Masjid in October 2002, with the prosecution alleging the appellant's role in the abduction. The High Court confirmed the conviction and life sentences, and the appellant challenged this before the Supreme Court. The judgment, authored by Justice Ranjana Prakash Desai, addresses the evidentiary issues arising from the trial of the accused for kidnapping and criminal conspiracy, including the testimony of child witnesses and statements recorded under Section 164 CrPC.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court granted special leave to appeal against the High Court's judgment confirming conviction and sentence under Sections 364A, 368, 149, and 120B of the IPC.
- The accused were convicted of kidnapping children aged between 3 and 8 years, with sentences of rigorous imprisonment for life and fines of Rs. 10,000 under Sections 364A/149 and 368/149 read with Section 120B of the IPC.
- All sentences awarded to the accused were ordered to run concurrently by the Sessions Judge.
- The High Court by judgment dated 14/09/2011 confirmed the conviction and sentence imposed by the Additional Sessions Judge, FTCI, Begusarai in Sessions Trial No. 304 of 2003.
- Statements of the child witnesses were recorded under Section 164 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 by the Judicial Magistrate, which formed part of the prosecution evidence.
