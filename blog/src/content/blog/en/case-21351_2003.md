# 21351_2003

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 19.06.2003

## Summary
**Summary:**

This civil appeal arose from a partition suit filed by the descendants of one Nithyanandam, who died intestate in 1956, seeking 4/5th shares in three items of suit property. The core legal question was whether items 1 and 2 of the suit properties belonged exclusively to the first defendant (who claimed item 1 was gifted to him by his father and item 2 was purchased with his wife's jewellery) or formed part of the estate of Nithyanandam in which all his wife, sons, and daughter were entitled to equal shares. The Trial Court had decreed 4/5th shares in all three items, but the Madras High Court modified the decree to grant partition only in respect of the third item, holding that items 1 and 2 were the exclusive properties of the first defendant. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court correctly held that the partition decree could not be sustained in respect of items 1 and 2 of the suit properties, but was sustainable only in respect of the third item.
- Where a deceased person died intestate leaving no ancestral property, his wife, sons, and daughter are each entitled to an equal share in the self-acquired property.
- The first defendant claiming items 1 and 2 as his exclusive property bore the burden of proving that item 1 was gifted to him by his father and item 2 was purchased from his wife's jewelry funds.
- A sale of suit property by one co-heir to a third party does not automatically defeat the rights of other heirs to seek partition and claim their lawful share.
