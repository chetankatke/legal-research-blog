# 1813_2013

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 02.01.2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court dismissed the State of Gujarat's review petition challenging its earlier judgment in *State of Gujarat v. Justice R.A. Mehta (Retd.)*, which had interpreted Section 3(1) of the Gujarat Lokayukta Act, 1986 regarding the appointment of the Lokayukta. The State sought review primarily by relying on a later decision in *Justice Chandrashekaraiah (Retd.) v. Janekere C. Krishna
## Key Holdings
- The review petition was dismissed as the grounds based on a subsequent decision in the Karnataka Lokayukta case were found to be without substance.
- The Court drew a clear distinction between Section 3 of the Karnataka Lokayukta Act, 1984 and Section 3(1) of the Gujarat Lokayukta Act, 1986, holding the two statutes were not materially identical for review purposes.
- None of the three grounds raised, namely divergence in interpretation of Section 3, role of constitutional authorities in consultation, and primacy of the Chief Justice's opinion over the Chief Minister, warranted review of the original judgment.
- A later decision of the Court cannot constitute a valid ground to review an earlier judgment rendered in respect of a different statutory provision.
- The Court reaffirmed that mere reliance on a subsequent decision dealing with a different State legislation is insufficient to justify review of its earlier judgment.
