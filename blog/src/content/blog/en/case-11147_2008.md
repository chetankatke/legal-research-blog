## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India, in *State of U.P. v. Naushad*, heard an appeal against the Allahabad High Court's judgment acquitting the accused (the prosecutrix's maternal uncle's son) of rape charges under Section 376 IPC, reversing the trial court's conviction and life sentence. The prosecution alleged that Naushad had sexual intercourse with Shabana on a false promise of marriage, resulting in her pregnancy. The key legal issue before the Court was whether the High Court was justified in acquitting the accused by holding that the prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt, particularly regarding whether consent obtained through a false promise of marriage constitutes rape. The judgment addresses the settled legal position on consent obtained on a false promise of marriage in cases of sexual offences, examining the distinction between a "false promise" and a "breach of promise" of marriage.
## Key Holdings
- Sexual intercourse obtained by a false promise of marriage can constitute the offence of rape under Section 376 of the Indian Penal Code.
- The prosecution must establish beyond reasonable doubt that consent was vitiated by the false promise of marriage to secure a conviction under Section 376 IPC.
- The High Court erred in acquitting the accused by reversing the trial court's conviction where the accused, being a close relative, induced the prosecutrix into sexual relations on a false promise of marriage.
- Mere presence of a familial relationship between the accused and the prosecutrix does not by itself negate the offence of rape committed on a false promise of marriage.
