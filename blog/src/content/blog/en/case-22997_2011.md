## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed a mercy plea in a Section 138 Negotiable Instruments Act case, where the appellant sought reduction of an additional Rs.80,000 compensation imposed by the High Court in lieu of a six-month imprisonment sentence for issuing a dishonoured cheque of Rs.69,500. Noting that the complainant had already received the cheque amount along with the original compensation of Rs.80,000, and that the appellant had already faced prosecution through the Magistrate, Sessions Court, and two revisions before the High Court, the Court found that the High Court's order was unclear regarding the appellant's total liability. The Court directed the appellant to pay only Rs.20,000 within eight weeks, in default of which he would undergo six months of simple imprisonment, holding that this would meet the ends of justice given
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court may exercise its mercy jurisdiction to reduce the additional compensation imposed in lieu of imprisonment under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, where the appellant has already faced prolonged prosecution across multiple forums.
