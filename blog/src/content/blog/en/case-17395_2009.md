# 17395_2009

## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal challenged the High Court of Bombay's confirmation of the death sentence imposed on Shankar Kisanrao Khade, who was convicted of kidnapping, repeatedly raping, and murdering an 11-year-old girl with moderate intellectual disability, with his wife as the co-accused. The central legal question before the Court was whether the death penalty was warranted in light of the aggravating circumstances, particularly the vulnerability of the victim, the brutality of the offenses, and the manner in which the crime was executed. The Court examined the evidence against the appellant, including the circumstances leading to the kidnapping, the series of rapes, and the strangulation murder, to assess whether the conviction and capital sentence should be upheld. The Court's holding ultimately addressed whether the case fell within the "rarest of rare" category justifying capital punishment, given the heinous nature of the crimes against a defenseless minor with disabilities.
