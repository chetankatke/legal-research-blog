# 16753_2011

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India heard an appeal by Jai Bhagwan, an Assistant Wireless Operator in the Delhi Police, who was dismissed from service following disciplinary proceedings for misconduct, including being found locked in his wireless cabin in plain clothes, shouting at a senior inspector, and snatching the log book when asked to produce it. The inquiry held the charges proved, and both the appellate authority and the Delhi High Court (dismissing his writ petition and review petition) upheld the dismissal. The legal question concerned the validity and proportionality of the disciplinary action and the adequacy of the inquiry conducted against the appellant police personnel. The judgment excerpt indicates the Supreme Court granted special leave to appeal and proceeded to determine whether the dismissal could be sustained in light of the established misconduct.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court granted leave to appeal against the High Court's order dismissing the writ petition challenging the appellant's dismissal from service as Assistant Wireless Operator.
- The disciplinary inquiry properly established the charge of misconduct against the appellant based on evidence of misbehavior, insubordination, and unauthorized conduct during duty hours.
- The appellant's act of shouting at the inspecting officer, being in plain clothes instead of uniform, and snatching the log book from the Inspector constituted serious misconduct under Section 21 of the Delhi Police Act.
- Making irrelevant transmissions on the District Network during duty hours aggravated the misconduct and was a valid ground for disciplinary action.
- The High Court was justified in dismissing the writ petition as the domestic inquiry was conducted fairly and the findings of guilt were based on credible evidence.
