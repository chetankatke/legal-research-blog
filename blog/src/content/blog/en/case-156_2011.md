# 156_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 23.08.1997
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this civil appeal arising from a suit for specific performance of an agreement of sale dated 23.08.1997, the Supreme Court considered whether the Trial Court erred in decreeing the suit without framing an additional issue regarding the status of the suit property. The defendant/appellant had admitted during cross-examination (as P.W.1) that the property was a joint family/ancestral property, yet the Trial Court failed to frame a specific issue on this crucial aspect. The Supreme Court set aside the concurrent findings of the Trial Court and High Court, holding that the issue of the property's status was relevant and significant for the effective disposal of the suit. The matter was remanded to the Trial Court with directions to frame the additional issue and permit both parties to lead further evidence, while also protecting the defendant's possession and title over the property until the suit's final disposal.
