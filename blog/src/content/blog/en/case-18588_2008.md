## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether the employer (U.T. Chandigarh) could retrospectively rectify the pay of an ex-serviceman (respondent Gurcharan Singh) who had been appointed as a Clerk on the ex-servicemen quota in 1992, with the mistake in initial pay fixation only being discovered upon an audit query after his retirement in 1997. The respondent had challenged the 1998 re-fixation order, which was dismissed by the Central Administrative Tribunal but allowed by the Punjab and Haryana High Court. The Court considered the Central Civil Services (Fixation of Pay of Re-employed Pensioners) Orders, 1986, which had consolidated various notifications governing pay fixation of re-employed pensioners. Ultimately, the Court ruled in favor of the employer, holding that the re-fixation of pay was justified under the 1986 Orders to correct the original error
