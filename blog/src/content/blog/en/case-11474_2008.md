# 11474_2008

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 24.08.2007
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court considered whether adverse observations made by the High Court of Bombay (Goa Bench) against a District and Sessions Judge, while discharging contempt proceedings against her, should be permitted to stand. The High Court had discharged the contempt notice but directed the Registrar General to initiate disciplinary proceedings, and its Division Bench had upheld adverse remarks that could harm the appellant's judicial career. Holding that such observations would unjustly cast a shadow on the appellant's professional development as a judicial officer, the Supreme Court allowed the appeal and set aside the impugned orders, including the Registrar General's communication, while expressly leaving undisturbed the order discharging the contempt proceedings.
## Key Holdings
- Observations made by the High Court that would cast a shadow on the judicial career of a judicial officer must be set aside to protect her professional development.
- The career of a bright judicial officer should not be imperiled or stunted by adverse observations, especially at a crucial juncture of her growth.
- The Court allowed the appeal and set aside the impugned judgment and order in the Contempt Appeal, the Criminal Writ Petition, and the communication of the Registrar General of the High Court.
- The Court clarified that its order did not disturb the High Court's order discharging the contempt proceedings initiated against the appellant.
- Directions issued by the High Court to the Registrar General to initiate disciplinary proceedings after discharge of contempt were set aside as not warranted.
