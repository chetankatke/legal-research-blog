## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal in *Fakhruzamma v. State of Jharkhand* raised the question of whether prior sanction under **Section 197 Cr.P.C.** was required to prosecute a former Sub-Inspector of Police for offences including murder (Section 304 IPC), where the alleged act was committed in the course of his official duty but he had already been removed from service under the Jharkhand Police Manual. The High Court had held that no sanction was necessary since the appellant was no longer in service, relying on *Nagraj v. State of Mysore*. The appellant contended, relying on *Sankaran Moitra v.
