# Court in Surinder Singh Brar and others v. Union of India and others

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 18.3.2011
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this case, the Supreme Court addressed appeals by landowners challenging the acquisition of their land by the Chandigarh Administration for the Chandigarh Technology Park under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894. The central legal question was whether officers of the Union Territory of Chandigarh—other than the Administrator—had the authority to issue notifications under Sections 4(1) and 6(1) of the 1894 Act. The Court, drawing on its earlier decision in *Surinder Singh Brar v. Union of India* (2013) 1 SCC 403, held that only the Administrator of the Union Territory could issue such notifications, and therefore the impugned acquisition was invalid. Consequently, the Court allowed the appeals and quashed the acquisition of the appellants' land.
## Key Holdings
- Notifications under Sections 4(1) and 6(1) of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 for acquiring land for Phase-III of Chandigarh Technology Park were quashed.
- The question of whether officers of the Union Territory of Chandigarh other than the Administrator could issue acquisition notifications under Sections 4(1) and 6(1) of the 1894 Act was a key issue considered.
- Article 239 of the Constitution, both unamended and amended, governs the administration of Union Territories like Chandigarh and the issuance of notifications by delegates of the Administrator.
- The Land Acquisition Officer's report under Section 5A must reflect genuine consideration of objections, not merely a formality, before a Section 6(1) declaration can be issued.
- The Supreme Court reversed the High Court's order and set aside the acquisition of land for Phase-III of Chandigarh Technology Park along with related notifications.
