## Summary
This case involves Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) challenging the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) before the Supreme Court on issues concerning the scope of TRAI's regulatory powers vis-à-vis telecom licenses. A two-Judge Bench referred several substantial questions of law to a larger Bench, primarily concerning (i) whether the TRAI Act, 1997 prevails over inconsistent terms in licenses issued under the Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, and (ii) whether TRAI has the power to fix terms and conditions of interconnection between service providers under Section 11, particularly in light of the 2000 Amendment to the TRAI Act. The core legal issue is the harmonious construction of Section 11(1)(b)(ii) and the provisos introduced by the 2000 Amendment, including the extent of TRAI's authority over licenses issued both before and after 24.01.2000.
