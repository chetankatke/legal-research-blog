## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court of India case, decided by Justice Chelameswar, addresses the legal status of persons appointed as Special Police Officers (SPOs) under Section 17 of the Police Act, 1861, in the context of the large-scale law and order disturbances in Punjab during the 1980s. The appellants were appointed as SPOs to provide security to public sector banks, with the banks bearing their financial costs, following a 1984 decision by the Advisor to the Governor of Punjab.
