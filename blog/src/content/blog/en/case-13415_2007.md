## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Kusti Mallaiah v. State of Andhra Pradesh* (Criminal Appeal No. 642 of 2008), the Supreme Court addressed an appeal challenging the High Court's judgment upholding the appellant's conviction under Sections 302 and 404 read with Section 34 IPC, arising from the rape and murder of Neelagiri Parvamma in February 1997, and the subsequent theft of her gold and silver ornaments. The prosecution relied on eyewitness testimony (PW-6), recovery of stolen ornaments from the accused, and medical/forensic evidence to establish guilt, which the trial court and High Court found sufficient to sustain the conviction. The appellant's appeal before the Supreme Court questioned the legal propriety of the concurrent findings of guilt and the sentence of life imprisonment plus three years' rigorous imprisonment.
