## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a dispute over agricultural land in Dharwad, Karnataka, where the appellants claimed ownership since 1956 while the 2nd respondent (Gangappa, now deceased) sought occupancy rights under the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1974, by claiming to have filed a Form No. 7 application in 1975. The central legal question is whether the 2nd respondent sufficiently proved that he had actually submitted the Form No. 7 application to the Land Tribunal, which would entitle him to be registered as an occupant. ## Key Holdings
- The Land Tribunal must conduct an enquiry to verify whether a tenant actually filed a Form No.7 application claiming occupancy rights under Section 48-A of the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1974.
- Mere production of a xerox copy of a postal receipt and acknowledgment is insufficient to prove that a Form No.7 application was filed with the Land Tribunal.
- The burden lies on the claimant tenant to prove that the Form No.7 application was in fact sent and received by the Land Tribunal, and failure to discharge this burden defeats the claim for occupancy rights.
- Where the Land Tribunal finds on evidence that the tenant did not file Form No.7, the claim for grant of occupancy rights over the land cannot be sustained.
- The High Court's direction to remand the matter to the Land Tribunal for verification of Form No.7 filing was a proper exercise of jurisdiction and warranted no interference in appeal.
