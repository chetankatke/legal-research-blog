## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India heard an appeal by the State of Maharashtra challenging a Bombay High Court judgment that granted freedom fighter's pension to three respondents under the "Freedom Fighters Pension Scheme" (Government Resolution dated 4.7.1995). The respondents had claimed entitlement as "Underground Freedom Fighters" who participated in either the "Bharat Chodo" agitation of 1942-44 or the Hyderabad Liberation Movement of 1947-48. The High Court had quashed the State's rejection orders and directed payment of pension along with arrears from the date of first application. Before the Supreme Court (per A.K. Sikri, J.), the central legal question concerned the proper interpretation of the eligibility criteria and certification requirements for underground freedom fighters under the 1995 Scheme.
