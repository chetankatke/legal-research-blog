# 25188_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** September 9, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court allowed the complainant's appeal challenging bail granted by the High Court to five accused charged under Section 302 IPC for the murder of Nagendra Pandey. The Court found that the High Court erred in concluding that no overt act was attributed to the accused, noting that the charge-sheet clearly showed Jitendra Pandey wielded a dagger, while Amukh Pandey and Hare Ram Pandey pushed the victim, and Ram Pujan Pandey also played a role. However, the Court found no specific role attributed to Tanuj Pandey. Consequently, the bail orders for the four accused were set aside, and they were directed to surrender within two weeks.
## Key Holdings
- The High Court erred in granting bail to the four accused (Amukh, Jitendra, Hare Ram, and Ram Pujan Pandey) despite the charge-sheet attributing specific overt acts to them in the commission of the murder under Section 302 IPC.
- The charge-sheet established that Jitendra Pandey pulled the victim from the motorcycle, Amukh Pandey and Hare Ram Pandey pushed the injured victim down, and Jitendra Pandey used the dagger, making them ineligible for bail.
- No specific role was attributed to Tanuj Pandey in the charge-sheet, and consequently the court did not interfere with the bail granted to him.
- The orders of the learned Single Judge of the High Court granting bail to the four accused were set aside, and they were directed to surrender before the concerned police station within two weeks.
