## Summary
In this brief order, the Supreme Court of India addressed a Contempt Petition (C) No. 303 of 2013, which had been filed in connection with Writ Petition (C) No. 598 of 2013 by Aneesh D. Lawande and another against the State of Goa. The court held that since the underlying writ petition had already been disposed of, no cause of action survived in the contempt petition. Accordingly, the contempt petition was dismissed as not maintainable, with the bench comprising Justices Anil R. Dave and Dipak Misra delivering the order on August 30, 2013.
## Key Holdings
- A contempt petition does not survive once the underlying writ petition has already been disposed of.
- When the main writ petition is disposed of, the related contempt petition must be dismissed as nothing remains to be enforced.
- The Supreme Court dismissed the contempt petition as non-survivable following the disposal of the connected writ petition.
