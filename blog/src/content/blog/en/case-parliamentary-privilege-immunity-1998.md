## Key Holdings

- Bribe givers do not enjoy immunity under Article 105(2) of the Constitution and must face trial for substantive offences under Section 12 of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 and Section 120-B of the Indian Penal Code.
- Bribe takers who are Members of Parliament are entitled to the immunity conferred by Article 105(2) of the Constitution for any vote or speech given in Parliament.
- Members of Parliament qualify as public servants within the meaning of Section 2(c) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
- Members of Parliament cannot be prosecuted for offences under Sections 7, 10, 11, and 13 of the Prevention of Corruption Act for want of a competent authority to grant sanction.
- Members of Parliament who give bribes cannot claim immunity from prosecution under Article 105(2) or Article 105(3) of the Constitution.
