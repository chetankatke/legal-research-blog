## Summary
**Summary:**

This criminal appeal before the Supreme Court arose from the conviction of Arshad Hussain under Section 302 read with Section 34 of the IPC for fatally shooting Iqbal, with the incident occurring on 18.12.2000 near Ashfaq's house in Udaipur,
## Key Holdings
- Common intention under Section 34 IPC can be established where accused persons coordinate an attack by calling out to the shooter to fire.
- The actual shooter who fires the fatal shot on hearing the co-accused's call is liable for murder under Section 302 IPC.
- Co-accused who merely instigated or shouted without participating in the actual assault may be acquitted if common intention is not independently proved.
- Mere presence at the scene and prior enmity are insufficient to convict under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC without clear evidence of shared design.
