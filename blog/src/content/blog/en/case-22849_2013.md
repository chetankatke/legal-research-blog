## Summary
**Summary:**

This case concerns the legal validity of the Medical Council of India's (MCI) approval dated 15 July 2013, granting renewal of permission to Chintpurni Medical College & Hospital, Pathankot, to admit its third batch of 150 MBBS students for the academic year 2013-14. The college had come under scrutiny after an MCI inspection on 19/20 April 2012 revealed serious deficiencies and the presence of a fake faculty member who was simultaneously shown as working at another medical college (S.N. Medical College, Agra), prompting a show cause notice. The MCI's Board of Governors had earlier denied renewal of permission for the second batch (2012-13) due to these deficiencies, despite granting the college a personal hearing. ## Key Holdings
- MCI has the authority and discretion to grant or refuse renewal of permission for admission of MBBS students based on its assessment of medical colleges.
- Presentation of a fake faculty before the MCI Inspection Team constitutes a serious deficiency and misrepresentation by the medical college.
- A faculty member working simultaneously at two medical colleges while being shown as full-time faculty amounts to misrepresentation warranting cancellation of registration.
- The Board of Governors of MCI may decline renewal of permission to admit a fresh batch of MBBS students where deficiencies pointed out by the Inspection Team are of a serious nature.
- Medical colleges are bound to comply with the conditions and requirements prescribed by the MCI, failing which approval for admissions can be withdrawn.
