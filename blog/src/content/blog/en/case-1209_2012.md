## Summary
In *Satya Jain v. Anis Ahmed Rushdie*, the Supreme Court of India had previously (on 3.12.2012) allowed the appeals and decreed specific performance of a contract for sale of property located at 4, Flag Staff Road, Civil Lines, Delhi, directing that the sale deed be executed at the market price as on the date of the order, with the matter remanded to the trial court for valuation. Subsequently, several interlocutory applications were filed seeking impleadment, clarification, and modification of that judgment—including applications by third parties who had purchased 1,500 sq. yards
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court allowed the civil appeal, set aside the decree of the Delhi High Court, and decreed the suit for specific performance of the sale agreement.
- The sale deed was directed to be executed by the defendants in favour of the plaintiffs at the market price of the suit property as on the date of the order.
- The learned trial judge of the High Court of Delhi was requested to assess the correct market value of the suit property with due expedition due to absence of material on record.
- The applications for impleadment arose from the fact that during pendency of the appeal, two parcels of the suit property measuring 1500 sq. yds. were sold to third parties, leaving the successor-in-interest with rights over only 3873 sq. yds.
- Ms. Sameen Rushdie Momen was recognized as the legal heir and successor-in-interest of the deceased sole defendant Anis Ahmed Rushdie by virtue of a Will dated 9.1.1984.
