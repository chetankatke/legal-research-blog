# 10339_2008

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** April 16, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arose from a High Court order directing Neyveli Lignite Corporation to absorb and regularize contract workers (members of respondent Union No.1) as regular employees based on seniority determined by their date of entry into the Corporation's service. The legal question concerned the regularization of contract labour in regular employment against future vacancies. During the pendency of the appeal, the Corporation prepared a common seniority list of all contract workmen, taking into account their date of entry through any private contractor, which was acceptable to the Union. The Supreme Court disposed of the appeal, holding that since the seniority list had been duly prepared and would be acted upon, the matter required no further adjudication, while allowing individual workmen to submit grievances regarding the list to the management for consideration.
## Key Holdings
- Contract workers may seek regularization in the regular employment of the principal employer based on the date of their entry into the corporation's service, whether as contract labour or otherwise.
- A common seniority list for regularization of contract workers must be prepared by considering the date of entry into the corporation's service through any private contractor, not merely the date of membership with the union.
- Regularization of contract workers is to be effected as and when vacancies arise due to demise, voluntary retirement, or superannuation of regular employees.
- When a proper seniority list is prepared and no party has grievance, the appellate court may dispose of the appeal without keeping it alive.
- Workmen aggrieved by the prepared seniority list may submit their objections to the management, which is bound to consider such grievances.
