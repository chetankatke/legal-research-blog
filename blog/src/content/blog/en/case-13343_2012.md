## Summary
This appeal arises from a motor accident claim where the appellant's 21-year-old son died in a collision, and the Tribunal initially awarded Rs. 13,07,000/- in compensation, which the High Court later reduced to Rs. 6,75,000/- on an appeal filed by the Insurance Company. The central legal question is whether the Insurance Company could maintain its appeal challenging the quantum of compensation without first obtaining the Tribunal's permission under Section 170(b) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. The judgment addresses the maintainability of such an appeal by an insurer, who is generally precluded from contesting the claim on merits unless granted leave. The matter was before the Supreme Court to determine the correctness of the High Court's interference with the Tribunal's award in light of the insurer's failure to seek Section 170(b) permission.
