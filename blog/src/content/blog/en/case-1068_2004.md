# 1068_2004

## Citation

- **Date:** 9.10.2003
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from the Punjab and Haryana High Court's dismissal of the appellant's writ petition challenging his non-promotion to the post of Battalion Commander in the Punjab Home Guard. The legal question centers on the interpretation of Rule 8 of the Punjab Home Guard, Class-I Rules, 1988, which prescribes that 75% of Battalion Commander posts be filled by promotion based on the "seniority-cum-merit" principle, with the appellant contending that his seniority could not be ignored in favor of a junior candidate (Respondent No. 5). The appellant also challenged the executive instructions dated 29.12.2000 and 6.9.2001, which were used to bypass seniority considerations. The matter before the Supreme Court examines whether the High Court erred in rejecting the claim for promotion and whether the executive instructions could override the statutory promotion rules.
## Key Holdings
- Promotion to the post of Battalion Commander is governed by Rule 8 of the Punjab Home Guard, Class-I Rules, 1988, under which 75 per cent of posts are filled by promotion based on the principle of 'seniority-cum-merit'.
- Under the 'seniority-cum-merit' principle, seniority of a candidate cannot be ignored when considering promotion to the relevant post.
- Executive instructions dated 29.12.2000 cannot be applied to determine promotions where the vacancies on promotional posts had occurred prior to the issuance of such instructions.
- Selection for promotion must be made on the basis of past five years' Annual Confidential Reports and other relevant records of the eligible candidates.
