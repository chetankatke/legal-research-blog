## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed whether a neighbor—claiming infringement of his right to light and air but disclaiming any right, title, or interest in the suit property—could be impleaded as a "proper party" under Order 1 Rule 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure in a suit filed by the property owner against the Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation challenging a notice under Section 452 of the GHMC Act, 1955. ## Key Holdings
- A person who does not claim any right, title or interest in the suit property may still be impleaded as a proper party under Order 1 Rule 10 CPC if his right of light and air is affected by the construction.
- A neighbour whose right of light and air is alleged to be infringed by the plaintiff's construction is a proper and necessary party to the suit challenging the municipal notice regarding that construction.
- Under Order 1 Rule 10 CPC, a party becomes a necessary or proper party if they have a direct interest in the subject matter to be adjudicated in the suit, even without claiming title over the property.
- The validity of a notice issued under Section 452 of the Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act, 1955 can be challenged by the affected property owner, and any person whose light and air rights are affected has a locus to be joined as a party.
