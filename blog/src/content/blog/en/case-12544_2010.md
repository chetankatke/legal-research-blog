## Summary
This appeal concerns the dismissal of R. Mahalingam, an Assistant with the Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC), who was implicated in an examination irregularity where six candidates in Hall No. 76 were mistakenly distributed afternoon question papers during the morning session on 17.2.1990. The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the disciplinary action against Mahalingam could be sustained, given that the primary evidence against him was the statement of Chief Invigilator Syed Abdul Kareem, who alleged that Mahalingam persuaded him not to report the incident to the TNPSC office. The appeal arose from the Madras High Court's Division Bench upholding the Single Judge's order dismissing Mahalingam's challenge to the punishment.
