# 16561_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 could be invoked for offences under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988. The Court relied on its prior decisions in *State Through SP, New Delhi v. Ratan Lal Arora* and *State v. A. Parthiban* to hold that when a statute prescribes a minimum sentence—as Sections 7 and 13 of the PC Act do (six months and one year, respectively)—the provisions of the Probation of Offenders Act cannot be invoked. Accordingly, the Court upheld the High Court's order imposing substantive one-year sentences (concurrent) on the retired post office employee who had misappropriated Rs. 1,35,240, and dismissed the appeal.
## Key Holdings
- The Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 does not apply to offences under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, which prescribe minimum sentences.
- Where a specific statutory provision prescribes a minimum sentence, the provisions of the Probation of Offenders Act cannot be invoked.
- Sections 7 and 13 of the Prevention of Corruption Act prescribe minimum sentences of six months and one year respectively, precluding relief under the Probation Act.
- The High Court was justified in setting aside the Trial Court's order releasing the appellant under the Probation of Offenders Act and imposing substantive sentence.
- The settled legal position from State Through SP, New Delhi v. Ratan Lal Arora and State v. A. Parthiban confirms that the Probation Act is inapplicable to Prevention of Corruption Act offences.
