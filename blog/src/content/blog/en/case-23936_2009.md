## Summary
This appeal before the Supreme Court challenged the Karnataka High Court's reversal of the Sessions Court's acquittal, which had convicted the appellant under Section 304B IPC (dowry death), Section 498A IPC (cruelty), and Sections 3 and 4 of the Dowry Prohibition Act. ## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court addressed a criminal appeal arising from a conviction by the High Court reversing the Sessions Court's acquittal in a dowry death case under Section 304B IPC.
- The High Court convicted the appellant under Section 304B IPC and sentenced him to 7 years rigorous imprisonment based on the circumstances of the wife's unnatural death within seven years of marriage.
- The appellant was convicted under Section 498A IPC for cruelty and under Sections 3 and 4 of the Dowry Prohibition Act, with all sentences directed to run concurrently.
- The medical evidence established that the deceased sustained approximately 95% ante-mortem burns and the cause of death was shock resulting from the burn injuries.
- The court examined the applicability of dowry death provisions where the wife's death by burns occurred shortly after a quarrel with the husband regarding demands for money.
