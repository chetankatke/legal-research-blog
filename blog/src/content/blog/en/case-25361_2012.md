# 25361_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 10.07.2012
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court considered whether the High Court erred in directing the reinstatement of the respondent (Karan Pal Singh) based on an earlier writ court order, which had stipulated that his retirement was "subject to the outcome of the pending proposal before the State Government." The respondent sought contempt relief after a Government Order dated 03.07.2012 was issued, and the High Court allowed him to resume service, treating his period of absence as leave. The appellants challenged this direction, arguing the original writ order was only prospective in nature, but the High Court held they were bound to implement it. (The judgment excerpt is incomplete, cutting off before the final holding of the Supreme Court is recorded.)
## Key Holdings
- The retirement of the employee was made subject to the outcome of a pending proposal before the State Government by order dated 30.01.2012.
- The employee was held entitled to the benefit of the Government Order dated 03.07.2012 by virtue of the earlier writ court order, requiring implementation of that order.
- The High Court directed the employee to rejoin duty within one week and the Director to allow resumption of duties, with the service period counted for all purposes.
- The period of the employee's absence from duty was directed to be treated in accordance with the Leave Rules applicable to the Organization.
