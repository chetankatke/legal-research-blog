## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal by Md. Iqbal and another accused challenged their conviction under Section 376(2)(g) IPC (gang rape) by the trial court and the Jharkhand High Court, with the prosecution case resting on the prosecutrix's allegation that she was abducted from a vegetable market and gang-raped in a school by the appellants on June 13, 1999. The appellants argued that the prosecutrix's testimony was inconsistent with the medical evidence, her conduct was unnatural, and most prosecution witnesses—including her own father—turned hostile during trial, undermining the prosecution's case. The court was thus called upon to assess whether, despite the hostile witnesses, the conviction could be sustained based on the prosecutrix's testimony and other circumstantial evidence.
