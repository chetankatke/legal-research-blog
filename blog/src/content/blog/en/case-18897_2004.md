# 18897_2004

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 21.11.1970
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed two key issues: (1) whether the will of Smt. Nagammanni (dated 24.10.1943 and registered the following day) was validly executed and duly proved by the original plaintiffs (sons of her cousin), and (2) whether the High Court of Karnataka was justified in disturbing the concurrent findings of the Trial Court and Lower Appellate Court while exercising its power under Section 100 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The dispute concerned ancestral agricultural property in Karnataka's Mandya district, where the plaintiffs claimed ownership through the will while the appellants (descendants of Smt. Nagammanni's husband from his second wife) contested the will's validity. The plaintiffs had filed Suit No. 32 of 1975 seeking declaration of title, permanent injunction, and alternatively recovery of possession with mesne profits
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court examined whether the will of Smt. Nagammanni dated 24.10.1943 was validly executed and duly proved by the respondents.
- A key issue before the Court was whether the High Court of Karnataka was justified in interfering in Second Appeal under Section 100 of the Code of Civil Procedure against the concurrent findings of the Trial Court and the Lower Appellate Court.
- The dispute concerned ancestral property consisting of 11 parcels of dry land and 2 parcels of wet land, where the will did not cover three specific parcels of dry land.
- The appellants (sons of the testatrix's husband from his second wife) contested the suit by denying Smt. Nagammanni's ownership of the suit property and the validity of the will.
- The plaintiffs claimed title through the registered will and sought declaration of title and permanent injunction, or alternatively recovery of possession with future mesne profits.
