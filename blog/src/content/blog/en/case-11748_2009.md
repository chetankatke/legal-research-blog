## Summary
**Summary:**

This batch of civil appeals before the Supreme Court of India raised the legal question of whether the reservation of privately owned land in Regional/Development plans prepared under the Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, would be deemed to have lapsed if the concerned planning authority failed to acquire the land or initiate acquisition proceedings within six months of receiving a purchase notice under Section 127 of the Act. The lead case involved respondents whose land in Shrirampur was reserved for a primary school and playground in the 1991 Development Plan, but was not acquired for over eleven and a half years, prompting them to issue a purchase notice under Section 127 to the Shrirampur Municipal Council. The appeals arose from multiple similar disputes across Maharashtra concerning the same statutory interpretation issue under Section 127, with the Supreme Court having granted leave to address this recurring question.
