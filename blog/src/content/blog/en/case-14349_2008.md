# High Court in the case of Jatinder Singh vs. The State of Haryana and ors

## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court of India case involves land-owners challenging compensation awarded for their lands acquired by the State of Haryana near Karnal for development of an urban estate under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894. The acquired lands, re-christened as Sectors 6, 3, 9, and 7, were the subject of multiple acquisition notifications issued between 1980 and 1982, with compensation determined by the Land Acquisition Collector at varying rates depending on land classification (Chahi Nehri, Barani, Banjar Qadim, and Ghair Mumkin). The land-owners sought reference under Section 18 of the Act for fair and just compensation, leading to the present appeals. The court noted that a prior High Court decision in *Jatinder Singh vs. State of Haryana* had enhanced compensation to Rs.22 per square yard for similar lands, and the appeals were disposed of by common judgment based on identical issues.
