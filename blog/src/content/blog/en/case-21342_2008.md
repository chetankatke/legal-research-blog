## Summary
This Supreme Court case addresses whether the Nagar Panchayat of Kurwai (under the Madhya Pradesh Municipality Act, 1961) has the statutory authority to levy parking/entry fees on motor vehicles, trucks, and buses using its bus stand. The Madhya Pradesh High Court's Division Bench had held that Sections 349, 358(4)(b) and (d), and 358(7)(m) of the 1961 Act did not confer such power, reversing the Single Judge's contrary decision. The Supreme Court framed the core legal principle that no person has a fundamental right to use another party's land without permission or payment, and emphasized that local authorities are responsible for maintaining and managing parking places under the Motor Vehicles Rules, 1994.
