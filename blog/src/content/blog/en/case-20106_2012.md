# 20106_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** July 8, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court heard an appeal by the State of U.P. against a Labour Court award and High Court order directing reinstatement of the respondent (Idrish) with 50% back-wages following his discontinuance from service. The respondent had raised an industrial dispute after being discontinued, and the Labour Court found he had worked continuously from 5 December 1996 to 1 December 1998. Before the Supreme Court, both parties consented to modify the award, with the respondent agreeing to forgo back-wages entirely in exchange for reinstatement with continuity of service. The Court accordingly modified the orders, directing reinstatement with continuity of service but without any back-wages, and disposed of the appeal.
## Key Holdings
- The Labour Court properly found that the respondent worked continuously from 5 December 1996 to 1 December 1998, warranting relief in the industrial dispute.
- Where an employee is illegally or improperly discontinued from service, reinstatement with continuity of service is an appropriate remedy.
- The court may modify the quantum of back-wages awarded by the Labour Court and upheld by the High Court where the employee voluntarily forgoes back-wages in exchange for reinstatement.
- By consent of both parties, the order of reinstatement with 50% back-wages was modified to grant reinstatement with continuity of service but without any back-wages.
- The appeal was disposed of in terms of the parties' joint suggestion, reflecting the court's willingness to mould relief to achieve substantive justice in service matters.
