## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal (Ganeshlal v. Shyam) raised the question of whether Consumer Forums under the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 have jurisdiction over a dispute arising from a simple sale of a plot of land, where the seller failed to hand over possession under a 1999 agreement. The appellant contended that a sale of immovable property simpliciter does not constitute a "service" attracting the Act's provisions, and that specific performance claims lie exclusively before civil courts. The District Forum, State Commission, and National Commission had all upheld jurisdiction by treating the failure to deliver possession as a "deficiency in service." The Supreme Court was called upon to decide whether such a land-sale transaction falls within the statutory definition of "complaint" under Section 2(1)(c) of the Act.
## Key Holdings
- A simple sale of a plot of land does not constitute a deficiency in service under the Consumer Protection Act, 1986.
- The Consumer Forum lacks jurisdiction to entertain complaints relating to the sale of immovable property such as plots of land.
- A claim for specific performance of an agreement to sell land lies before the Civil Court and not before the Consumer Redressal Forum.
- Disputes concerning the sale of land are civil in nature and cannot be treated as complaints of unfair trade practice or deficiency in service under Section 2(1)(c) of the Act.
- The fact that possession of the land was not handed over pursuant to a sale agreement does not convert the transaction into a service rendered by the seller to the buyer.
