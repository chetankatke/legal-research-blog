## Summary
This case before the Supreme Court of India concerns whether M/s I.C.D.S. Ltd., a non-banking finance company engaged in leasing and hire-purchase, is entitled to claim depreciation under Section 32 of the Income Tax Act, 1961 on vehicles that it financed and leased to customers, where the lessees were registered as owners under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. The dispute specifically involves whether the assessee could claim (a) normal depreciation as the true owner of the vehicles, and (b) a higher rate of depreciation on the ground that the vehicles were used in the business of running on hire. The Assessing Officer rejected both claims, the Commissioner (Appeals) allowed normal depreciation but not the higher rate, while the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal allowed both claims
