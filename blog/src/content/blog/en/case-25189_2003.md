## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed appeals against the Andhra Pradesh High Court's judgment upholding the acquittal of R.V. Surya Rao of charges under Sections 306 and 498A IPC, arising from the suicide of his wife Vijaya Bala by consuming poison. The core legal question was whether the prosecution had established beyond reasonable doubt that the respondent abetted the suicide or subjected the deceased to cruelty, given the existence of a suicide note (Ext.P1) explicitly stating that no one was responsible for her death and that the decision to commit suicide was her own. ## Key Holdings
- A signed suicide note by the deceased stating that no one was responsible for her death and that the decision was her own will exonerates the accused under Sections 498A and 306 IPC.
- The prosecution must prove abetment of suicide beyond reasonable doubt, and mere circumstances such as a door being bolted from outside are insufficient to establish the offence.
- The High Court rightly declined to interfere with the acquittal where there was no evidence of harassment or cruelty by the accused and the deceased had expressed suicidal tendencies on several occasions.
- The criminal appeal filed by the complainant abates upon his death during the pendency of the appeal.
- Quarrels between spouses over loans, without more, do not constitute cruelty or abetment of suicide under Sections 498A and 306 IPC.
