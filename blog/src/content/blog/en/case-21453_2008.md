## Summary
In *Rajendra Singh v. State of Uttaranchal*, the Supreme Court heard a criminal appeal arising from the Uttarakhand High Court's judgment reversing the trial court's acquittal and convicting the appellant (a tailor) under Section 302 IPC for murdering Kishan Singh Rana by attacking him with scissors over a dispute regarding unstitched clothes. The prosecution's case rested primarily on the testimony of three eyewitnesses, including the deceased's brother (the first informant) and the deceased's wife. The core legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the High Court was justified in overturning the trial court's acquittal and whether the prosecution had proven the appellant's guilt beyond reasonable doubt. Although the provided excerpt is truncated, the appeal squarely raises the issue of the proper appellate standard for interfering with a trial court's acquittal in a murder case.
