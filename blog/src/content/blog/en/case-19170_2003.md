## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard Civil Appeals challenging a Karnataka High Court judgment that reversed the trial court's decree in favor of the appellant, a Class I contractor who had been awarded contracts by Karnataka Neeravari Nigam Ltd. for irrigation canal construction work (including the Alur combined branch and Malaprabha right bank canal works) in Dharwad and Belgaum districts, with work orders issued between November 1988 and March 1989. The central legal issue concerned the contractor's claims arising from the execution of these public works contracts, including alleged difficulties in obtaining essential materials (cement, RCC templates, and PCC slabs) from the Executive Engineer, which impacted timely performance. ## Key Holdings
- The case involves a construction contract dispute where the contractor plaintiff challenged the High Court's judgment reversing the trial court's decree in his favor.
- The contract required completion within 15 months excluding the monsoon season from June 15 to September 15, and the dispute centers on work execution of canal construction.
- The plaintiff alleged that the defendant authorities failed to supply essential materials like cement, RCC templates, and PCC slabs despite repeated requests.
- The appeals arise from a common judgment in multiple RFA cases, indicating consolidated proceedings involving several related canal work contracts.
