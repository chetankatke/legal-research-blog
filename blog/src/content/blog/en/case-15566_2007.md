## Summary
This criminal appeal before the Supreme Court of India arose from a challenge to the High Court of Allahabad's decision reversing the trial court's acquittal of the appellants in a murder case. The prosecution alleged that the accused—including Habib (the appellant's son-in-law, whose wife had left him due to a dowry dispute)—ambushed a group traveling along a canal road, during which Manuwa and Habib opened fire, resulting in the death of Fakira. While the Sessions Court had acquitted all accused, the High Court set aside the acquittals of Habib and Manuwa, convicting Habib under Section 302 IPC and Manuwa under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC.
