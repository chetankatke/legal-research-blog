## Summary
**Summary:**

This case concerns a government servant, Rajesh Gupta, who was prematurely retired from service by the State of Jammu and Kashmir in April 2005, despite three criminal cases registered against him being found "Not Proved" upon investigation, and no departmental action ever being initiated. The appellant challenged the premature retirement order through a writ petition before the High Court, which was dismissed by the Single Judge and subsequently confirmed by the Division Bench. ## Key Holdings
- The Constitution of a Committee under Government Order No. 306-GAD of 2003 to consider premature retirement of government servants is valid under Article 226(2) and 226(3) of the Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services Regulations, 1956.
- Criminal allegations against a government servant that are found 'Not Proved' upon investigation cannot by themselves form a valid basis for compulsory premature retirement.
- A government servant who has been promoted and continued in service without departmental action cannot be subjected to arbitrary premature retirement without following due procedure under the applicable Service Regulations.
- Promotion orders issued by competent authority must be given effect to and cannot be arbitrarily stalled or refused by subordinate authorities.
- The writ court will not ordinarily interfere with a premature retirement order if the statutory procedure under the relevant Service Regulations has been substantially complied with.
