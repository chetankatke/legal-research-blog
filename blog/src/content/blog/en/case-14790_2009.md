# 14790_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 11.02.2009
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether a writ petition challenging disciplinary action can be dismissed by the High Court without recording any reasons. The appellant, a delinquent employee dismissed from service and later modified to compulsory retirement upon review, had his writ petition rejected by the Bombay High Court through a non-speaking order. The Supreme Court held that an order lacking reasons is "no order in the eye of law" and set aside the High Court's judgment. The matter was remanded to the High Court for fresh disposal after affording an opportunity of hearing, keeping all contentions of both parties open for adjudication.
## Key Holdings
- A non-speaking order is no order in the eye of law and must be set aside.
- An order that does not contain reasons requires to be set aside.
- The High Court's order dismissing the writ petition was set aside and the matter remanded for fresh disposal in accordance with law.
- The High Court was directed to afford opportunity of hearing to the parties before disposing of the writ petition.
- All contentions raised by both parties were kept open to be agitated before the High Court.
