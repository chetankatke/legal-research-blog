## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India disposed of a criminal appeal filed by Raj Kumar Luthra and another against an interim order of the Punjab & Haryana High Court, which had directed the appellants to appear before the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Gurdaspur, and deposit Rs. 15 lakhs. The Court made its earlier interim order dated 24th July 2009 absolute, thereby allowing the appellants to substitute the deposit of Rs. 15 lakhs (to be held without disbursement without the Court's leave) in lieu of the High Court's direction. ## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court made absolute its earlier interim order dated 24th July 2009 staying the High Court's direction, subject to the condition that the appellants deposit Rs. 15 lakhs.
- The amount of Rs. 15 lakhs deposited by the appellants pursuant to the interim order shall not be disbursed without leave of the Supreme Court.
- The appellants shall not be arrested in connection with the proceedings until further orders.
- Liberty was reserved to the appellants to request disposal of their Section 482 CrPC application pending before the High Court.
- The appeal was disposed of in terms of making the interim protection order absolute, subject to the conditions specified therein.
