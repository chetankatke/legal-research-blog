## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal by the State of Bihar challenges observations made by the High Court while dismissing a writ petition filed by accused Lalu Singh. The legal question concerns whether a charge-sheet filed under Section 173(2) of the CrPC by an Inspector of the Criminal Investigation Department (CID)—rather than the officer-in-charge of the police station—is legally valid, given that the DGP had entrusted the investigation to the CID. The High Court declined to quash the charge-sheet itself but observed that, under Sections 36 and 173(2) of the CrPC, the authority to file the final charge-sheet rests exclusively with the officer-in-charge of the police station, and higher officials can only supervise or participate in the investigation. The Supreme Court (per Chandramauli Kr. Prasad, J.) granted leave
## Key Holdings
- Under Section 36 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, higher police officials have the same powers as the officer-in-charge of a police station, but only with respect to supervising or participating in the investigation.
- Under Section 173(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, the final view on filing a charge sheet or final report must be taken by the officer-in-charge of the police station, and he alone has the authority to file the charge sheet.
- A charge sheet submitted by an Inspector of the Criminal Investigation Department, who is not the officer-in-charge of the concerned police station, is not a valid charge sheet under Section 173(2) of the Code.
- The Director General of Police has the power to entrust investigation of a case to the Criminal Investigation Department, but this does not alter the requirement that the charge sheet be filed by the officer-in-charge of the police station.
