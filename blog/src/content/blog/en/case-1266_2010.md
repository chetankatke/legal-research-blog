# 1266_2010

- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:**

## Summary
This appeal arises from a High Court order directing the trial court to summon two additional accused (the appellants) under Section 319 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, after the trial court had twice rejected applications by the complainant to add them as accused. The legal question concerns the proper stage and criteria for exercising the power under Section 319 Cr.P.C. to summon persons not named in the charge-sheet, where the complainant had specifically deposed about the appellants' role in the offense during his examination-in-chief. The Supreme Court (per Justice M.Y. Eqbal) was called upon to determine whether the High Court was justified in setting aside the trial court's rejection and directing summoning of the appellants, given the conflicting considerations of the prosecution's evidence and the stage of the trial.
## Key Holdings
- Section 319 of the Code of Criminal Procedure empowers the court to summon any person as accused who appears to have committed the offense based on evidence adduced during trial.
