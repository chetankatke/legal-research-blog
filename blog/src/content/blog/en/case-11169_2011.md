## Key Holdings
- The conviction under Section 302 IPC was upheld based on the totality of circumstances including the appellant's suspicious conduct and prior ill-treatment of the deceased wife.
- The Supreme Court affirmed that in cases of alleged suicide within marriage, the conduct of the accused both before and after the death is a relevant circumstance to determine guilt.
- The High Court and trial court rightly relied on the recovery and retention of property to sustain the conviction under Section 404 IPC relating to dishonest retention of stolen property.
- Concurrent running of sentences for offences under Sections 302 and 404 IPC was held to be proper as both arose from the same transaction.
- The court reiterated that appellate courts should not interfere with concurrent findings of fact unless the reasoning is perverse or based on misreading of evidence.
