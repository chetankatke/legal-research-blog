## Summary
In *Bhola Ram v. State of Punjab*, the Supreme Court considered whether the appellant was rightly convicted by the Trial Court and High Court for the dowry death of Janki Devi (his brother's wife) under Sections 304-B and 498-A IPC, allegedly driven by the family's repeated demands for additional dowry. While the prosecution alleged that Bhola Ram and other family members harassed Janki Devi for failing to meet demands (including Rs. 10,000 for a car and Rs. 30,000 for a service station), which led her to commit suicide by consuming poison on 6 September 1989, the Court found no evidence directly implicating the appellant. The Supreme Court acquitted Bhola Ram, holding that mere association with the marital family was insufficient to establish his culpability for the offence of dowry death.
