## Summary
In *Balwant Rai Saluja & Anr. v. Air India Ltd. & Ors.*, the Supreme Court of India addressed an appeal concerning the obligation to provide canteen facilities to workers under the Factories Act, 1948, and Rules 65–71 of the Delhi Factory Rules, 1950. The case involved Air India Ltd., the Hotel Corporation of India (a wholly owned subsidiary of Air India), and Chef Air Flight Catering (a unit
## Key Holdings
- By Section 3 of the Air Corporations (Transfer of Undertakings and Repeal) Act, 1994, Air India Limited stood vested in Indian Airlines Limited.
- Hotel Corporation of India is a wholly owned subsidiary of Air India, with Air India controlling the Board of Directors and empowered to issue binding directions on the Corporation's finances and business affairs.
- Section 46 of the Factories Act empowers the State Government to frame rules requiring factories ordinarily employing more than 250 workers to provide and maintain a canteen for workers.
- Rules 65 to 71 of the Delhi Factory Rules, 1950 were enacted in exercise of the power under Section 46 of the Factories Act, with Rule 65(1) to take effect on dates notified by the Chief Commissioner in the Official Gazette.
