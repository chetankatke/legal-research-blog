# 14315_2008

## Summary
**Summary:**

In this civil appeal, the appellants-landlords challenged the reversal of a trial court decree in their favor and the dismissal of their cross-objection, arising from a rent dispute with Punjab National Bank (PNB), which had occupied their premises in Kharagpur, West Bengal, under an unregistered lease agreement effective from 01.06.1978. The dispute centered on the bank's refusal to pay enhanced rent as recommended by its own regional building committee (Rs.2/- per square foot, with a 45% further enhancement), which led the landlords to file Money Suit No. 143 of 1994 claiming arrears of approximately Rs.9,46,892.50 plus 12% interest. The bank contested the claim, asserting that no concluded agreement for enhanced rent existed, as the higher authority disowned the Senior Manager's decision and the 1990 settlement/1993
## Key Holdings
- The delay in filing the application for substitution was condoned and the application itself was allowed.
- An agreement for enhancement of rent arrived at by the Senior Manager of the bank was not binding, as the higher authority of the bank disowned the Senior Manager's authority to take such a decision.
- The recommendations of the regional building committee dated 05.07.1993, which were signed by the Manager (GAD), Senior Manager, and Regional Manager, recommended renewal of the lease at an enhanced rent of Rs.2/- per square foot.
- The continued occupation of the premises by the defendant-bank at the old rate of rent, despite the committee's recommendation for enhancement, entitled the appellants-plaintiffs to claim arrears of rent at the enhanced rate.
- The appellants-plaintiffs instituted Money Suit No.143 of 1994 claiming arrears of rent calculated at the enhanced rate of Rs.2/- per square foot for the period from 01.07.1989 to 30.06.1994, along with interest at 12% per annum.
