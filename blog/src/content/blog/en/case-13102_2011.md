## Summary
**Summary:**

A life convict filed a contempt petition against the State of West Bengal and its officers, alleging willful disobedience of the Supreme Court's order dated 24.11.2010, which had directed the State to consider his premature release within eight weeks in light of the *State of Haryana & Ors. vs. Jagdish* (2010) decision. The petitioner contended that he had already undergone more than 20 years of imprisonment including remission, and that the State failed to release him in accordance with the West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992. ## Key Holdings
- The State must consider a life convict's claim for premature release/remission in accordance with the Statute or Policy applicable on the date of conviction, as established in State of Haryana & Ors. vs. Jagdish, 2010 (4) SCC 216.
- The period of actual imprisonment undergone by a life convict, including earned remission and set off, must be properly calculated to determine eligibility for remission under the applicable sentencing policy.
- When directing consideration of remission, the Court may fix a specific timeframe (here, eight weeks) within which the State must conclude its determination and pass appropriate orders.
- Non-compliance with the Court's specific directions regarding consideration of remission within the prescribed period constitutes grounds for initiating contempt proceedings against the State and its officers.
