## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court (Civil Appeal No. 4805 of 2013, arising from SLP(C) No. 19296/2013) heard an appeal by Kalpana Dilip Bahirat, who had been elected as a Corporator to the Pune Municipal Corporation for a seat reserved for Other Backward Classes, against the Bombay High Court's refusal to entertain her writ petition. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court refused to entertain the writ petition on the ground that the appellant did not approach it with clean hands.
- Under Article 243ZG of the Constitution, no election to any municipality shall be called in question except by an election petition presented in the manner provided by State law.
- The Municipal Commissioner declared the appellant's election ab initio null and void upon finding that the caste certificate produced was not genuinely issued by the Caste Scrutiny Committee.
- The existence of a pending election petition challenging the same election did not preclude the Municipal Commissioner from acting on information regarding a fraudulent caste certificate.
