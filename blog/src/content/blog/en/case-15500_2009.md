# 15500_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 03.02.2009
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
The Supreme Court of India allowed a civil appeal challenging the Karnataka High Court's reduction of compensation awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT) in a motor accident case, from Rs. 11 lakhs to Rs. 5,38,800. The Court held that, given the facts and circumstances, there was no justification for the High Court to have reduced the MACT's compensation award. Accordingly, the Court set aside the High Court's order and restored the MACT's original award. The appeal was disposed of with no order as to costs.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court held that the High Court erred in reducing the compensation awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal from Rs. 11 lakhs to Rs. 5,38,800/- without adequate justification.
- The Court set aside the High Court's order and restored the compensation as awarded by the MACT, finding no reason for the reduction in the peculiar facts of the case.
- The Court reaffirmed that appellate courts should not lightly disturb the compensation assessed by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal absent valid grounds.
- Leave was granted and the appeal was disposed of with no order as to costs.
