## Summary
This Supreme Court of India case arose from criminal appeals challenging the Punjab & Haryana High Court's judgment affirming the conviction of Mohan Lal and others under Sections 376(2)(g) and 366 of the IPC for gang rape and kidnapping. The prosecution's case was that a Class X student (the prosecutrix) was forced by her Physical Education Director to ride on the appellant's scooter and was subsequently taken to the house of a co-accused, where she was gang-raped. The trial court and High Court had convicted the appellants, and the matter came before the Supreme Court on appeal. The text excerpt provided is truncated and does not contain the Supreme Court's final reasoning or operative holding, so the ultimate disposition cannot be summarized from the available material.
