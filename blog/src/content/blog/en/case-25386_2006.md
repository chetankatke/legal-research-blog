## Summary
**Summary:**

In this appeal, the Supreme Court of India examined the conviction of the appellants under Section 302/34 IPC for allegedly poisoning Madhabi Patra, who died after falling ill at a ceremonial function called 'Sadh.' The prosecution's case rested primarily on the testimony of PW 12, the doctor who conducted the postmortem. However, the Court found that the postmortem report (Ext. P2) did not state the cause of death, as PW 12 had reserved his opinion pending the Chemical Examiner's report, and the Inquest Report noted that no froth was seen on the deceased's nostrils and mouth.
