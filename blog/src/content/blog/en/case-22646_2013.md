## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court can be invoked under Article 32 of the Constitution to challenge the Medical Council of India's decision revoking permission for renewal of admission in a medical college.
- Under Section 10A of the Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, the Medical Council of India is empowered to inspect medical colleges and assess compliance with prescribed standards before granting or renewing permission for admissions.
- The Medical Council of India may refuse to renew permission for admission of students where the college fails to meet the minimum statutory requirements regarding teaching faculty, residents, OPD attendance, and bed occupancy as mandated by the MCI Regulations.
- Before refusing renewal of permission, the Medical Council of India must afford the medical college a reasonable opportunity to submit a compliance report rectifying the deficiencies pointed out during inspection.
- The Executive Committee of the Medical Council of India, after considering inspection reports and compliance reports, is competent to recommend to the Central Government against renewal of permission for the academic session.
