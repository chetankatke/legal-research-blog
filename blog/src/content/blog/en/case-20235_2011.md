## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court disposed of a criminal appeal filed by Md. Shakir Jamal against the State of Jharkhand, arising from an earlier interim order dated 25.07.2011, which had granted the petitioner protection against arrest (anticipatory bail) on the condition of being released on bail to the satisfaction of the trial court if arrested. The case related to a minor discrepancy of Rs. 2,000/- found in an audit report during the petitioner's tenure as Block Development Officer (B.D.O.) at Maheshpur. After hearing counsel for both parties and examining the records, the Court (Justices H.L. Dattu and Jagdish Singh Khehar) made the interim order absolute and disposed of the appeal accordingly, effectively confirming the bail protection granted to the petitioner.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court made absolute its earlier order dated 25.07.2011, thereby confirming the grant of anticipatory bail to the petitioner.
- The Court relied upon the Audit Report showing only a minor discrepancy of Rs. 2000/- during the petitioner's tenure as B.D.O. at Maheshpur as a basis for the order.
- The Court directed that in case the petitioner is arrested, he shall be released on bail to the satisfaction of the trial court.
- Leave was granted in the Special Leave Petition and the appeal was disposed of in terms of the earlier interim order.
