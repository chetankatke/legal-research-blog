## Summary
This case concerns a challenge to the 1995 amendment to the Master Plan of Delhi, which permitted land originally earmarked for Nursery Schools to be used for other purposes, and the consequential allotment of a 1,000 sq. yard plot in Gulmohar Park to Kala Ashram (a School of Dance and Drama) instead of a Nursery School. ## Key Holdings
- The key question is whether the 1995 amendment to the Master Plan of Delhi permitting use of sites earmarked for nursery schools for other purposes is ultra vires the Delhi Development Authority Act, 1957.
- The Court considered whether allotment of 1000 sq. yards of land in Gulmohar Park, originally earmarked for a nursery school, to Kala Ashram for a School of Dance and Drama violated the Constitution and the DDA Act.
- A site initially allotted to Bethal Education Society for a nursery school but left unutilized for a long time could be reallocated by the DDA.
- The appellants were permitted to withdraw their writ petition (CWP No.1185/1998) with liberty to file a consolidated writ petition before the Division Bench after seeking to convert it into a public interest litigation.
- The appellants' challenge to the allotment had to be accompanied by a challenge to the notification amending the Master Plan, failing which the writ petition was liable to be dismissed.
