## Summary
In *RSRTC v. Smt. Santosh*, the Supreme Court addressed whether a "Jugaad" qualifies as a "motor vehicle" under Section 2(28) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, and whether it must be registered, insured, and driven only by a licensed driver. The matter arose from a vehicular accident claim where the High Court had fastened entire compensation liability on RSRTC while absolving the Jugaad's driver and owner. ## Key Holdings
- A 'Jugaad' is a motor vehicle as defined under Section 2(28) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.
- A 'Jugaad' must have registration and insurance, and its driver must possess a valid driving licence before it is permitted to ply on the road.
- No person should be permitted to ply a 'Jugaad' as plying it without registration, insurance, and a licensed driver violates the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.
- All States are under a legal obligation to enforce the Central Government circular dated 26.7.2007 treating 'Jugaad' as a vehicle under Section 2(28) of the Act.
- Enforcement of the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act and the rules framed thereunder lies within the jurisdiction of State Governments, which must be directed to ensure strict compliance.
