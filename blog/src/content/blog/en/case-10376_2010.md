## Summary
In *Thana Singh v. Central Bureau of Narcotics* (Criminal Appeal No. 1640 of 2010), the Supreme Court addressed the issue of an undertrial accused who had been incarcerated for over twelve years awaiting trial under the NDPS Act, despite the maximum punishment for the offence being only twenty years, with bail having been consistently denied. The Court noted the constrained applicability of its earlier ruling in *Supreme Court Legal Aid Committee v. Union of India* due to the stringent bail restrictions under Section 37 of the NDPS Act, while also expressing concern over the systemic failure to conduct NDPS trials expeditiously. Criticizing
