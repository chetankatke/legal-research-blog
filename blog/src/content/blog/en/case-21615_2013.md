## Summary
**Summary:**

In this civil appeal, the Supreme Court addressed whether the appellant was a lawful licensee or an unauthorized occupant of an industrial plot owned by the respondent (now represented by his legal representatives) on lease from MIDC. The respondent had initially permitted the appellant to use the shed solely for lunch and dining purposes for her employees, but she subsequently commenced manufacturing activities and made structural modifications without consent. Despite repeated notices and disconnection of utilities, the appellant refused to vacate the premises, leading the respondent to file a suit for possession and mesne profits. The Supreme Court upheld the concurrent findings of the trial court, District Judge, and Bombay High Court, holding that the appellant was neither a partner nor a licensee but an unauthorized occupant, and accordingly dismissed her appeals.
