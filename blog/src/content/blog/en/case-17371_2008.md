## Summary
**Summary:**

This criminal appeal arises from the death of Dr. Monica Thapar, wife of appellant Rajiv Thapar, who died in Surat in September 1992 after being initially treated for malaria and subsequently diagnosed with a hole in her heart. Her father, respondent Madan Lal Kapoor, suspected foul play (poisoning) because the body had turned blue, prompting the Sub-Divisional Magistrate to initiate inquest proceedings under Section 176 of the Cr.P.C. A Medical Board conducted a post-mortem and concluded that the death was due to cardiac decomposition. The legal question before
## Key Holdings
- When a body is suspected to have died from poisoning, the Sub-Divisional Magistrate may exercise powers under Section 176 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to initiate inquest proceedings.
- A Medical Board was duly constituted comprising specialists from Civil Hospital and G.B. Pant Hospital, New Delhi, to conduct the post-mortem examination of the deceased.
- The post-mortem examination by the constituted Medical Board concluded that the cause of death was cardiac decomposition.
