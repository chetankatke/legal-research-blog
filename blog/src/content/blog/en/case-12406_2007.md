## Summary
**Summary:**

This case involves two cross-appeals before the Supreme Court arising from a common judgment of the Rajasthan High Court (16 March 2007) in a murder case stemming from an incident on 4 April 2000, in which Mukhtyar Singh was attacked by a group of seven accused persons armed with axes, swords, lathis, and country-made pistols, resulting in his death. Criminal Appeal No. 1915/2008
## Key Holdings
- The court held that participation in a criminal act with a pre-arranged plan establishes common intention under Section 34 IPC, making each participant liable for the resulting offence.
- The court distinguished between common intention under Section 34 and common object under Section 149, noting that the former requires a pre-arranged plan while the latter requires membership in an unlawful assembly.
- The court held that mere presence or exhortation without participation in the actual assault is insufficient to convict an accused under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC.
- The court held that the mere pointing of weapons and exhortation to kill, without actual participation in the assault causing death, does not constitute common intention to cause death.
- The court held that acquittal of co-accused will be justified where the prosecution fails to establish their specific role in the actual commission of the offence beyond reasonable doubt.
