# 20390_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 31.12.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This case concerns the selection of officers into the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) from Non-State Civil Services under the IAS (Recruitment) Rules, 1954, which provides three sources of recruitment, including selection from gazetted officers not belonging to the State Civil Service. The appellant, an Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax, challenged the State of Andhra Pradesh's decision to restrict the "zone of consideration" for three category (iii) vacancies only to higher-ranked officers (Joint and Additional Commissioners of Commercial Tax). The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the respondents erroneously interpreted the eligibility criteria by excluding eligible lower-ranked officers from the selection panel. The CAT had rejected her interim application, and the Andhra Pradesh High Court dismissed her writ petition, leading to this appeal by special leave where leave was granted by the Supreme Court.
## Key Holdings
- Selection into the Indian Administrative Service is governed by the All India Services Act, 1951 and the IAS (Recruitment) Rules, 1954.
- The IAS (Recruitment) Rules provide three sources of recruitment: direct recruitment, promotion of substantive state civil service members, and selection from gazetted officers of non-state civil services.
- The selection of non-state civil service officers into the IAS must be made from among all eligible candidates holding gazetted posts in substantive capacity, and the zone of consideration cannot be arbitrarily restricted to only higher-ranking officers.
- An eligible officer cannot be denied consideration for IAS selection merely because she holds a post lower than other candidates in her department if she satisfies the prescribed eligibility criteria.
- The Supreme Court granted special leave to challenge the High Court's dismissal of the writ petition against the Central Administrative Tribunal's order rejecting the interim application.
