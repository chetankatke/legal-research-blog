## Summary
The Supreme Court of India heard a civil appeal filed by the Advocates Association, Bangalore, challenging the Karnataka High Court's order constituting a Special Investigation Team (SIT) to investigate the broadcasting of news items by television channels regarding a scuffle that occurred on 02.03.2012 at the City Civil Court Complex, Bangalore. The incident arose when a large crowd, including media persons, gathered for the production of former Karnataka Minister Janardhana Reddy in a CBI case, leading to violence between advocates, police, and media, resulting in injuries, property damage, and over 191 registered cases. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court has the inherent power to constitute a Special Investigation Team (SIT) to investigate incidents involving police atrocities and media conduct in court premises.
- Media reporting of court proceedings must be balanced with the need to maintain order and decorum in court complexes, and cannot be exercised at the cost of public safety.
- An in-house inquiry by a senior police officer does not preclude the High Court from ordering an independent investigation through an SIT when circumstances warrant it.
- The right of advocates to practice their profession freely must be protected, and police excesses against advocates during incidents in court premises are subject to judicial scrutiny.
- The broadcasting of sensationalized news regarding incidents in court complexes requires examination to determine whether it incites violence or disrupts judicial proceedings.
