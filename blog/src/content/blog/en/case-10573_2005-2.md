## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court addressed the penological approach in cases of murders that shock the conscience of the court and community, coupled with extreme revulsion.
- The case examines the scope of consideration of a Death Reference by the High Court under Chapter XXVIII of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.
- The decision considers whether there is any restriction on the exercise of power under Section 432 Cr.PC for remission and Section 433 Cr.PC for commutation in cases of minimum sentence.
- The Sessions Court followed the precedent in Kamta Tiwari vs. State of Madhya Pradesh, where rape and murder of a young child warranted the death sentence.
- The High Court erred in declining to confirm the death sentence without properly referencing aggravating or mitigating circumstances or special reasons, failing to apply the rarest of rare cases standard.
