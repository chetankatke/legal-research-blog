## Summary
**Summary:**

This Special Leave Petition arises from a Madras High Court judgment concerning a granite quarrying partnership firm (M/s PRP Exports) whose operations were sealed by Tamil Nadu authorities on allegations of unauthorized quarrying. The legal question before the Supreme Court centers on whether the State could suspend the petitioner's mining operations and seal its factory without first issuing show cause notices regarding cancellation of the quarrying licenses, notwithstanding serious allegations of illegal mining worth crores of rupees and pending criminal investigations against the partners. The High Court had balanced the petitioner's right to livelihood (given no cancellation notice was issued) against the State's interest in preserving evidence and ensuring fair investigation, disposing of the writ petitions with conditional directions. ## Key Holdings
- The State itself conceded before the High Court that the order of sealing the Petitioner's factory premises, vehicles and instruments was illegal and could not be supported in law.
- In the absence of a show cause notice for cancellation of the leases or licences granted to the Petitioner, there is no justification for the authorities to stop the mining operations of the lessee and thereby deprive thousands of employees of their livelihood.
- While the investigation of serious allegations of illegal mining must not be hampered, a blanket order in favour of the petitioner restraining all investigative steps is also not permissible in law.
- Where a petitioner undertakes to cooperate with the criminal investigation by furnishing all sealed documents, records and books of accounts, to not tamper with evidence, and to not threaten witnesses, courts may permit continued operations subject to such conditions to balance competing equities.
- The right of livelihood of workers engaged in lawful mining operations cannot be taken away merely on the allegation of unauthorized quarrying, without following the procedural requirement of issuing a show cause notice for cancellation of the lease or licence.
