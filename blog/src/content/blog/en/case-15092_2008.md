## Summary
**Summary:**

In this criminal appeal, the Supreme Court considered the conviction of the appellant under Sections 143, 147, 148, and 302 read with Section 149 of the IPC for the murder of Purushothaman, which had been confirmed by the Kerala High Court. The central legal question was whether the prosecution had sufficiently established the appellant's identity as one of the assailants, given that the Trial Court had acquitted the other five co-accused due to failure of identification. The Court upheld the conviction, holding that the appellant's specific identification by eyewitness PW-1 in the FIR, corroborated by PW-2, and further supported by the medical evidence linking the injuries to the pestle
## Key Holdings
- The appellant was specifically implicated in the FIR by eyewitness PW-1, providing a strong basis for his identification as the assailant.
- The testimony of PW-2 corroborated PW-1's identification of the appellant, strengthening the prosecution's case against him.
- The deadly blow inflicted by the appellant using the pestle (MO1) on the deceased's head was consistent with the injuries recorded in the Post Mortem Report.
- The five other accused persons were rightly acquitted as their identity and presence as members of the unlawful assembly could not be conclusively established by acceptable evidence.
- The High Court correctly confirmed the appellant's conviction under Sections 143, 147, 148, and 302 read with Section 149 IPC after re-appreciating the entire evidence on record.
