## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal under Section 125 of the Electricity Act, 2003, filed by U.P. Power Corporation Ltd. against N.T.P.C. Ltd., concerns the CERC's order permitting capitalization of an additional Rs. 4.521 crores over the approved cost of the Feroz Gandhi Unchahar Thermal Power Station Stage-I for the tariff period 2001-2004, with the respondent also being granted return on equity and interest on loan on this amount. The central legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the CERC and Appellate Tribunal correctly interpreted Regulation 2.5 of the CERC (Terms and Conditions for Determination of Tariff) Regulations, 2001, in allowing such additional capital expenditure to be considered for tariff fixation without prior approval from the Central Electricity Authority (CEA). ## Key Holdings
- The primary question for consideration is whether additional capital expenditure can be taken into account for tariff fixation without prior approval of the Central Electricity Authority under Regulation 2.5 of the CERC (Terms and Conditions for Determination of Tariff) Regulations, 2001.
- Both the Central Electricity Regulatory Commission and the Appellate Tribunal rejected the contention that approval by the Central Electricity Authority was mandatory for additional capital expenditure to be considered in tariff determination.
- The CERC permitted capitalization of Rs.4.521 crores over the approved cost for completion of Feroz Gandhi Unchahar Thermal Power Station Stage-I for the period 1st April, 2001 to 31st March, 2004, and allowed the respondent return on equity and interest on loan on the capitalized amount payable with the tariff for 2004-2009.
- The Central Government had approved the takeover cost of the generating station at Rs.925 crores, with subsequent CEA approval of an additional Rs.2.85 crores under the Environment Action Plan, taking the total approved project cost to Rs.927.85 crores.
