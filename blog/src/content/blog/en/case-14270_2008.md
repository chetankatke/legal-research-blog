## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a dispute where the respondent (Reliance Communication) obtained an ex parte decree against the appellant (TVC Skyshop) in a summary suit under Order XXXVII Rule 3 CPC for non-payment of dues related to mobile phone connections, after the appellant failed to seek leave to defend or appear despite being served with summons. The legal question before the Court was whether such a summary decree could be set aside under Order IX Rule 13 CPC, which permits setting aside ex parte decrees only on the grounds of non-service of summons or sufficient cause for non-appearance. Both
## Key Holdings
- Order IX Rule 13 of the Code of Civil Procedure, which permits setting aside an ex parte decree, does not apply to a decree passed under Order XXXVII Rule 3 CPC.
- A decree passed under Order XXXVII for the defendant's failure to file an application for leave to defend or appear is not an ex parte decree that can be set aside under Order IX Rule 13 CPC.
- The two grounds contemplated under Order IX Rule 13, namely non-service of summons and sufficient cause for non-appearance, are not available for setting aside a decree passed under Order XXXVII CPC.
- When a defendant served with summons in a summary suit under Order XXXVII fails to apply for leave to defend or appear, the court is justified in passing a decree in terms of clauses (a) and (b) of the plaint.
