## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court is hearing an appeal by Pariwar Co-operative Housing Society Ltd. challenging the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission's order directing it (along with its former Chief Promoter, respondent No. 2) to refund a member (respondent No. 1) with 9% interest, after the member was neither allotted an apartment nor refunded his payments despite an earlier State Commission order in his favor. The core legal question is whether the co-operative housing society can be held jointly liable for the mismanagement of funds and non-allotment of flats allegedly caused by its former Chief Promoter. ## Key Holdings
- A cooperative housing society is liable to refund amounts paid by a member when it fails to allot an apartment as promised.
- Both the housing society and its Chief Promoter can be held jointly responsible for failure to refund the member's money due to mismanagement of society funds.
- The National Commission upheld the State Commission's award directing payment of the refund amount with interest at 9% per annum.
- A cooperative society cannot escape liability for non-allotment of an apartment or non-refund of money by attributing the fault solely to its office bearers.
- The Consumer Protection Act, 1986 applies to disputes between a cooperative housing society and its members regarding non-delivery of allotted flats.
