# K. Raheja Development Corporation v. State of Karnataka

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 19.8.2008
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This Supreme Court of India matter concerns whether the legal position laid down in *K. Raheja Development Corporation v. State of Karnataka* (2005) — that a development agreement coupled with a tripartite agreement constitutes a "works contract" under the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 — is correct. A two-Judge Bench in *Larsen & Toubro v. State of Karnataka* expressed prima facie difficulty with that proposition, noting that the developer had merely undertaken to develop the property owner's land, and the show cause notice only characterized the tripartite agreement as a works contract without alleging monetary consideration in the development agreement. The Bench framed the key question as whether the department could treat the tripartite agreement as a works contract when the development agreement itself was not one. Accordingly, the matter was referred to a larger Bench to determine the correctness of the *Raheja Development* ratio.
## Key Holdings
- Whether a Development Agreement between a developer and a landowner constitutes a 'works contract' under the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, is a question of legal importance requiring determination by a larger Bench.
- The legal distinction between a 'contract of sale' and a 'works contract' is determinative of the tax liability of a developer under the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957.
- If the Development Agreement is not a works contract, the Department cannot rely on a subsequent Tripartite Agreement to characterize the transaction as a works contract for tax purposes.
- The correctness of the ratio in Paragraph 20 of K. Raheja Development Corporation v. State of Karnataka (2005) 5 SCC 162 was referred to a larger Bench for reconsideration.
- The existence of monetary consideration under a development contract is a material factor in determining whether the transaction is a works contract subject to sales tax.
