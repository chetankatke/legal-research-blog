## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard an appeal challenging a Gujarat High Court judgment that set aside the termination of a Civil Judge (Junior Division) during her probationary period. The central legal question was whether the termination was a stigmatic removal attracting the safeguards of Article 311(2) of the Constitution (which require the employee to be informed of charges and given a hearing), or merely a discontinuation of probation due to unsuitability, which does not require such procedural protections. The respondent contended that adverse remarks and the manner of her termination cast a stigma, while the appellants (Registrar General of Gujarat High Court) argued it was a bona fide assessment of her suitability during probation. ## Key Holdings
- Termination of a probationer on the ground of unsuitability is not stigmatic and does not attract Article 311(2) of the Constitution.
- A termination of service will be treated as stigmatic if it casts a slur on the character or reputation of the employee, thereby attracting the safeguards of Article 311(2).
- Where a termination is stigmatic, the minimum requirements of Article 311(2) must be complied with, namely informing the employee of the charges and affording an opportunity of being heard.
- The nature of the termination order, and not the label attached to it, determines whether it is stigmatic and whether constitutional safeguards apply.
