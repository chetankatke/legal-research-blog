## Summary
In *Ashok Kumar Sharma v. State of Rajasthan* (Criminal Appeal No. 817 of 2008), the Supreme Court considered whether the empowered officer under Section 50 of the NDPS Act, 1985 is legally bound to apprise an accused of the right to be searched before a Gazetted Officer or Magistrate, and whether this procedure is mandatory. The appellant had been convicted by the Sessions Court and High Court for possession of 344 grams of heroin, challenging the conviction on the ground that the Section 50 procedure was not properly complied with. The case examined the evidence of PW1 (Additional SP) regarding whether the accused was duly informed of his statutory right and whether the search and seizure could be sustained. ## Key Holdings
- The empowered officer under Section 50 of the NDPS Act is legally obliged to apprise the accused of his right to be searched before a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate, and compliance with this provision is mandatory.
- Written consent given by the accused on the notice (Ex.P-3) to be searched by the empowered officer constitutes valid compliance with the procedural safeguards of Section 50 of the NDPS Act.
- Non-compliance with the procedure under Section 50 of the NDPS Act vitiates the conviction of the accused in cases involving recovery of narcotic substances.
