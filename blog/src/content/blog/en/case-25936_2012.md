# 25936_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** July 8, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
**Summary:**

In this appeal, the State of U.P. challenged the Labour Court's award (upheld by the High Court) of reinstatement with 50% back-wages in favor of Gamal Yadav, who had worked for about 20 years in the appellant's establishment before being discontinued. The appellant contended that the activity of extracting silk from silk worms did not constitute an "industry" within the meaning of labor law. The Supreme Court modified the award by reducing the back-wages from 50% to 25%, while directing reinstatement with continuity of service. The Court, however, left the larger question of law—whether such silk-related activity qualifies as an "industry"—open for future determination.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court modified the Labour Court award reducing back-wages from 50% to 25% while granting reinstatement with continuity of service.
- The respondent, who had worked for about 20 years, was held entitled to reinstatement upon his illegal discontinuance from service.
- The question of law regarding whether the activity of taking out silk from silk worms constitutes an 'industry' under the relevant industrial law was left open for determination in future cases.
- The Court exercised its discretion to balance the interests of the long-term workman with the financial burden on the employer by reducing the back-wages component.

In our view, the impugned order of award can 
be  modified  by  granting  25%  back-wages.  We 
accordingly modify the orders passed by the Labour 
Court  as  also  the  High  Court  and  direct  that  the 
respondent  shall  be  reinstated  in  service  with 
continuity of service and 25%  back-wages.  However, 
the  question  of  law  raised  in  this  appeal  shall 
remain open.
