# 17868_2007

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed whether a mortgagor can induct a person as a tenant in a mortgaged property, pendente lite, to the prejudice of the mortgagee, in violation of Section 52 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (doctrine of lis pendens). The dispute arose from a 1953 mortgage executed by Vijaysingh Mohta in favour of Gangabai, with subsequent prolonged litigation over the mortgaged property spanning several decades, including partition, auction purchases, and multiple civil suits for rent recovery. The mortgagors (Respondents Nos. 2 and 3) allegedly inducted tenants and collected rents without the mortgagee's consent during the pendency of the proceedings. The Court granted leave to appeal to determine whether such induction of tenants by the mortgagor during pending litigation was void under Section 52 of the Transfer of Property Act.
