## Summary
**Summary:**

This criminal appeal arises from the Punjab and Haryana High Court's judgment confirming the conviction and death sentence of the appellants under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC for the brutal murder of Kulwant Singh, his wife Sarabjit Kaur, and their two young sons on the intervening night of 20-21 August 2000. The prosecution case was that the murders stemmed from a long-standing land dispute, where the appellants and their associates were pressuring the complainant, Sharam Singh (PW1), to mutate ancestral land shares in their names, and had even issued prior death threats. The appellants, armed with a Toka, Datar, and Kirpan, attacked the sleeping family at around 1:00
## Key Holdings
- The High Court rightly dismissed the appeal and confirmed the death sentence of the appellants who were convicted under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC for the murder of four family members.
- The prosecution established a clear motive of land dispute, as the accused had been pressuring the victim to mutate ancestral land in their names and had made specific death threats 8 to 9 days prior to the incident.
- The premeditated and coordinated nature of the attack, with multiple accused arriving armed with Toka, Datar, and Kirpan in the early hours, established common intention under Section 34 IPC.
- The prior threats made by the appellants, including sitting outside the victim's house a day before the incident and warning that they would not be spared, demonstrated clear pre-meditation and intention to commit murder.
- The conviction was sustainable as the appellants acted in concert with shared common intention to eliminate the entire family to grab the disputed property.
