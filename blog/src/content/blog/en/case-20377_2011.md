# 20377_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 23.11.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from an income tax dispute where the appellants (Tek Ram, through legal representatives) sought to introduce additional documents before the Supreme Court that had not been placed before the High Court of Punjab and Haryana earlier. The Court found these newly filed documents to be of some relevance to the case and held that they ought to be considered by the High Court before rendering a final decision. Accordingly, the Supreme Court set aside the High Court's order and remanded the matter back for fresh disposal of the income tax appeal, leaving all contentions of both parties open and awarding no costs.
## Key Holdings
- When a party places relevant documents before the Supreme Court that were not previously before the High Court, the matter should be remanded to the High Court for fresh consideration of those documents.
- The Supreme Court may set aside the High Court's order and remand the case when additional relevant documents are produced which were not available during the earlier proceedings.
- The High Court must examine the relevance of newly produced documents before deciding whether the appeal should be allowed or rejected.
- Upon remand, all contentions of both parties are left open to be raised and decided afresh.
- The Civil Appeal was disposed of without any order as to costs.
