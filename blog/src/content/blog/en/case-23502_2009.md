## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard an appeal by Smt. K. Vijaya Lakshmi, an advocate who had been selected as a Junior Civil Judge in Andhra Pradesh (at Serial No. 26 in the general category merit list) but was denied appointment on the ground that her husband allegedly had close links with the CPI (Maoist) party, a prohibited organization. The legal question before the Court was whether the State could lawfully deny appointment to a duly selected candidate based solely on adverse antecedents attributed to her spouse, without any material showing the candidate herself was disloyal or unsuitable.
