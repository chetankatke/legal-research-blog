# 16345_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 3.2.2011

## Summary
This appeal arose from a partition suit where the appellant, a sister, sought to amend her plaint to include the property of her deceased brother (Plot No.1401, Ghogha Road, Bhavnagar), in addition to the father's property she had originally claimed. Both the Trial Court and the Gujarat High Court rejected her amendment application. The Supreme Court held that since partition had not yet occurred, the appellant would have a share in the deceased brother's property, and it was desirable that this claim be decided in the pending suit. ## Key Holdings
- The property of a deceased brother in a family partition suit is one in which the surviving sister/appellant is entitled to a share and should be included for determination.
- It is desirable that the share of the deceased brother's property be decided in the pending partition suit rather than requiring a separate proceeding.
- The application for amendment of the plaint to include the deceased brother's property (Plot No.1401 Ghogha Road, Bhavnagar) was allowed, setting aside the orders of the Trial Court and the High Court.
- The appellant was directed to amend the plaint within four weeks, and respondents were given four weeks thereafter to file their written statement, with all contentions regarding the added property reserved for trial.
