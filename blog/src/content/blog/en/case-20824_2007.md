## Summary
In *Sudish Prasad & Ors. v. Babui Jonhia alias Manorma Devi & Ors.*, the Supreme Court (Civil Appeal No. 1012 of 2013) heard an appeal against a Patna High Court Division Bench judgment that declared the plaintiff-respondent the absolute owner of suit properties originally held by Sukai Mahto. The central legal question concerned the succession rights to the property of Sukai Mahto, who died in 1946 leaving a widow (Mst. Parbatia) and a minor daughter (the plaintiff), with the widow having subsequently remarried in *Sagai* form, and whether alleged fraudulent *zerpesgi* deeds executed by the court-appointed guardian Bal Kishun Mahto in favour of his nephew could defeat the daughter's claim. ## Key Holdings
- Upon the remarriage of a Hindu widow in Sagai form, the inheritance of her deceased husband's property passes to his daughter as the next legal heir.
- A court-appointed guardian of a minor or incapacitated person is in a fiduciary position and cannot alienate the ward's property for inadequate consideration or for his own benefit.
- Zerpesgi deeds executed by a guardian in favour of his own relatives without consideration and while the ward was under disability are void and unenforceable against the ward's heirs.
- A person declared a minor by court order remains under legal disability regarding property matters notwithstanding any later claim of attainment of majority in personal understanding.
- The plaintiff, as the daughter and sole surviving heir after her mother's remarriage, became the absolute owner of the suit properties inherited from her father.
