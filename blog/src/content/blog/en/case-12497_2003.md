# 12497_2003

- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:**

## Summary
In this criminal appeal arising from a dowry death conviction, the Supreme Court addressed three principal issues, primarily whether the appellant was a juvenile under Section 2(k) of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 at the time of the offence. Based on a report called for by the Court, it was determined that the appellant was indeed a juvenile on the date of the occurrence. While the conviction under Sections 304-B and 498-A IPC was upheld, the Court held that the appellant must be dealt with in accordance with Section 20 read with Section 15 of the Juvenile Justice Act, 2000. The Court also proposed issuing directions to all Magistrates to prevent the recurrence of juveniles being erroneously tried by regular criminal courts.
## Key Holdings
- The appellant was determined to be a juvenile as defined under Section 2(k) of the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 on the date of the offence.
- The conviction of the appellant under Section 304-B (dowry death) and Section 498-A IPC was upheld on merits despite the appellant being a juvenile.
- The appellant was to be dealt with under Section 20 read with Section 15 of the Juvenile Justice Act, 2000, regarding the quantum of sentence.
- The Court issued directions to all Magistrates to inquire about the age of accused persons at the earliest stage to avoid trial of juveniles by regular criminal courts.
- The case arose from a dowry death where the victim was set on fire for failing to meet dowry demands, resulting in her death.
