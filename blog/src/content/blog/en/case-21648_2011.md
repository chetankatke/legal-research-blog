## Summary
This Supreme Court cross-appeal between competing footwear manufacturers (Paragon Rubber Industries and Pragathi Rubber Mills) raised the legal question of whether the District Court at Kottayam, Kerala had territorial jurisdiction to entertain a suit under the Copyright Act, 1957 and the Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, when the defendant was located in Jalandhar, Punjab. The High Court had set aside the trial court's order holding that jurisdiction existed under Section 62(2) of the 1957 Act, and directed the plaintiff to amend the plaint to establish maintainability. The Supreme Court was called upon to determine the correctness of the High Court's ruling on the territorial jurisdiction issue in cross-appeals arising from the same civil revision petition. ## Key Holdings
- The issue of territorial jurisdiction in a suit for infringement of copyright and trademark must be determined as a preliminary issue at the earliest stage of the proceedings.
- Section 62(2) of the Copyright Act, 1957 is the governing provision for determining the territorial jurisdiction of a court in copyright infringement suits.
- A court has jurisdiction to entertain a suit for infringement of copyright only if a part of the alleged infringing activity was carried out within its territorial limits.
- The plaintiff must plead and establish the specific acts of infringement committed within the territorial jurisdiction of the court where the suit is filed.
- Where the plaint is found to be deficient on the issue of territorial jurisdiction, the plaintiff may be granted liberty to amend the plaint to cure the defect and establish jurisdiction.
