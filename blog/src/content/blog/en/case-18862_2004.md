## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from the termination of the appellant, Devendra Kumar, from the post of Constable in Uttaranchal, on the ground that he had suppressed the fact of his involvement in a criminal case while submitting his affidavit at the time of joining training. The appellant, who had successfully cleared physical, written, and medical tests, was discharged without inquiry in April 2002 after character
## Key Holdings
- A temporary government servant can be removed from service without holding a formal inquiry.
- The disclosure obligations under clauses 4 and 7 of the Proforma of Affidavit must be read together to determine what criminal information a candidate is required to disclose.
- Termination of service is justified where a selected candidate suppresses material information about involvement in a criminal case in the affidavit submitted during training.
- The burden lies on the candidate to disclose involvement in any criminal case regardless of whether the case was pending or resulted only in submission of a final report.
