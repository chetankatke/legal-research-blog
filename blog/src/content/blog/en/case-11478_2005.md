## Summary
In this case arising from a 1999 bus accident in Himachal Pradesh where a vehicle rolled down a hill, killing and injuring several passengers, the Supreme Court addressed whether National Insurance Co. Ltd. could deny compensation liability on the ground that the bus was carrying more passengers than its permitted capacity, relying on its earlier decision in *National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Anjana Shyam*. The Insurance Company argued that overloading the bus beyond its sanctioned capacity absolved it of liability to pay compensation to the victims and their legal representatives. ## Key Holdings
- The Insurance Company is not liable to pay compensation when the vehicle was carrying passengers beyond its permitted capacity at the time of the accident.
- The Motor Accident Claims Tribunal found that the bus was not overloaded beyond its permitted capacity, as the total number of injured and deceased persons did not exceed 51 claim petitions filed.
- The decision relies on the precedent in National Insurance Co. Ltd. Vs. Anjana Shyam & Ors. regarding the insurer's liability in cases of overloading.
- Compensation was directed to be paid to the injured persons and to the legal representatives of the deceased passengers involved in the accident.
