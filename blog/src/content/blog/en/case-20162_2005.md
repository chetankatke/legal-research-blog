# Court in U.P. Jal Nigam and others vs. Prabhat Chandra Jain and others

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** December  12,  2006
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This case was referred to a larger Bench of the Supreme Court to resolve an apparent inconsistency between two prior decisions on the legal question of whether the downgradation of an Annual Confidential Report (ACR) from "Very Good" to "Good" constitutes an "adverse entry" that must be communicated to the employee. In *U.P. Jal Nigam vs. Prabhat Chandra Jain*, the Court had held that such a downgradation may not ordinarily be an adverse entry, since both gradings are positive, and therefore need not be communicated. The referral order was necessitated because this position appeared to conflict with the Court's later decision in *Union of India vs. Major Bahadur Singh*, requiring authoritative resolution by a larger Bench.
## Key Holdings
- A downgraded entry in an Annual Confidential Report, such as falling from 'very good' to 'good', does not ordinarily constitute an adverse entry since both gradings are positive and does not require mandatory communication to the employee.
- Only entries reflecting an adverse element that materially affects the employee's career are compulsorily communicable to the concerned employee.
- The Departmental Promotion Committee must assess an employee's suitability for promotion on the basis of service records, with particular reference to the Confidential Reports for the five preceding years, irrespective of qualifying service prescribed in the Recruitment Rules.
- Where the grading system contains positive categories like 'outstanding,' 'very good,' and 'good,' a step-down within these positive gradings ordinarily lacks the adverseness required to trigger communication obligations under service rules.
- The benchmark for promotion fixed as 'Very Good' under applicable Office Memoranda governs the eligibility criteria for consideration by the Departmental Promotion Committee.
