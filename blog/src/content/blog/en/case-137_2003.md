## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Ramji Gupta & Anr. v. Gopi Krishan Agrawal (D) & Ors.*, the Supreme Court addressed a dispute over shop no. 53/11, Nayayaganj, Kanpur Nagar, where the appellants claimed to be tenants/inheritors of tenancy rights, while the respondents asserted ownership based on a registered adoption deed. The core legal question was whether a landlord-tenant relationship existed between the appellants (heirs of original tenant Badri Vishal) and the respondents (who claimed through Janki Bibi's adoption of Gopi Krishan). The High Court had affirmed the Small Causes Court's finding that no such landlord-tenant relationship existed, rejecting the appellants' writ petition. The Supreme
## Key Holdings
- The courts below correctly held that the relationship of landlord and tenant did not exist between the respondents and the appellants.
- Janki Bibi (2nd) was only a life estate holder in respect of Radhey Shyam's property and could not transfer absolute ownership beyond her lifetime.
- A life estate holder has no right to alienate the property beyond her life interest, and any such transfer cannot create tenancy rights in favor of the transferee after her death.
- The High Court rightly affirmed the judgments of the Small Causes Court and the Revisional Court dismissing the appellants' claim.
- The plea of Gopi Krishan being the adopted son of Janki Bibi (2nd) was a material issue, and without establishing valid adoption, no ownership rights in the suit property could be claimed.
