## Summary
**Summary:**

This Special Leave Petition arises from a Dental College operated by the petitioner Charitable Trust, which sought to increase its BDS intake capacity from 50 to 100 seats for the academic year 2013-14. The Central Government rejected the expansion scheme on the ground that the petitioner had failed to obtain recognition of its existing 50-seat capacity, which was a statutory precondition under Section 10-A of the Dentists Act, 1948, and it could not produce the essential documents by the cutoff date of 31.12.2012. The Kerala High Court dismissed the writ petition challenging this refusal, and the Supreme Court was called upon to determine whether the High Court erred in upholding the Government's decision. ## Key Holdings
- Recognition of the existing course capacity is a mandatory pre-condition for processing an application to increase intake of seats under Section 10-A of the Dentists Act, 1948.
- The Central Government is justified in returning an application that fails to furnish essential documents by the statutory last date of 31st December for forwarding to the Dental Council of India.
- A writ of mandamus cannot be issued to compel statutory authorities to relax mandatory eligibility conditions or bypass the prescribed time schedule for considering schemes for enhancement of seats.
- The scheme for increase of seats must fulfil the eligibility criteria as determined by the Central Government, and non-fulfilment thereof warrants rejection of the application.
- The petitioner having failed to obtain recognition of the existing 50 BDS seats could not seek enhancement of capacity, and the High Court rightly dismissed the writ petition challenging the rejection order.
