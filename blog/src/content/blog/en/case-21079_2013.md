## Summary
In *Wajidmiya Abdul Raheman Shaikh v. Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation*, the Supreme Court heard a batch of civil appeals arising from multiple interim orders passed by the Bombay High Court (Aurangabad Bench) in land acquisition matters, which had rejected the appellants' "reasonable prayer" relating to the release of enhanced compensation. The appellants, who had been awarded enhanced compensation, sought release of those amounts pending the appeals. Allowing the appeals, the Court directed that 50% of the enhanced compensation be released to the appellants without security, while the remaining 50% be released upon furnishing security to the satisfaction of the Collector. The Court condoned the delay in filing the special leave petitions and disposed of the appeals accordingly.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court condoned the delay in filing the Special Leave Petitions.
- Leave was granted to appeal the High Court's interim orders rejecting the appellants' prayer for release of enhanced compensation.
- The Court held that the appellants' prayer for release of enhanced land acquisition compensation should be accepted and granted.
- Fifty percent of the enhanced compensation was directed to be released to the appellants without security.
- The balance fifty percent of the enhanced compensation was ordered to be released only upon the appellants furnishing security to the satisfaction of the Collector.
