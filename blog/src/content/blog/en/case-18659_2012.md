## Summary
This writ petition under Article 32 of the Constitution concerned the fate of Saba and Farha, minor craniopagus (conjoined) twins whose surgical separation carried significant mortality risks. The petitioner sought the Court's intervention to arrange specialized medical treatment, and the Court exercised its parens patriae jurisdiction to direct that the twins be transported to AIIMS, New Delhi by air ambulance for examination by a medical team. While the parents ultimately declined the proposed medical intervention and sought only financial assistance to care for the twins, the Court emphasized its fundamental duty to safeguard the interests of minor children, particularly those in life-threatening medical conditions. The matter was pending further consideration based on the AIIMS medical team's examination and recommendations regarding the feasibility and risks of separation surgery.
