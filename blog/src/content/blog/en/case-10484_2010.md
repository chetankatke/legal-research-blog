## Summary
In *State of Haryana v. Rajinder*, the Supreme Court addressed the State's appeal challenging the High Court's acquittal of the respondent from charges of murder and other offenses under the IPC in connection with the death of one Lachhman, where 10 accused had been convicted by the Sessions Court. The State contended that the High Court erred in distinguishing the respondent from the other co-accused, relying on PW-8's testimony that he had given a "Lalkara" and a Gandasa blow on the left cheek of the deceased. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court was justified in acquitting the respondent where the defense witness DW-4, supported by Exhibit D-C (Haryana State Electricity Board register), established that the accused was working at another place at the time of the offense.
- The medical evidence contradicted the prosecution's case, as there was no injury on the left cheek of the deceased as alleged, and the fatal Injury No. 5 was located on the chest.
- The Supreme Court declined to interfere with the High Court's acquittal, recognizing that the Sessions Judge's assessment of the defense witness could be validly reassessed on appeal.
- Where the prosecution's version of the assault is belied by the medical evidence regarding the nature and location of injuries, the benefit of doubt must be extended to the accused.
- The mere association of an accused with a group of perpetrators is insufficient to sustain conviction in the absence of reliable evidence linking him to the specific criminal act.
