# 13978_2010

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 27.1.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal concerns whether a claimant is entitled to compassionate appointment under a 1983 Scheme or merely ex-gratia payment under a new 2006 Scheme, where the deceased bank employee's son had applied for appointment before the new scheme's commencement. The Rajasthan High Court had directed the Bank to appoint the respondent under the 1983 Scheme, reasoning that the cause of action arose prior to the new scheme. The Supreme Court is reviewing this decision, emphasizing the settled legal principles that compassionate appointment is not a vested right but an exception to Articles 14 and 16, available only when the family faces genuine financial hardship, and the candidate must meet eligibility requirements.
## Key Holdings
- Every appointment to public office must be made strictly in compliance with the mandatory requirements of Articles 14 and 16 of the Constitution.
- Compassionate appointment is not a vested right and cannot be claimed as a matter of right, as it is merely an exception to alleviate financial hardship caused by the death of a government employee in harness.
- Mere death of an employee in harness does not entitle the family to compassionate employment; the competent authority must examine the financial condition of the family and the claimant must possess the required eligibility for the post.
- Where the cause of action for compassionate appointment arose prior to commencement of a new scheme providing only for ex-gratia payment, the application must be considered under the previous scheme that provided for compassionate appointment.
- Courts should not stretch compassionate appointment provisions by liberal interpretation beyond their scope.
