## Summary
**Summary:**

This case involves J. Sundramma, a widow who sought to have a plot of land originally allotted to her deceased husband transferred to her name by the Mysore Urban Development Authority (MUDA). Although the allotment was made in her favor in 1998 at a total price of Rs. 10,000, she paid only Rs. 7,657 due to illiteracy, leaving a balance of Rs. 2,343 unpaid for several years, prompting MUDA to cancel the allotment in
## Key Holdings
- The appellant's failure to pay the balance sale consideration of Rs.2343/- within the stipulated time justified cancellation of the site allotment by the Mysore Urban Development Authority.
- The widow of the original allottee, notwithstanding her illiteracy, was required to exercise due diligence in making payments in accordance with the terms of the allotment order.
- The High Court held that the lapse of eleven years since the allotment disentitled the appellant from claiming any equity in her favour.
- Both the writ petition and the writ appeal were dismissed on the ground that the appellant had not demonstrated due diligence in completing payment of the full sale consideration.
- Once the stipulated period for payment had expired and no extension was granted, the Mysore Urban Development Authority acted within its authority in cancelling the allotment.
