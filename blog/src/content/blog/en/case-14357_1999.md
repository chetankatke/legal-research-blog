# 14357_1999

## Summary
**Summary:**

These civil appeals, referred to a three-Judge Bench of the Supreme Court, arose from a full Bench judgment of the Kerala High Court dismissing writ petitions filed by holders of *jenmom* rights over lands in the Malabar area of Kerala. The central legal question was whether owners of *jenmom* lands are also the proprietors of the minerals lying beneath the soil, or whether such minerals vest in the State Government. The Kerala High Court answered the question in the negative, holding that the minerals belong to the Government, rejecting the appellants' claim that *jenmom* rights extended to sub-soil mineral wealth. The Supreme Court (per Chelameswar, J.) was called upon to decide this significant question of ownership of mineral rights vis-à-vis traditional *jenmom* tenure in the former Malabar region.
## Key Holdings
- The Full Bench of the Kerala High Court was required to determine whether owners of jenmom lands in the Malabar area are proprietors of the soil and minerals underneath the soil.
- The Full Bench held that minerals beneath jenmom lands in the Malabar area belong to the Government and not to the holders of jenmom rights.
- The appellants claimed that the holder of jenmom rights is the proprietor not only of the soil but also of the mineral wealth lying beneath the soil.
- The appellants contended that the State has no legal authority to demand payment of royalties on minerals excavated by the holder of jenmom rights.
- The matter was referred to a three-Judge Bench of the Supreme Court for decision on the points of law arising from the Full Bench judgment.
