## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arises from a long-running dispute over the recovery of a loan advanced by Indian Bank to M/s. Sunrise Plaza, secured by an equitable mortgage of the partnership firm's properties. After the borrower defaulted, the Debt Recovery Tribunal passed an ex-parte decree, and the mortgaged property was sold in a public auction on 7 March 2003, with the appellants (C.N. Paramsivan & Anr.) emerging as successful bidders and ultimately receiving a sale certificate. ## Key Holdings
- An equitable mortgage created by deposit of title deeds is enforceable under the Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act.
- Failure of a borrower to appear and contest proceedings before the Debt Recovery Tribunal can result in an ex-parte decree being passed against them.
- An application seeking to set aside a court-auction sale must satisfy the maintainability requirements under the relevant procedural rules.
- Confirmation of an auction sale in recovery proceedings may be made conditional upon the judgment-debtor depositing a specified amount within a stipulated time-frame.
- Once a sale certificate is issued in favour of the successful auction purchaser, the title of the purchaser is perfected subject to the conditions prescribed by law.
