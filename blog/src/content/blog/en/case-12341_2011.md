# 12341_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 08.10.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The State of Orissa appealed to the Supreme Court against the Orissa High Court's decision refusing to interfere with an order of the Orissa Administrative Tribunal, which had ruled in favor of the respondent, Sri Jagabandhu Panda, an Ore Dressing Engineer appointed in 1984. The central legal question concerns whether the respondent was entitled to re-designation of his upgraded post as "OSD-cum-Deputy Director (Steel)" rather than being treated as an ex-cadre post of "OSD-cum-Deputy Director," which would lapse upon his retirement. The State contended that the post was always an ex-cadre position
## Key Holdings
- The post of Ore Dressing Engineer held by the respondent was an ex-cadre post throughout his service in the Directorate of Geology.
- There is no scope of promotion in an ex-cadre post, and such posts are not entitled to be re-designated as regular cadre posts at the instance of the incumbent.
- The upgraded post of OSD-cum-Deputy Director was expressly designated as ex-cadre and was to lapse on the retirement of the respondent.
- A government servant who has accepted the benefits of an upgraded ex-cadre post cannot subsequently claim re-designation of that post into a regular cadre post.
- Deployment of an ex-cadre employee to another department on certain terms and conditions does not alter the ex-cadre character of his original post.
