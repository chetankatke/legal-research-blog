# 1787_2004

## Summary
**Summary:**

This batch of civil appeals before the Supreme Court challenged a Madras High Court judgment that had affirmed the dismissal of writ petitions filed by private Arts and Science colleges in Tamil Nadu running MCA courses without AICTE approval. The core legal question was whether colleges affiliated to universities required separate AICTE approval for technical courses like MCA, even though the affiliating university itself was not required to obtain such permission under the AICTE Act, 1987. The appellants contended that the High Court misinterpreted the AICTE Act and disregarded the precedent laid down in *Bharathidasan University v. AICTE*. The Supreme Court was called upon to determine the correct interpretation of Section 10(k) of the AICTE Act and the applicability of the 1994/1997 Regulations to existing affiliated colleges offering approved technical programmes.
