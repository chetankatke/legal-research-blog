# 19539_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This appeal arose from a tender process for leasing parcel van space on a railway train, where the appellant was the highest bidder at Rs. 1,46,872/- per trip, but the railway administration cancelled the tender on technical and administrative grounds, including the omission of terms, conditions, and a penalty clause in the tender documents. The Single Judge of the Gauhati High Court allowed the appellant's writ petition holding that the cancellation was prejudicial since the appellant's bid rates had become public and that reasons for cancellation should have been communicated upfront, but the Division Bench reversed this and dismissed the writ petition. The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the highest bidder in a public tender acquires a vested right to the allotment of the contract, and whether the railway administration was justified in cancelling the tender despite reserving the power to do so. The Supreme Court was called upon to determine the correctness of the Division Bench's decision that set aside the Single Judge's direction to consider the appellant's bid subject to acceptance of the penalty clause.
## Key Holdings
- A bidder acquires no vested right to the allotment of a contract merely because its bid was found to be the highest.
- The power to cancel or withdraw a tender notice may be specifically reserved by the tendering authority in its own favour.
- Every public authority is required to act fairly and reasonably while granting contracts, including in the cancellation of a tender process.
- Reasons for cancellation of a tender process should be set out in the communication sent to the bidder at the time of cancellation rather than being disclosed subsequently in a counter affidavit.
- Disclosure of a bidder's quoted rates during a discharged tender process can cause prejudice to the bidder, justifying judicial scrutiny of the cancellation.
