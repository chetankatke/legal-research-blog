# 10659_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** September 2, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this appeal arising from a terminated LPG dealership, the Supreme Court addressed the dispute between Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd. and M/s. Siba Gas Agency, where the respondent had taken on a partner without prior permission leading to termination. The key legal question was whether the respondent should be permitted to pursue a writ petition in the High Court (filed nearly two years after termination) when the dealership agreement contained an arbitration clause for dispute resolution. The Court allowed the appeal and set aside the High Court's order, holding that the parties must be relegated to the agreed remedy of arbitration by approaching the Managing Director of HPCL. The Court emphasized that arbitration was the better course for resolving disputes between the parties under the terms of their agreement.
## Key Holdings
- Parties to a dispute governed by an arbitration clause must be relegated to the agreed arbitral remedy rather than pursuing writ jurisdiction.
- The High Court's order was set aside and the parties were directed to resolve their disputes through arbitration in accordance with the agreement.
- Respondent No. 2 was permitted to approach the Managing Director of the appellant Corporation to invoke the arbitration clause.
- The existence of a contractual arbitration clause providing for reference to the Managing Director or his nominee as sole arbitrator is valid and enforceable.
