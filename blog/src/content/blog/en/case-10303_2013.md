# 10303_2013

- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:**

## Summary
This case involves multiple civil and criminal appeals arising from a joint venture dispute between Chandran Ratnaswami (representing ORE Holdings, a Fairfax Group company) and K.C. Palanisamy (representing CG Holdings) concerning the construction of a hotel, shopping complex, and IT Park through Cherraan Enterprises Private Limited (CEPL), where ORE invested Rs. 75
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court granted leave to appeal in all the connected civil and criminal matters arising from the same factual matrix.
- Common questions of law warranted hearing the multiple appeals and contempt petition together and disposing them by a common judgment.
- The factual background established a joint venture dispute involving ORE Holdings, CG Holdings, and N. Athappan for development of properties of Cherraan Properties Limited and Vasantha Mills Limited through Cheran Enterprises Private Limited.
- Prior proceedings before the Company Law Board had already adjudicated issues of alleged oppression and mismanagement by respondent No. 1, leading to the present appeals.
