## Summary
This appeal by the State of Maharashtra challenged the Bombay High Court's judgment acquitting the respondent of charges under Sections 363, 364A, 386, 302, and 201 IPC in a kidnapping-for-ransom and murder case involving a young child, Sanket, who was abducted from his school in November 1999. The trial court had convicted the respondent on all charges, but the High Court reversed the conviction and acquitted him. The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the High Court properly interfered with the trial court's conviction based on its assessment of the evidence. The judgment excerpt indicates the Supreme Court was examining the circumstances of the kidnapping, ransom demands, and the eventual fate of the child to determine the correctness of the acquittal.
