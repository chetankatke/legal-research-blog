# 18761_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** August 8, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed an appeal seeking enhancement of compensation for land acquired by the State of Punjab, noting that the matter had been directed to be tagged with similar special leave petitions involving comparable acquisition disputes. The Court observed that the tagged cases had already been decided in *Ashrafi and Ors. v. State of Haryana and Ors.*, (2013) 5 SCC 527, where compensation was enhanced to Rs. 7,25,000/- per acre. Applying the principle of consistency in compensation for similarly situated land owners, the Court allowed the appeal and modified the Land Acquisition Officer's award, entitling the appellants to enhanced compensation at the uniform rate of Rs. 7,25,000/- per acre.
## Key Holdings
- The application for substitution of the deceased appellant was allowed and leave to appeal was granted.
- The court followed its earlier judgment in Ashrafi v. State of Haryana (2013) 5 SCC 527, which fixed the enhanced compensation at Rs. 7,25,000 per acre.
- The award of the Land Acquisition Officer was modified to grant compensation at the enhanced rate of Rs. 7,25,000 per acre, applicable at par to the appellants.
- The appeal was disposed of with the direction that the appellants would be entitled to the same compensation rate as granted in the tagged Ashrafi case.
