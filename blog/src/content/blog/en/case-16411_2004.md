# Procedure and after referring to decisions in Jai Jai Ram Monohar Lal v. National Building Material Supply, Gurgaon

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 11.8.1993
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court of India addressed whether a defendant could introduce a counter-claim by way of amendment to a written statement after the limitation period had expired. The plaintiff agents had sued Peerless General Finance for unpaid commissions, and the defendant sought to amend its written statement in 1998 (nearly five years after filing) to assert a monetary claim of Rs. 4,19,509.43 against the plaintiffs. Relying on Order VI Rule 17, Order VIII Rule 6 and Rule 6-A of the CPC, and precedents including *Jai Jai Ram Monohar Lal v. National Building Material Supply* and *Suraj Prakash Bhasin v. Raj Rani Bhasin*, the Court held that no counter-claim could be entertained when the time limitation had long expired and the claims were not identical in nature. The
## Key Holdings
- Delay in filing the application for substitution of appellant No. 2 was condoned and substitution was allowed.
- A counter-claim cannot be entertained by way of amendment of written statement when the limitation period has long expired.
- The defendants cannot introduce a counter-claim or set-off through amendment of written statement if the cause of action is not identical to that of the plaintiff's claim.
- Where claims are not identical in nature and time has expired, a party cannot seek adjustment of any claim based on a different cause of action.
- Amendments to written statements under Order VIII Rule 6 CPC are not permissible as a device to circumvent limitation by introducing fresh claims.
