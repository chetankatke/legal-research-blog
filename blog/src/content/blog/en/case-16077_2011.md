# Placing reliance on its earlier decision on Nitya Hari Kundu and others vs. State of W.B. and others

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 02.09.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed whether a Sub-Registrar under the Registration Act, 1908, is bound by the stamp duty assessment made by the court based on suit valuation when registering a partition decree. The dispute arose when the Sub-Registrar of Siliguri objected to registering a compromise decree in a partition suit (valued at Rs. 50 lakhs), arguing there was no proper valuation for registration purposes, but the Civil Judge and the High Court (relying on *Nitya Hari Kundu v. State of W.B.*) held that the Registrar could not reassess the value once fixed by the court. The Court began its analysis by referring to the definition of "market value" under Section 2(16B) of the Indian Stamp Act, 1899
