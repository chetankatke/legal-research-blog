## Summary
In this criminal appeal, the Supreme Court considered whether the High Court of Himachal Pradesh was justified in acquitting the respondents (accused 1 to 5) of charges related to the death of Jeet Singh, who died during a pilgrimage to Shah Talai. The prosecution alleged that the accused, being annoyed with the food prepared by the deceased, beat him after tying his hands with a cloth, and his body was subsequently found in a rivulet called "Saryali Khud" with contusion injuries noted in the post-mortem report. The appellant Swarn Kaur, as the de facto complainant, challenged the High Court's acquittal judgment dated 18.06.2008 in Crl.A.No.280 of 2005.
