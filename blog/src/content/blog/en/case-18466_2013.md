## Summary
In *Chatterjee Petrochem Co. v. Haldia Petrochemicals Ltd.*, the Supreme Court addressed disputes arising from a 2002 restructuring agreement between CPMC, the Government of West Bengal, WBIDC, and HPL, which contained an arbitration clause referring all disputes to the ICC, Paris. The appellant sought arbitration over share allotment issues, while the respondent challenged the arbitration agreement as void, unenforceable, and inoperative, with related proceedings involving the Company Law Board and Calcutta High Court concerning the transfer of 155 million shares to IOC. The High Court had set aside the CLB's order directing share transfer, and the Supreme Court's earlier judgment (September 2011) held that the transfer of shares to IOC altered the private character of the company and did not constitute oppression. The case concerned the enforceability of the arbitration clause in light of these intertwined contractual, corporate, and statutory disputes over shareholding
