## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal concerned the saleability of a flat mortgaged by Respondent No. 5 (an associate company of the principal borrower) to Union Bank of India as security for the loan advanced to Respondent No. 2. The Appellant, Hill Properties Ltd., contended that Respondent No. 5 was merely a shareholder holding a single "A" equity share and, under the Articles of Association, only had a right to occupy the flat—without authority to mortgage it to the Bank. ## Key Holdings
- A shareholder's right to occupy a flat owned by the company under its Articles of Association constitutes a valuable, transferable right that can be mortgaged to secure repayment of debts.
- A flat mortgaged by an associate company to secure the dues of its principal can be validly attached and sold in execution proceedings before the Debt Recovery Tribunal.
- The mere fact that a shareholder holds only a single equity share in the company does not divest the shareholder of the right to mortgage the beneficial interest in the company-owned flat.
- The right of a shareholder to use and occupy a company-owned flat is a proprietary right capable of being attached and sold in satisfaction of the company's secured debt obligations.
- A company seeking release of an attached flat was granted liberty to purchase the flat at a price determined by the Valuer or the Auditor of the Company, whichever was higher.
