# 24703_2012

- **Date:** 23.4.2012

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a case where the trial court (Gram Nyayalaya) accepted the accused-respondents' guilty plea for offences under Sections 323 and 343 IPC, convicted them with a nominal fine of Rs. 500, and granted them the benefit of probation under the Probation of Offenders Act
## Key Holdings
- The trial court erred in entertaining the guilty plea and concluding the trial on the same day without issuing notice to the complainant/victim, as the complainant was a necessary party to the proceedings.
- Before granting the benefit of the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, the court must give notice to the informant/victim and consider the nature and gravity of the offence.
- A mechanical acceptance of a guilty plea without proper application of mind to the seriousness of the alleged offence of kidnapping renders the conviction and order unsustainable.
- The High Court ought to have interfered under Section 482 CrPC where the trial court disposed of the case without affording an opportunity of hearing to the victim of the offence.
- Allowing the accused to plead guilty to lesser offences under Sections 323 and 343 IPC while the FIR was lodged under Section 365 IPC raises serious questions about the fairness of the proceedings.
