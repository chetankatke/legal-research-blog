# 1866_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether a person whose nomination was rejected on grounds of disqualification qualifies as a "candidate" under Section 79(b) of the Representation of the People Act, 1951, such that their non-joinder as a respondent under Section 82(b) would invalidate an election petition alleging corrupt practices against them. Relying on the definition in Section 79(b), the Court held that Jaswant Singh, whose nomination was rejected due to disqualification, could not be regarded as a "candidate" since he neither was "duly nominated" nor could he "claim to have been duly nominated" when he was not qualified to contest the election. Consequently, the Court upheld the High Court's ruling that his non-joinder as a respondent did not render the election petition liable to be rejected. The appeal was accordingly dismissed.
