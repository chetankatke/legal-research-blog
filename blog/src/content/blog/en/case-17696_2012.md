# 17696_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** August 26, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court considered whether the fine of Rs.60,000 imposed by the High Court on Prem Chand for his conviction under Section 324 IPC (after the High Court overturned his Trial Court acquittal) was excessive. The appellant, represented as a poor laborer with a Gram Panchayat certificate supporting his financial condition, sought reduction of the fine. The Supreme Court reduced the fine to Rs.10,000 and directed that the excess amount already paid by the Gram Panchayat on his behalf be refunded, allowing the appeal to this limited extent.
## Key Holdings
- The only question before the Court was whether the fine of Rs. 60,000 imposed on the appellant was steep.
- The conviction under Section 324 IPC was retained by the High Court after reversing the Trial Court's acquittal, but the fine of Rs. 60,000 was reduced to Rs. 10,000 considering the appellant was a poor labourer.
- A certificate from the Gram Panchayat certifying the appellant's status as a poor labourer was considered sufficient ground to reduce the fine to the minimum.
- Since the Gram Panchayat had already paid the steep fine of Rs. 60,000 on behalf of the appellant, the excess amount over and above Rs. 10,000 was ordered to be refunded to the appellant/Gram Panchayat.
- The appeal was allowed only to the extent of the reduction and refund of the excess fine amount.
