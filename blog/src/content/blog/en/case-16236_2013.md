## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Sambhavana v. University of Delhi* (Civil Appeal No. 4722-4723 of 2013), the Supreme Court addressed a writ petition seeking inclusive accommodations for students with vision impairment in Delhi University's new four-year undergraduate programme, including bridge courses, accessible reading materials, teacher training, and representation on university bodies. The University had already constituted a 14-member Empowered Committee to examine the special needs of disabled students and suggest modifications to curricula, reading materials, and assessment methods, with a deadline of 15th June 2013 to submit its interim report. The appellant expressed apprehension that the committee's report would not be finalized before the admission process commenced and sought preponement of the deadline. The Supreme Court granted leave and considered the matter, ultimately finding no reason to disturb the High Court's decision that the committee's ongoing deliberations adequately addressed the concerns raised.
## Key Holdings
- Leave granted to the appellant-organisation to appeal against the High Court's order concerning disability accommodations in the four-year undergraduate programme.
- The respondent-University constituted an Empowered Committee of fourteen academicians to examine the special needs of students with disabilities and recommend modifications to curricula, instruction methods, and assessment.
- The appellant-organisation was permitted to make suggestions to the Empowered Committee before it submitted its report to the Vice-Chancellor regarding disability-related accommodations.
- The Court considered the appellant's apprehension that the Empowered Committee's deadline of 15th June 2013 should be preponed so that recommendations could be implemented before the admission process commenced.
- The substantive issues involved the introduction of bridge courses, foundation courses, accessible reading materials, teacher training, and representation of persons with disabilities on academic bodies of Delhi University.
