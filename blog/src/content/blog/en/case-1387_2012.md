# 1387_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 21.12.2011
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In this appeal arising from Uttarakhand, the Supreme Court addressed the legal question of whether the dissolution of a Municipal Council upon its upgradation to a Municipal Corporation under Article 243Q of the Constitution required prior notice and an opportunity of hearing to the elected body. The Division Bench of the High Court had held that Section 8-AA of the Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Act, 1959 (as applicable in Uttarakhand) does not contemplate automatic dissolution of a Municipal Council merely upon upgradation to a Municipal Corporation. While the full text of the Supreme Court's reasoning is not visible in the excerpt, the appeal was directed against the High Court's treatment of the issue concerning the procedural safeguards owed to the elected Municipal Council before dissolution.
## Key Holdings
- Dissolution of a Municipal Council under Section 8-AA of the Act requires compliance with the principles of natural justice, including an opportunity of hearing, before issuing the dissolution notification.
- Section 8-AA of the Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Act, 1959 does not contemplate automatic dissolution of a Municipal Council merely upon its upgradation to a Municipal Corporation under Article 243Q of the Constitution.
- A show cause notice and opportunity of hearing must be afforded to the elected representatives of a Municipality before the State Government can dissolve it and appoint an Administrator.
- The upgradation of a Municipal Council to a Municipal Corporation is a distinct statutory exercise from the dissolution of the Council and does not, by itself, justify supersession of the elected body without due process.
