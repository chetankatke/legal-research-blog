## Summary
**Summary:**

These consolidated appeals before the Supreme Court arose from disputes regarding promotions in Rushikulya Gramya Bank, a Regional Rural Bank, with six appeals concerning promotion from Junior Management Scale-I to Middle Management Scale-II, and two relating to promotion from Clerk to Junior Management Scale-I. The central legal question was whether the management of the Bank was entitled to prescribe an additional benchmark over and above the criteria already fixed by the promotion rules for granting promotion on a seniority-cum-merit basis. ## Key Holdings
- The key question is whether the management of a Regional Rural Bank can lay down a benchmark beyond the criteria fixed by the rules for grant of promotion on seniority-cum-merit basis.
- Promotion from Junior Management Scale-I to Middle Management Scale-II must be made strictly on the basis of seniority-cum-merit as prescribed by the applicable rules.
- The selection process for promotion is governed by the division of marks for written test, interview, and performance appraisal reports for the preceding five years.
- For promotion to Middle Management Scale-II, the zone of consideration is restricted to four times the number of vacancies, whereas for Junior Management Scale-I, all eligible candidates are permitted to take the exam.
- The Bank's promotion circular must conform to the promotion rules of the Government of India and cannot impose additional or extraneous benchmarks not sanctioned by the rules.
