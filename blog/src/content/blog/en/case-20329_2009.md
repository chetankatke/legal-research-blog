# 20329_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
In this case, the Supreme Court addressed whether a Bank of Baroda employee, who was removed from service as a disciplinary punishment but with entitlement to superannuation benefits, could claim pension and leave encashment. The bank had denied these benefits on the ground that employees dismissed/removed from service forfeit accumulated leave and pension, relying on its own circular. The Industrial Tribunal and the High Court both ruled in favor of the employee, holding that the order of punishment itself entitled him to superannuation benefits, and that denying pension and leave encashment was neither legal nor justified. The Supreme Court upheld this view, affirming the employee's entitlement to all termination benefits including pension, leave encashment, gratuity, and commutation of pension.
## Key Holdings
- An employee punished with removal from service coupled with superannuation benefits is entitled to all superannuation benefits including pension, leave encashment, gratuity, and commutation of pension.
- The original disciplinary order granting superannuation benefits cannot be nullified by subsequent orders passed by other officers of the employer denying such benefits.
- A bank cannot deny pension and leave encashment to an employee removed from service where the punishment order itself stipulated entitlement to superannuation benefits.
- The Industrial Tribunal was justified in setting aside the employer's action denying superannuation benefits to a workman whose punishment order expressly granted such benefits.
- The compassionate allowance provision does not apply to an employee whose removal order specifically awarded superannuation benefits as part of the punishment.
