## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal by M/s. Integrated Finance Co. Ltd. (an NBFC) arises from a Division Bench of the Madras High Court setting aside a Single Judge's order that had sanctioned a scheme of arrangement/compromise between the company and its deposit/bond holders under Section 391 of the Companies Act, 1956. The core legal question concerns the maintainability of such a scheme when the RBI's inspection in 2005 revealed that the company had a negative Net Owned Fund of Rs. 10,666.06 lakh, along
## Key Holdings
- A scheme of arrangement/compromise under Section 391 of the Companies Act, 1956 requires sanction of the court, which may be granted with conditions.
- The Reserve Bank of India has statutory authority under Section 45N of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 to inspect the books of accounts of Non-Banking Finance Companies.
- A Non-Banking Finance Company whose Net Owned Fund is negative violates the provisions of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 and is subject to regulatory action.
- A Division Bench of the High Court has the power to set aside an order of a Single Judge sanctioning a scheme of arrangement under Section 391 of the Companies Act.
- Non-Banking Finance Companies that fail to comply with RBI regulations may be directed to stop accepting deposits from investors and to repay existing deposits immediately.
