# 18508_2005

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 29.4.2005
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court considered whether negotiations between landowners and the beneficiaries of a land reservation in a Development Plan under the Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966 could constitute steps toward acquisition of land under Section 127 of the Act. The land in question was reserved for "timber industries" in the revised Development Plan of Pune City, and although the Municipal Corporation's Standing Committee resolved to approve acquisition and wrote to the Collector, no actual acquisition notification was ever issued. The Bombay High Court had declared the reservation lapsed due to non-acquisition within the statutory period, and these appeals challenged that order. The core issue was whether informal negotiations and agreements between the parties could substitute for the formal acquisition steps required by law, thereby saving the reservation from lapsing.
## Key Holdings
- Negotiations and agreements between landowners and perceived beneficiaries of a land reservation cannot be treated as steps towards acquisition of land under Section 127 of the Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966.
- The reservation of land for timber industries in the Development Plan was deemed to have lapsed because the land was not acquired within six months of the receipt of the purchase notice.
- A resolution by the Standing Committee of the Municipal Corporation approving acquisition and a letter to the Collector, without issuance of the required notification under Section 126(2), does not constitute valid steps towards acquisition.
- The fact that a tripartite agreement involving the Pune Municipal Corporation was contemplated but never executed demonstrates that the acquisition process under the Act was never lawfully commenced.
- The subsequent cancellation of the sale agreements between the landowners and the Timber Merchants Association did not revive the lapsed reservation under the Development Plan.
