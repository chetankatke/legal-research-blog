# 12505_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** February 26, 2006
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court allowed the State of Uttarakhand's appeal challenging a Uttarakhand High Court order that struck down, as violative of Article 16 of the Constitution, a condition in a 2006 Public Service Commission advertisement requiring candidates for Veterinary Doctor posts to be registered with the Uttaranchal Veterinary Council. The Court held that the High Court had disposed of the serious constitutional issue without adequate deliberation or discussion, and accordingly set aside its order. The matter was remanded to the High Court for a fresh hearing, with directions to consider both the constitutional provisions and the relevant statutory provisions before passing appropriate orders. The Court clarified that, in the interim, the State Public Service Commission was free to revert to the original terms of the advertisement.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's brief order on the serious constitutional issue regarding state-specific veterinary registration requirement, finding it lacked adequate deliberation.
- The matter was remanded to the High Court to reconsider the issue with reference to both the constitutional provisions and the relevant statutory provisions governing veterinary registration.
- The condition in a recruitment advertisement requiring registration with the State Veterinary Council was held to require detailed examination under Article 16 of the Constitution rather than being dismissed summarily.
- Pending fresh consideration by the High Court, the State Public Service Commission was held free to adhere to its original terms requiring Uttarakhand-specific veterinary registration.
- The appeal was allowed with no order as to costs, leaving the constitutional validity of the registration condition open for fresh adjudication.
