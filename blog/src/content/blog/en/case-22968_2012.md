# 22968_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 15.09.2011
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arose from a claim petition filed by the mother of a deceased person against the Railway Authorities, alleging that her son's death was caused by their negligence. The Railway Claims Tribunal awarded Rs. 4 lacs in compensation after considering the evidence on record, but the High Court of Andhra Pradesh set aside this award, prompting the mother to appeal to the Supreme Court. The Supreme Court found that the High Court was not justified in disturbing the Tribunal's well-reasoned order and accordingly allowed the appeal, setting aside the High Court's judgment and restoring the Tribunal's compensation award. The Court clarified, however, that this order shall not be treated as a precedent in any other case.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court allowed the appeal, set aside the High Court's order, and restored the Railway Claims Tribunal's award of Rs. 4 lacs compensation to the mother of the deceased.
