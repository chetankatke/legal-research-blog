# 21939_2011

## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from a challenge to BSNL's decision to blacklist M/s Kulja Industries Limited for all future contract allotments, following allegations that the company had fraudulently received excess payments of approximately Rs. 7.98 crores through a conspiracy involving BSNL officers who manipulated duplicate/triplicate bill copies to generate unauthorized payments. The key legal question framed was whether BSNL could permanently blacklist a contractor for future tenders based on alleged fraudulent conduct. The Bombay High Court had upheld the blacklisting order, and the appeal before the Supreme Court (by Special Leave Petition) sought to challenge that determination. The case engaged the broader legal principle concerning the permissible scope, duration, and proportionality of blacklisting orders
