## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India heard appeals by the State of Gujarat challenging a Division Bench judgment of the Gujarat High Court that directed regularization of daily wage workers (respondents) employed in various State departments such as PWD, Water Resources, Forest, and Agriculture. The legal question centered on whether the State Government was bound to regularize the services of long-serving daily wagers following a 1988 Government Resolution that accepted a Committee's recommendations prescribing wage and benefit structures based on the workers' years of service. The Court examined the scope of the State's obligations under the said Resolution, particularly in light of the protections envisaged under Section 25B of the Industrial Disputes Act for workers with specified tenure. While the full operative holding is not contained in the excerpt, the Court addressed the State's challenge to the High Court's direction to extend regularization benefits to the respondent-work
## Key Holdings
- The State Government constituted a Committee under the Chairmanship of the Minister of Road and Building Department to study the regularization of daily wage workers and made recommendations in their favour.
- The State Government accepted all recommendations of the Committee and resolved to provide graded wages and benefits to daily wagers and semi-skilled workers based on their years of service.
- Daily wagers with less than five years of service as on 1.10.1988 were to be paid daily wages as per prevailing Daily Wages Rules, with eligibility for paid Sunday, medical allowance, and national festival holidays upon 240 days of presence.
- Workers with more than five but less than ten years of service were entitled to a fixed monthly salary of Rs.750/- plus dearness allowance, along with optional leave, medical allowance, and provident fund deductions, in accordance with Section 25B of the Industrial Disputes Act.
- The High Court affirmed the Single Judge's order directing the State Government and its officials to regularize the services of the respondent-workmen, which was challenged by the State in this appeal.
