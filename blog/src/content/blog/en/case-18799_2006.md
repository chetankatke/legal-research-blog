# Centre for Enquiry into Health and Allied Themes v. Union of India

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed the persistent decline in India's female child sex ratio caused by sex-selective abortion, examining the implementation of the Pre-Conception and Pre-Natal Diagnostic Techniques (Prohibition on Sex-Selection) Act, 1994, which was enacted under Article 15(2) of the Constitution to combat this practice. Despite prior directions issued in the *Centre for Enquiry into Health and Allied Themes* cases (2001 and 2003), the Court found that various States and Union Territories had failed to ensure proper implementation of the Act, with the functioning of Central and State Supervisory Boards, Appropriate Authorities, and Advisory Committees remaining unsatisfactory.
