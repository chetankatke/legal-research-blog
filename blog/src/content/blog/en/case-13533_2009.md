## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court arose from a suit for recovery of Rs. 10,45,620/- filed by M/s. Gian Chand & Brothers, a partnership firm and commission agent, against the respondent who had taken advances over time in connection with agricultural produce transactions. The core legal question concerned whether the defendant was liable to repay the outstanding amount of Rs. 5,80,620/- (as on 30.4.2002) that he had acknowledged by signing the corresponding entry in the plaintiffs' account books (*rokar bahi*). ## Key Holdings
- Leave was granted to appeal the judgment and decree dated 26.2.2009 passed by the High Court of Punjab and Haryana at Chandigarh.
- The civil suit for recovery of Rs.10,45,620/- was initiated by a registered partnership firm (commission agent) against the defendant for outstanding balance acknowledged in the account books.
- The defendant's acknowledgment under his signature in the corresponding account entry as on 30.4.2002 for Rs.5,80,000/- constituted a documented acknowledgement of the outstanding balance.
- The transactions between the parties were duly recorded in the books of accounts (rokar bahi) maintained in the regular course of business, reflecting borrowings for financial years 2002-2003 and 2003-2004.
- The plaintiffs served a legal notice on 26.2.2004 and made repeated demands for payment before instituting the suit on 19.8.2004, having been refused compliance on 18.8.2004.
