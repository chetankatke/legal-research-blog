# 24293_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
In this civil appeal, the Supreme Court addressed the eviction of long-standing tenants who had occupied the premises for approximately five decades. While affirming the landlord's right to eviction by restoring the orders of the Rent Controller (upheld by the First Appellate Authority) and setting aside the High Court's contrary orders, the Court granted the tenants a five-year period to vacate and deliver peaceful possession. As conditions, the tenants were required to file an undertaking within four weeks stipulating no default in rent payment and no subletting, with the landlord permitted to seek police assistance for possession in case of any default.
## Key Holdings
- The Court granted five years' time to the long-standing tenants to vacate and deliver possession, considering their five-decade occupation of the premises.
- Tenants were required to file an undertaking committing to regular payment of rentals and refraining from subletting the premises to any third party.
- On any default by the tenants, the landlord was granted liberty to seek police assistance to obtain possession of the premises.
- The High Court's orders were set aside and the Rent Controller's order, as affirmed by the First Appellate Authority, was restored.
