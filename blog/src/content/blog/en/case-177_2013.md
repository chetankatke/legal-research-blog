# 177_2013

## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court considered whether the disciplinary proceedings against the appellant—a government officer dismissed for misconduct related to the irregular allotment of bitumen to M/s. Cosmo Transport—were conducted in accordance with principles of natural justice and whether the punishment of dismissal was proportionate. The legal question centered on whether the High Court's Division Bench was correct in reversing the Single Judge's order, which had set aside the dismissal on the grounds that documents were not supplied, witnesses were not examined, and the penalty was disproportionate to the charges proved. The Court held that the enquiry was not vitiated by procedural illegality, as the appellant had failed to specifically identify which documents were denied or which witnesses were not examined, and the doctrine of *actus reus* did not apply in disciplinary proceedings. The Court ultimately restored the Division Bench's judgment, upholding the order of dismissal.
