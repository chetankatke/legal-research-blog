## Summary
**Summary:**

The appellant, a member of a cooperative housing society in Mumbai, approached the High Court seeking a writ of mandamus directing the Municipal Corporation to repair a collapsed portion of his flat's roof. The High Court declined to issue the mandamus but granted liberty to the appellant to pursue execution of an existing order from the Maharashtra State Cooperative Appellate Court. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court rightly declined to issue a mandamus to the Municipal Corporation for repairs when the appellant had an alternative remedy of executing the order passed by the Cooperative Appellate Court.
- The dispute regarding a member's liability to contribute towards major repair expenses of a cooperative housing society falls within the jurisdiction of the Cooperative Court under the Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
- Liberty was granted to the appellant to secure execution of the order of the Maharashtra State Cooperative Appellate Court for recovery of the damages to his flat.
- The notice issued under Section 354 of the Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 directing the society to carry out repairs within two months was a valid exercise of statutory power.
