## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard an appeal challenging the prosecution of a Municipal Councillor for offences under Sections 7, 13(1)(d) read with Section 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988. The central legal question was whether a Municipal Councillor qualifies as a "public servant" within the meaning of the Act, so as to be amenable to prosecution thereunder. The appellant had been caught red-handed accepting Rs. 5,000/- as part of a larger Rs. 50,000 bribe demanded for the allotment of a kiosk, after which he sought to have the proceedings dropped on the ground that, being merely an elected Councillor, he was not a "public servant." Both the trial court and the Rajasthan High Court rejected this contention, and the matter came before the Supreme Court for determination of this threshold jurisdictional question.
