# 11435_2013

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:** The Supreme Court addressed whether Pt. Ravishankar Shukla University was obligated to issue a fresh mark sheet to M.Com students who took a subsequent examination to improve their division, even when their division remained unchanged. The High Court of Chhattisgarh had answered this question in the affirmative, but the Supreme Court (per Justice Madan B. Lokur) reversed, holding that Ordinance 24 of the University does not impose any such obligation to issue a new mark sheet when a student fails to improve their division. The Court interpreted the Ordinance as a composite scheme that only entitles a student to an improved division upon actually achieving one, not merely upon appearing for the subsequent examination.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court disagreed with the High Court and held that Ordinance No. 24 does not obligate the University to issue a fresh mark sheet to a student whose division does not change upon taking a subsequent improvement examination.
- Ordinance No. 24 constitutes a composite scheme for obtaining the M.Com degree and permits a repeat examination solely for the purpose of improving the division already obtained.
- A candidate's division is determined on the basis of the aggregate of marks obtained in both the M.Com Previous and M.Com Final examinations, with no division assigned on the result of the previous examination alone.
- Only candidates who have passed the M.Com examination in Third or Second Division are eligible to appear in a subsequent examination for improving their division, without attending a regular course of study.
