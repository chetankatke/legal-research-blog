## Summary
This appeal arises from a Customs, Excise & Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal order upholding the levy of customs duty on the import of furnace oil by M/s. Uniworth Textiles Ltd., a 100% Export Oriented Unit (EOU), along with a penalty under Section 112 of the Customs Act, 1962. The central legal question concerns whether the appellant was entitled to the customs duty exemption available under Notification No. 53/97-Cus. for goods imported by 100% EOUs, and whether the demand for duty and penalty was barred by limitation. The appellant, which had a private bonded warehouse license and purchased electricity from its sister EOU unit that generated power from a captive plant using imported furnace oil, contested the duty levy on limitation grounds. The Tribunal had rejected this plea, and the matter was before the Supreme Court in appeal under Section 130-E of the Customs Act.
