## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a demand of Rs. 94,27,334/- issued by the Assistant Provident Fund Commissioner under Section 7Q of the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, against M/s. Arcot Textile Mills Ltd. for interest on belated remittances of provident fund contributions. The central legal question is whether the High Court was justified in directing the appellant to approach the Employees' Provident Funds Appellate Tribunal (alternative remedy) instead of entertaining the writ petition under Article 226 of the Constitution. The appellant, a sick industrial company declared so by BIFR in 1999, contended that the alternative remedy was not efficacious given its financial condition and the peculiar facts of the case. ## Key Holdings
- An order passed by the Assistant Provident Fund Commissioner under Section 7Q of the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, demanding interest on belated remittances, is appealable before the Employees' Provident Funds Appellate Tribunal.
- When a statutory alternative remedy of appeal is available before the Tribunal, the High Court should not be approached under Article 226 of the Constitution.
- The Division Bench rightly concurred with the single Judge that the appellant ought to have availed the alternative remedy before the Tribunal instead of invoking writ jurisdiction.
- A company declared as a sick industrial company under the Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, may raise the issue of its financial inability before the appropriate authority while contesting demands under the EPF Act.
- The pendency of rehabilitation proceedings before BIFR does not automatically absolve an employer from remitting contributions or interest under Section 7Q of the Act.
