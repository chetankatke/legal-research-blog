# 1935_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** January 22, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether the High Court's dismissal of a writ petition could be sustained where it was disposed of without substantive reasoning. The Court held that a non-speaking, perfunctory order that fails to consider either the factual or legal controversy cannot be sustained in law. Accordingly, the impugned order was set aside and the matter was remanded to the High Court for fresh consideration on merits, with permission granted to the appellant to file a rejoinder affidavit and a direction to expedite the hearing.
## Key Holdings
- A non-speaking order disposing of a writ petition is legally unsustainable and must be set aside.
- Courts must adjudicate both the factual and legal controversies involved before disposing of a writ petition, and cannot do so in a perfunctory manner.
- Where a party was not afforded the opportunity to file a rejoinder affidavit, that party must be permitted to do so before the matter is finally disposed of.
- An appellate court may remand a writ petition to the High Court for fresh consideration on merits when the original order suffers from non-application of mind.
- Hearing and final disposal of a remanded writ petition should be expedited by the High Court.

                     :VERSUS:
STATE OF MAHARASHTRA & ORS.               Respondent(s)
O R D E R
Leave granted.
We  have  heard  the  learned  counsel  for  the 
parties.
A  perusal  of  the  order  passed  by  the  High 
Court clearly shows that it is a non-speaking order. 
The  writ  petition  has  been  disposed  of  in  a 
perfunctory  manner,  without  considering  either  the 
factual  or  the  legal  controversy  involved.  In  our 
opinion, the impugned order cannot be sustained in 
law. On this short ground alone, the impugned order 
passed by the High Court is set aside and the matter 
is remanded back to the High Court for consideration 
of the writ petition on merits.

JUDGMENT
-2-
It is brought to our notice by the learned 
counsel for the appellant that he did not have the 
opportunity to file rejoinder affidavit before the 
High  Court.  If  that  be  so,  the  appellant  may  be 
permitted  to  file  rejoinder  affidavit  before  the 
High Court and thereafter the writ petition be set 
down  for  final  disposal.  Hearing  of  the  writ 
petition also be expedited. 
The  appeal  is  disposed  of  with  the 
aforementioned observations and directions.
