# 15706_2011

## Citation

## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal arises from a Section 138 Negotiable Instruments Act case where the respondent-complainant alleged the appellant borrowed Rs. 1,50,000 and issued a cheque that was dishonored for "insufficient funds." The Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM) of Pathanamthitta acquitted the appellant, finding that the complainant gave prevaricating statements about the cheque's issuance, was unaware of the borrowing date, and that there was material alteration in the instrument, thereby failing to establish the offense. The Kerala High Court reversed the acquittal, convicted the appellant, and imposed a fine of Rs. 1,50,000 as compensation, prompting the appeal to the Supreme Court. The legal question centers on whether the High Court was justified in overturning the trial court's acquittal when the complainant's own testimony raised doubts about the cheque's issuance and the instrument's integrity, with the Supreme Court ultimately examining the correctness of this reversal.
