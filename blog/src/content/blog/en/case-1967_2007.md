## Summary
**Summary:**

These appeals before the Supreme Court of India raise the question whether retired employees of the Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation are entitled to pensionary benefits under the 1989 Pension Regulations despite non-compliance with the stipulated conditions. The employees had retired between January and March 1992 and were paid their Contributory Provident Fund (CPF), including the employer's share, before the Pension Regulations came into force on 11 January 1993. Although they exercised the option to join the pension scheme, they failed to deposit the employer's share of CPF with interest in lump sum within the prescribed time, which was an essential precondition under Clause 3(1) of the Regulations. Consequently, the Corporation rejected their pension claims, and the matter was challenged before the High Court, with the present appeals arising from those proceedings. ## Key Holdings
- Under the RSRTC Pension Regulations, an employee who exercises or is deemed to have exercised the option for pensionary benefits must deposit the employer's share of CPF with accrued interest in lumpsum within the stipulated time as a condition precedent to claiming pension.
- The option once exercised or deemed to have been exercised is final and no representation seeking revision of such option can be entertained.
- An existing employee who fails to exercise the option within the prescribed 90 days from publication of the Regulations shall be deemed to have opted for the Pension and CPF Regulations.
- Non-deposit of the employer's share of CPF with interest in lumpsum within the specified time is fatal to the claim of pensionary benefits under the Regulations.
- The departmental officer bears personal responsibility to ensure that the option reaches the office of Dy. G.M. (P&F) RSRTC, Jaipur in a timely manner.
