## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arises from proceedings under Section 32G of the Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, concerning a tenant's claim for deemed purchase of land (R.S. No. 51, Village Pandewadi, Kolhapur). The central legal question was whether the landlord had established that the land was leased specifically for growing sugarcane, which would determine the tenant's right to deemed purchase. ## Key Holdings
- The Maharashtra Revenue Tribunal held that continuous entries in the 'E' Patrak showing sugarcane cultivation since 1946, corroborated by independent witnesses, established that the main purpose of the lease was for growing sugarcane.
- An agreement of lease for sugarcane cultivation is established where crop records and witness testimony demonstrate continuous sugarcane growing over a substantial period.
- The landlord bears the burden of proving the specific purpose of the lease, and failure to do so on the basis of credible evidence will defeat the claim of such a lease.
- The High Court set aside the Tribunal's order on the ground that the Tribunal erred in disturbing the factual finding of the Sub-Divisional Officer that the land was not leased for sugarcane cultivation.
- A finding of fact recorded by a subordinate authority regarding the purpose of a tenancy cannot be lightly disturbed unless the evidence on record clearly warrants such interference.
