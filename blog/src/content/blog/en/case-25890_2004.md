## Summary
In *Vishnu (dead) by L.Rs. v. State of Maharashtra*, the Supreme Court considered whether Clause 30 of the B-1 Agreements—contracts executed between the Government of Maharashtra and the appellant for various irrigation/canal projects—constituted an arbitration clause. The appellant, after abandoning the works in 1985, sought to invoke arbitration under the 1940 Arbitration Act, and the trial court ultimately held Clause 30 to be an arbitration clause and appointed an arbitrator; however, the Bombay High Court partly allowed the respondents' revision application and set aside the order granting leave to amend the plaint. The core legal question framed for the Supreme Court's consideration was the proper characterization of Clause 30 as either a substantive arbitration agreement or a mere administrative provision, given the parties' dispute over whether mutual consent to arbitrate existed.
## Key Holdings
- The core question before the Court was whether Clause 30 of the B-1 Agreements between the Government of Maharashtra and the contractor constituted an arbitration clause.
- A court cannot refer parties to arbitration under Section 21 of the Arbitration Act, 1940 in the absence of consent from both parties to such reference.
- The trial court, after considering evidence on the nature of Clause 30, held it to be an arbitration clause and appointed the Chief Engineer (PWD) as the sole arbitrator to settle the disputes.
- The High Court in revision set aside the trial court's order granting the plaintiff leave to amend the plaint to incorporate a prayer for reference of the dispute to an arbitrator.
