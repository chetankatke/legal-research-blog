## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court of India decision addresses a reference to a larger Bench concerning the correctness of the two-Judge Bench ruling in *K. Raheja Development Corporation v. State of Karnataka* (2005), specifically its paragraph 20. The core legal question involves the distinction between a contract of sale and a works contract under the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, particularly whether a Development Agreement between a developer and a landowner constitutes a works contract, and whether tax authorities can instead rely on a subsequent Tripartite Agreement to characterize the transaction as a works contract for levy of sales tax. A two-Judge Bench in *Larsen & Toubro* had prima facie difficulty accepting the Raheja proposition, noting that the developer merely undertook to develop the property and that the show cause notice contained no allegation of monetary consideration under the Development Agreement itself.
