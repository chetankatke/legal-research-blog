# P.A. Mohammed Riyas v. M.K. Raghavan & Ors

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** [1963] 3 S.C.R. 573

## Summary
In this judgment, the Supreme Court addressed two procedural questions concerning election petitions under the Representation of the People Act, 1951. First, the Court held that an election petitioner is **not required** to file an additional affidavit under Order VI Rule 15(4) of the CPC to support averments in the election petition, beyond the affidavit required by the proviso to Section 83(1) for alleging corrupt practices, clarifying that its earlier decision in *P.A. Mohammed Riyas v. M.K. Raghavan* did not lay down correct law on this limited point. Second, the Court held that an election petition cannot be summarily dismissed merely because the affidavit accompanying allegations of corrupt practices is not strictly in statutory Form 25, provided there is substantial compliance, and the petitioner should be given an opportunity to cure any defect. The appeals arose from a Karnataka High Court order that had rejected an election petition challenging the appellant's election to the 15th Lok Sabha from the Davangere constituency on these procedural grounds.
## Key Holdings
- It is not mandatory under the Representation of the People Act, 1951 for an election petitioner to additionally file an affidavit in terms of Order VI Rule 15(4) of the CPC in support of averments in the election petition.
- The decision in P.A. Mohammed Riyas v. M.K. Raghavan & Ors., (2012) 5 SCC 511, to the extent it suggested such a mandate, does not lay down correct law.
- An election petition is not liable to be summarily dismissed merely because the affidavit in support of corrupt practice allegations is not strictly in the statutory Form No. 25 prescribed under the Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, provided there is substantial compliance.
- The election petitioner must be given an opportunity to cure any defect in the affidavit filed in support of corrupt practice allegations.
- A defect in the affidavit does not render the petition itself not an election petition within the meaning of the Representation of the People Act, 1951.
