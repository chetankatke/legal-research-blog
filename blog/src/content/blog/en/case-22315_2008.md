## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal under Section 15Z of the SEBI Act, 1992, arises from a dispute between Nirma Industries Ltd. and SEBI concerning the withdrawal of an open offer to acquire equity shares of Shree Ram Multi Tech Limited (SRMTL). Nirma had acquired SRMTL shares (24.25% of equity capital) by invoking a pledge that had been given as security for Optionally Fully Convertible Premium Notes issued by the promoters' group companies, which triggered the open offer requirement under Regulation 10 of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and
## Key Holdings
- Invocation of a pledge on equity shares constitutes an 'acquisition' under Regulation 10 of the SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997, thereby triggering the obligation to make a public announcement and open offer.
- Once the open offer obligation is triggered under the Takeover Code, the acquirer cannot withdraw the offer merely on the ground that the shares have been returned to the pledgor.
- SEBI was justified in rejecting the appellants' request to withdraw the open offer to acquire equity shares of Shree Ram Multi Tech Limited, as the acquisition had already occurred through enforcement of the pledge.
- The statutory appeal under Section 15Z of the SEBI Act, 1992 lies against the order of the Securities Appellate Tribunal, and the Supreme Court may examine whether the Tribunal correctly interpreted the Takeover Regulations.
