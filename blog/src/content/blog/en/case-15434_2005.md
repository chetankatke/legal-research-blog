# 15434_2005

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 29.3.2005
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether a writ court under Article 226 of the Constitution can correct manifest errors apparent on the face of the record, even when such errors are not specifically challenged by a party. The case arose from land acquisition proceedings where a de-notification under Section 48(1) of the Land Acquisition Act was recalled, and the Single Judge had set aside this recall order to remedy manifest injustice despite no specific challenge to the recall notification. The Division Bench reversed this, holding that absence of a specific challenge to the notification was fatal. The Supreme Court allowed the appeals, restoring the Single Judge's order and affirming the writ court's power to intervene to prevent manifest injustice arising from errors apparent on the face of the record.
