# 22299_2011

## Summary
The judgment addresses whether offences under Sections 467, 468, and 471 of the IPC, registered when triable by a Judicial Magistrate First Class, must be transferred and tried de novo by the Court of Sessions following the Madhya Pradesh Amendment Act of 2007, which made those offences exclusively triable by the Sessions Court. The case was referred to a Full Bench of the Madhya Pradesh High Court on whether the amendment applies retrospectively and how pending cases (where evidence had already been partially or wholly recorded by the Magistrate) should be handled. The courts below had held that the appellant could only be tried by the Court of Sessions after the amendment. The Supreme Court granted leave to appeal to determine this jurisdictional question arising from the interplay between the original CrPC schedule and the state-level amendment.
