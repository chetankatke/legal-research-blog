# 13856_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 25.02.2009
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether the cancellation of a temporary license granted to the appellant for collection of parking fees by the Bruhath Bangalore Mahanagara Palike was legally valid. The central legal question concerned the authority competent under Section 348 of the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976 to grant such licenses for collection of parking fees. The Court held that under Section 348, only the Standing Committee—not the Assistant Revenue Officer—had the power to grant such license/permission, and since the license was originally granted by an unauthorized officer, the respondents were justified in cancelling it. The Court found no error in the High Court's decision and dismissed the appeal.
## Key Holdings
- Under Section 348 of the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976, only the Standing Committee has the authority to grant license or permission for collection of parking fees.
- The Assistant Revenue Officer lacks the statutory power to grant a license or permission for collection of parking fees under the Act.
- Cancellation of a license or permission granted by an unauthorized authority is legally valid and proper.
- The High Court committed no error in sustaining the respondents' order dated 02.01.2009 cancelling the license/permission granted by the Assistant Revenue Officer.
