## Summary
**Summary:**

This case involves two criminal appeals (Nos. 1020 & 1021 of 2004) before the Supreme Court of India, challenging a common judgment of the Allahabad High Court that upheld the appellants' conviction. The appellants, Sheo Shankar Singh and Sarvajit Singh @ Sobhu, along with Ranjit Singh and Harihar Singh (the latter now deceased), were convicted under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC (murder with common intention) and Section 379 IPC (theft), and sentenced to life imprisonment plus two years rigorous imprisonment. ## Key Holdings
- The appeal of an accused abates upon his death during the pendency of the appeal before the appellate court.
- Offences under Section 302 read with Section 34 of the IPC require the prosecution to establish a shared common intention among the accused to commit the crime.
- When multiple accused participate in a coordinated attack, each can be held liable for the acts of the others under the doctrine of common intention.
- The accused were convicted for murder under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC and additionally for theft under Section 379 IPC, with concurrent sentences imposed.
- An exhortation by one accused to others to kill the victim constitutes a clear indication of common intention, making all participants liable for the resulting murder.
