# 1322_2013

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 4.7.2012
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court heard an appeal by the State of Madhya Pradesh challenging the High Court's order declining leave to appeal against the acquittal of the respondent in a case involving offences under Sections 294 and 436 IPC. The core legal question was whether the High Court was obligated to give reasons while dismissing an application for leave to appeal against a judgment of acquittal. The Court held that the High Court's order was cryptic and non-speaking, merely echoing the trial court's conclusion that the prosecution had failed to prove its case, without independent reasoning. The Court emphasized that it is obligatory on the High Court to record reasons when refusing leave to appeal, as such orders must demonstrate proper application of mind.
## Key Holdings
- The High Court is obligated to give reasons while dismissing an application seeking leave to appeal against a judgment of acquittal.
- An order that merely records that the prosecution failed to prove the offence without any elaboration is a cryptic, non-reasoned order and is unsustainable.
- The Supreme Court granted leave to the State to appeal against the High Court's order declining leave to appeal against the acquittal.
