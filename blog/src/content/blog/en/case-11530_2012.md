## Summary
**Summary:**

This case before the Supreme Court of India consolidated an appeal by Dr. Subramanian Swamy and a writ petition under Article 32, both challenging the High Court of Delhi's refusal to issue a writ of mandamus directing the Election Commission of India (ECI) to incorporate a "paper trail" or "paper receipt" mechanism into Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs). ## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court directed the Election Commission of India to introduce a paper trail (VVPAT) system in Electronic Voting Machines to allow voters to verify that their vote has been correctly recorded.
- The Court held that the existing EVM system, while functional, requires the additional safeguard of a paper receipt to ensure transparency, voter confidence, and proof of accurate vote registration.
- The writ petition under Article 32 of the Constitution was maintainable as it sought enforcement of the fundamental right to free and fair elections through necessary modifications to the EVMs and the Conduct of Election Rules, 1961.
- The Court recognized that electronic voting machines, like all electronic equipment, are susceptible to tampering and hacking, necessitating a verifiable paper backup as a convincing proof of the vote cast.
- The Election Commission was directed to take appropriate steps to incorporate the paper trail mechanism and frame necessary guidelines to effect amendments in the relevant election rules.
