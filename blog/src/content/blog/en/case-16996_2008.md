## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India heard appeals filed by M/s Telestar Travels Pvt. Ltd. challenging the Bombay High Court's order that had only partially reduced a penalty imposed for alleged contraventions of Sections 14 and 8(1) of the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 (FERA). The legal question centers on whether the appellant's commercial arrangement—using a UK-based travel agent (Clyde Travels Ltd.) and routing ticket payments through a BVI company (Bountiful Ltd.) with a Swiss bank account to secure cheaper crew tickets for foreign shipping companies—constituted a permissible business transaction or a violation of foreign exchange regulations. The Directorate of Enforcement contended that Bountiful Ltd. was a paper entity used to siphon funds abroad, while the appellants argued the arrangement was a legitimate method to obtain discounted seaman fares.
