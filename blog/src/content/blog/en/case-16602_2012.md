# 16602_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 05.07.2010

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard an appeal arising from a Section 482 Cr.P.C. petition filed by T.G. Nandakumar before the Kerala High Court seeking quashing of FIR No. 437/CR/OCW-II/EKM, where the High Court had initially stayed proceedings but declined to extend the interim order upon being informed that the investigation had been transferred to the CBI. The key issue was whether the interim stay should continue when the basis for the High Court's refusal to extend it — the CBI's involvement — had fallen away, as the CBI, through the Additional Solicitor General, expressly declined to take up the simple case. Holding that the interim order deserved extension, the Supreme Court directed that the stay granted on 04.05.2012 shall continue, and requested the High Court to decide the Section 482 petition on merits in accordance with law.
