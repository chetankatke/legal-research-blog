## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether the Orissa Municipal Act, 1950 applies to Sundargarh district in Odisha, which is a declared Scheduled Area under Clause 6(1) of the Fifth Schedule to the Constitution. The court examined the interplay between Article 244 (which governs administration of Scheduled Areas under the Fifth Schedule) and Article 243-ZC of Part IX-A, which provides that Part IX-A's provisions on municipalities do not apply to Scheduled Areas, while permitting Parliament to extend them with exceptions and modifications. The judgment focuses on resolving whether pre-existing municipal laws like the 1950 Act continue to operate in Scheduled Areas despite the constitutional bar on Part IX-A's application. The text excerpt cuts off before the final holding, but the analysis centers on the constitutional framework governing municipal governance in Scheduled Areas.

“6. Scheduled Areas.—(1) In this Constitution, 
the  expression  “Scheduled  Areas”  means  such 
areas as the President may by order declare to 
be Scheduled Areas.”
