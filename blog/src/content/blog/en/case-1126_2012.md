## Key Holdings
- SEBI is empowered under Section 11 of the SEBI Act, 1992 read with Section 30 to regulate Credit Rating Agencies through the CRA Regulations, 1999.
- The Securities Appellate Tribunal set aside SEBI's order requiring the respondent to produce audited annual accounts of its promoters for the two years ending December 2010 as a condition for registration as a Credit Rating Agency.
- The SAT remanded the matter to SEBI to consider the respondent's application for registration as a CRA without insisting on the promoters' audited annual accounts.
- Registration as a Credit Rating Agency under Regulation 3 of the CRA Regulations, 1999 does not necessarily require production of the promoters' audited accounts for the prescribed period.
- SEBI is duty-bound to protect investor interests and regulate the securities market, but such regulatory power is subject to the specific requirements of the governing regulations.
