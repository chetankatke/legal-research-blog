# 2448_2010

- **Date:** November 22, 2013

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court dismissed the Special Leave Petition challenging an ex-parte decree granted in a suit for specific performance of an agreement to sell dated 16.3.1993. The decree had been passed when the original defendants willfully absented themselves during evidence recording and were proceeded against ex-parte on 19.4.1996. After the original defendant's death, his Legal Representatives sought to set aside the ex-parte decree, but both lower courts refused, finding no sufficient grounds. The Supreme Court upheld these concurrent findings, holding that no substantial question of law arose warranting interference, and noted that cogent reasons had been given for not setting aside the ex-parte decree.
