## Summary
**Summary:**

These appeals before the Supreme Court of India raised the legal question of whether the opinion of the Chief Justice of the Karnataka High Court carries primacy over that of the Chief Minister when advising the Governor on appointments to the post of Lokayukta or Upa Lokayukta under Section 3(2)(a) and (b) of the Karnataka Lokayukta Act, 1984. The Karnataka High Court (Division Bench) had held that the Chief Justice's opinion indeed has primacy, declaring the Governor's appointment of Justice Chandrashekaraiah as Upa Lokayukta on 21.1.2012 illegal for non-consultation with the Chief Justice, and issued consequential directions. ## Key Holdings
- The Division Bench of the Karnataka High Court held that under the Karnataka Lokayukta Act, 1984, the opinion expressed by the Chief Justice of the High Court has primacy while tendering advice by the Chief Minister to the Governor for appointment of Lokayukta or Upa Lokayukta.
- The Governor's order dated 21.1.2012 appointing Justice Chandrashekaraiah as Upa Lokayukta was declared illegal for having been passed without consulting the Chief Justice of the High Court of Karnataka.
- The High Court issued directions to the State and the Principal Secretary to the Governor to take steps for filling up the post of Upa Lokayukta in accordance with the law as laid down in the judgment.
- The statutory consultation process requires the Chief Minister to seek a panel of eligible persons from the Chief Justice, the Chairman of the Legislative Council, the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly, and the Leaders of the Opposition in both Houses before advising the Governor.
