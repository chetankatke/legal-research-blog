## Summary
**Summary:**

These civil appeals before the Supreme Court arose from a motor accident on April 16, 1990, in which Joy Kuruvila, a 45-year-old Manager earning 2,500 USD per month in New York, died in a head-on collision between his car and a bus. The dependents (widow, two minor children, and elderly mother) filed a claim under Sections 140 and 166 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, seeking Rs. 57,25,000 in compensation, which the Tribunal awarded and the Kerala High Court Division Bench further enhanced while apportioning liability at a 50:50 ratio. The legal questions before the Supreme Court concern the proper computation of compensation (including loss of future earnings, pain and suffering, and loss of consortium) for a high-earning NRI deceased, as well as the correctness of the 50:50 liability apportionment between the car and bus drivers.
