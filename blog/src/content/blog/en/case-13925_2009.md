# 13925_2009

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** January  22, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_

## Summary
In this case, the Supreme Court of India addressed whether interest under Section 16B of the Gift Tax Act was chargeable on revocable transfers of equity shares by the assessees to a partnership firm (M/s Yogesh Chandra & Brothers Associates) for Assessment Year 1989-90. The High Court of Punjab & Haryana had answered the substantial questions of law in favor of the Revenue, holding that since gift tax was leviable on the revocable transfer, interest was payable under Section 16B. The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's judgment, which had relied on a common order in two related cases (G.T.A. No. 2/2001 and 3/2001) that it simultaneously set aside and remanded for de novo consideration. Consequently, all three connected appeals were allowed by remanding the matters back to the High Court for fresh consideration on the merits, with no order as to costs.
## Key Holdings
- Interest under section 16B of the Gift Tax Act is chargeable on revocable transfers of equity shares to a partnership firm.
- The provisions of section 16B(3) of the Gift Tax Act are applicable to revocable transfers of equity shares made to associates.
- The High Court's order holding the assessees liable to pay interest on gift tax was set aside by the Supreme Court.
- The matters were remanded to the High Court for fresh consideration on the merits of the case.
- The appeals were allowed with no order as to costs.
