## Summary
This judgment by the Supreme Court of India, authored by Justice P. Sathasivam, addresses three connected appeals arising from the July 2009 hooch tragedy in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, where 147 persons died and 205 others suffered serious injuries after consuming spurious liquor laced with methyl alcohol. The legal question before the Court concerned the grant and refusal of regular bail under Section 439 of the CrPC in cases involving offences under Sections 302, 307, 328, 120B IPC and various provisions of the Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949, particularly where the allegations included criminal conspiracy to manufacture and distribute poisonous liquor. The appellant Ravindersingh @ Ravi Pavar (A-11) challenged the Gujarat High Court's dismissal of his bail application, while the State of Gujarat challenged the High Court's grant of bail to co-accused Jayesh Hiralal Thakkar (A-2) in connected FIRs registered at Odhav and Kagdapith Police Stations. The Court disposed of all three special leave petitions by common judgment
## Key Holdings
- Leave was granted in all the special leave petitions since they raised common questions of law and fact arising from the same hooch tragedy.
