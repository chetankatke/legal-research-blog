## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal arose from a prosecution under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, where the respondent had issued a cheque for Rs. 1,15,000 in favour of the appellant-complainant, which was dishonoured for insufficient funds. The trial court convicted the respondent, but the High Court reversed the conviction, accepting the respondent's defence that the cheque was merely a security deposit given in the context of a milk-supply arrangement, rather than repayment of a loan. The legal question before the Supreme Court was whether the High Court was justified in disturbing the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the trial court in favour of the complainant
## Key Holdings
- The case arises under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, concerning dishonour of a cheque for Rs. 1,15,000 due to insufficient funds.
