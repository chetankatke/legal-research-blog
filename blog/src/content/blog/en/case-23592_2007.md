## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal concerns a dispute over evacuee property in Masjid Moth, New Delhi, which was auctioned to the appellant's husband in 1958 under the Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, but was subsequently notified for acquisition by the Delhi Administration in 1962 under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894. The legal question centers on the competing rights between the auction purchaser (who received only provisional possession in 1960 and paid the full balance price only in 1980) and the Delhi Development Authority, which claimed the land under the acquisition proceedings. The Division Bench of the Delhi High Court had reversed the Single Judge's decision in favor of the appellant. While the full holding cannot be determined from the excerpt, the case engages with the interplay between the 1954 Act's provisions on evacuee property and the urgency-based acquisition under Section 17(1)(iv) of the Land Acquisition Act.
