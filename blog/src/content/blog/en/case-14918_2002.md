## Summary
In this batch of criminal appeals, a three-judge bench of the Supreme Court was constituted to resolve a conflict of opinion between earlier decisions—particularly *Kishun Singh v. State of Bihar* and *Ranjit Singh v. State of Punjab*—regarding the power of the Sessions Court under Section 193 read with Section 228 of the CrPC, 1973. The core legal question was whether the Sessions Court, at the stage of framing of charge, could take cognizance against persons shown in column 2 of the charge-sheet (accused not put up for trial) when the materials on record disclosed their prima facie complicity, or whether it had to wait until the Section 319 stage.
