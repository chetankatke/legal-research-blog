# State of U.P. v. Hari Ram this special leave petition is dismissed

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** April  08, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In Special Leave Petition (Civil) No. 9711 of 2013, the State of U.P. and another challenged an order against Ram Swaroop & Ors. before a Supreme Court bench comprising Justices K.S. Radhakrishnan and Dipak Misra. The Court dismissed the petition on April 8, 2013, relying entirely on its earlier judgment dated March 11, 2013 in Civil Appeal No. 2326 of 2013 (State of U.P. v. Hari Ram). The short order provides no further reasoning, indicating that the Court treated the issues in the present petition as already settled by the prior decision.
## Key Holdings
- The special leave petition was dismissed in light of the prior judgment in State of U.P. v. Hari Ram pronounced on 11.3.2013.
- The Court followed the principle of consistency with its own earlier precedent when disposing of connected matters.
- The order demonstrates that identical or connected issues are disposed of by reference to a governing judgment without fresh adjudication.
- The dismissal was passed by the bench of K.S. Radhakrishnan and Dipak Misra, JJ. on April 08, 2013.
- No fresh legal issues were adjudicated, as the petition stood covered by the earlier binding decision.
