# 22601_2013

- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:**

## Summary
The Supreme Court dismissed the civil appeal filed by the Goa Coastal Zone Management Authority against Rudresh Naik, finding it devoid of merit. While upholding the underlying decision, the Court exercised leniency by deleting the Rs. 25,000 costs that the Tribunal had imposed on the appellant. The Court granted the appellant a further three weeks to comply with the orders and directions issued by the Tribunal.
## Key Holdings
- The civil appeal was dismissed as being devoid of merit.
- The costs of Rs. 25,000 imposed by the Tribunal were deleted in the peculiar facts of the case.
- The appellant was granted an additional three weeks from the date of the order to comply with the Tribunal's orders and directions.
