## Summary
In this appeal, the J&K Institute of Management, Public Administration & Rural Development challenged the J&K High Court's decision upholding Renu Bala's writ petition against the selection of candidates for Computer Operator/Lab Assistant/System Administrator posts. The core legal issue was whether the selection committee had unlawfully relaxed eligibility criteria in favor of an in-service candidate (Ashok Kumar Koul), thereby violating the rights of other applicants. The High Court found the relaxation genuine and the allegations substantiated, directing Renu Bala's appointment. ## Key Holdings
- Relaxation of selection procedure criteria in favor of a particular candidate violates the rights of all other candidates who applied for the post.
- The genuineness of certificates produced by candidates must be fully examined by the selection authority before including them in the select list.
- In-service candidates are not entitled to undue favor or relaxed standards in the matter of selection to public posts.
- The High Court found the allegations of undue favor granted to an in-service candidate to be genuine and directed his exclusion from the select list.
- The selection committee must act fairly and without bias, and any irregularity in the selection process vitiates the entire selection.
