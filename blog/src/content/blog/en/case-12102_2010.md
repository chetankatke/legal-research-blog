# 12102_2010

## Summary
The Supreme Court heard a criminal appeal arising from the conviction of the appellant under Section 376(2)(f) of the IPC for the brutal rape of an eight-year-old girl, where notice had initially been restricted to the quantum of sentence. The prosecution established the case through 11 witnesses, including the child victim, her parents, and doctors, and both the trial court and the Delhi High Court had concurred in imposing life imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 5,000. The Supreme Court, while expressing grave anguish at the depravity of the crime, affirmed the conviction and the sentence of rigorous imprisonment for life along with the fine. The Court emphasized the horrific nature of the offense against a child and the lifelong trauma it would cause, treating the matter as one warranting the maximum penalty prescribed.
