## Summary
**Summary:**

In *State of Orissa v. Khaga @ Khageswar Naik & Ors.*, the Supreme Court of India addressed the State's appeal against a High Court judgment that had altered the respondents' conviction from Section 302/34 IPC (murder with common intention) to Section 304 Part II IPC (culpable homicide not amounting to murder). The case arose from a 1995 incident in Sambalpur, Odisha, where the three respondents entered the house of Tikeshwar Naik at night, molested his daughter, and when he intervened, assaulted him with a *budia* (axe) and *lathi* (stick), leading to his death. The trial court had convicted the respondents under Sections 457, 354, 506, 302, and 201
