## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court of India raised the legal question of whether an insurance company is liable, under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, to pay compensation for bodily injury sustained by a claimant who was travelling in a goods vehicle as a "spare driver," despite being employed as a driver in a different vehicle owned by the same vehicle owner. The claimant, Saju P. Paul, suffered permanent disability when the goods vehicle capsized due to the rash and negligent driving
## Key Holdings
- An insurance company is liable to pay compensation for bodily injury sustained by a person travelling in a goods vehicle as a spare driver when he is employed as a driver by the owner of that vehicle under the policy of insurance.
- A spare driver employed by the owner of the goods vehicle is not an unauthorized passenger, and the risk of injury to such a person falls within the scope of the insurance policy covering the goods vehicle.
- The liability of the insurer is joint and several with the owner and driver of the goods vehicle for compensation arising out of a motor accident.
- The presence of other unauthorized passengers in the goods vehicle does not absolve the insurer of its liability towards an employee travelling in pursuance of his contract of employment.
