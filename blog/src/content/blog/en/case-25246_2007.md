## Summary
This is a Criminal Appeal (No. 601 of 2008) before the Supreme Court of India arising from the appellant's conviction under Sections 376 (rape) and 417 (cheating) of the Indian Penal Code by the Trial Court, affirmed by the appellate court and the Madras High Court. The factual foundation of the case is the prosecutrix Poomari's allegation that the appellant, a neighbor from the same caste, entered her house when she was alone, gagged her mouth to prevent her from raising an alarm, and forcibly had sexual intercourse with her on the false promise of marriage—events the prosecution claimed began approximately six months before the complaint was formally lodged on 10.10.2003.
