# 23618_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 27.02.2012
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court of India allowed this appeal challenging a Karnataka High Court judgment that had halved the compensation awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (from Rs. 2,16,000 to Rs. 1,08,000) in a motor accident claim case against Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd. The core legal question was whether the High Court was justified in reducing the quantum of compensation granted by the Tribunal. The Court held that on the peculiar facts and circumstances, the High Court ought not to have reduced the compensation, and accordingly set aside the High Court's order while restoring the Tribunal's original award.
## Key Holdings
- Leave granted to appeal against the High Court's order reducing motor accident compensation.
- The High Court ought not to have reduced the compensation awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal on the peculiar facts of the case.
- The Supreme Court set aside the High Court's judgment and restored the Tribunal's compensation award of Rs. 2,16,000/- with interest.
- The Tribunal's assessment of compensation is entitled to deference and should not be lightly disturbed on appeal.
