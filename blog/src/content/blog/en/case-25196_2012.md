## Summary
This appeal before the Supreme Court of India concerns a candidate's challenge to his non-appointment as a District and Sessions Judge (Entry Level) in the Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service, despite his name appearing in the select list. The legal question is whether completion of 35 years of age is a mandatory eligibility requirement for appointment to the post, when the Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service Rules, 2007 do not expressly contain such a provision. The appellant argued that the High Court erred in denying him appointment based merely on the recommendation of the Justice Shetty Commission that candidates should have completed 35 years of age, since such a recommendation was only a suggestion and not a binding rule. The appeal, arising from the High Court's dismissal of the writ petition, was heard by Justice Anil R. Dave after leave was granted.
## Key Holdings
- A candidate for appointment as District and Sessions Judge (Entry Level) must satisfy the eligibility criteria, including the minimum age of 35 years, as on the date of the advertisement.
- The Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service Rules, 2007 do not expressly require completion of 35 years of age for appointment to the post of District and Sessions Judge (Entry Level).
- The High Court erred in denying appointment to the appellant solely on the basis of the Justice Shetty Commission's recommendation regarding the 35-year age requirement, which was merely recommendatory.
- Although the appellant had completed seven years of standing at the bar, he was not eligible for appointment as he had not attained the prescribed minimum age at the relevant time.
