## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Centre for Public Interest Litigation v. Union of India* (Writ Petition No. 681 of 2004), the petitioner sought the constitution of an independent expert committee to evaluate the harmful health effects of soft drinks, particularly on children, along with directions for mandatory disclosure of contents/labels with warnings, and curbs on misleading advertising. The Union of India contended that the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, together with its Rules and Regulations (including the 2011 Packaging and Labelling Regulations), already constituted a comprehensive regulatory regime, and that the Food Authority had addressed the petitioner's grievances via its order dated 12.9.2012.
