## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard appeals filed by the wife (Rumi Bora Dutta) and nephew (Probal Dutta) of the deceased challenging their conviction and life sentence under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC, as affirmed by the Gauhati High Court. The prosecution case was that the wife and nephew conspired to murder the deceased—motivated by the wife's illicit relationship with her nephew—after initially lodging a false FIR alleging a robbery by unknown assailants. The central legal question before the Court was whether the prosecution had sufficiently established the conspiracy and common intention (Section 34 IPC) to murder, given that the accused had fabricated the story of an external robbery. The Court upheld the conviction, finding the evidence—particularly the false FIR and the conduct of the appellants in planting
## Key Holdings
- The conviction of the appellants under Section 302 read with Section 34 IPC for the murder of the deceased was upheld, confirming the existence of a common intention between the wife and nephew to commit the offense.
- The High Court's affirmation of the life sentence imposed by the trial court was maintained, as the prosecution successfully established the guilt of the accused through the chain of circumstances.
- The First Information Report (FIR) lodged by the appellant-wife was held to be false, concocted, and incredible, supporting the prosecution's case that the crime was orchestrated by the appellants themselves.
- The judicial finding that the deceased-husband was murdered by his wife and nephew due to their illicit relationship was sustained, as the court found the circumstantial evidence and confession sufficient to prove the offense beyond reasonable doubt.
- The appeals filed by the convicted appellants were dismissed, affirming the concurrent findings of the trial court and the High Court regarding the culpability of both accused persons in the commission of the murder.
