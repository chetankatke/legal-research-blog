## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal concerned whether a sale deed executed in 1973 was void for having been made in contravention of a temporary injunction order dated 06.05.1971 passed in Suit No. 49 of 1970. The trial court had held the sale deed invalid due to the violation of the injunction, but the lower appellate court reversed, taking the view that the injunction did not survive once the suit was returned for presentation to the court of competent jurisdiction, and further found the 1977 decree fraudulent and not binding on the plaintiffs. The High Court dismissed the second appeal, and the Supreme Court (per G.S. Singhvi, J.) was called upon to decide whether the injunction's subsistence was affected by the return of the plaint.
