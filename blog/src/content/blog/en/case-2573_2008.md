## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court (per Madan B. Lokur, J.) addressed whether the appellant Bhupendra was rightly convicted by the Additional Sessions Judge, Morena, and the High Court of Madhya Pradesh for offences under Sections 498-A, 304-B, and 306 of the Indian Penal Code in connection with the dowry-related death of his wife Geeta Bai, who consumed wheat tablets after being subjected to persistent demands for additional dowry. The Court found the evidence sufficient to establish that the deceased was harassed and tortured for dowry, that her death occurred within seven years of marriage under abnormal circumstances linked to such harassment, and that the appellant was responsible for abetting her suicide. Accordingly, the Court answered the question in the affirmative, upheld the conviction, and dismissed the appeal as being without merit.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court upheld the conviction of the appellant under Sections 498-A, 304-B and 306 of the Indian Penal Code for the dowry-related death of his wife.
- The Court found no merit in the criminal appeal and affirmed the concurrent findings of the trial court and the High Court of Madhya Pradesh.
- The repeated demands for additional dowry, including a she buffalo and Rs. 10,000 in cash, established the offence of cruelty under Section 498-A IPC.
- The circumstances of the deceased consuming poison in her matrimonial home following dowry demands were sufficient to sustain a conviction under Section 304-B IPC for dowry death.
