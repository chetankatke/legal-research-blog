## Summary
This Supreme Court case concerns whether Junior Hindi Translators working in subordinate offices under the Commerce Ministry are entitled to the same upgraded pay-scale (Rs.5500-175-9000) as those in the Central Secretariat Official Language Service (CSOLS), following a 2003 Home Ministry Office Memorandum. The Central Administrative Tribunal and the Calcutta High Court both ruled in favor of the respondents, applying the "equal pay for equal work" doctrine, noting that the Union of India failed to demonstrate any material difference in functional requirements between the two posts since both involved translation work. ## Key Holdings
- The doctrine of 'equal pay for equal work' applies to Junior Hindi Translators performing identical translation functions in subordinate offices as those in the Central Secretariat Official Language Service.
- The burden lies on the employer to demonstrate with material evidence that the functional requirements of the posts are different before denying pay parity between similarly situated employees.
