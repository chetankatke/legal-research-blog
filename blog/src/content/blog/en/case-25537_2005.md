## Summary
This case involves a massacre of five persons in village Railgaon, Rajasthan, on the intervening night of June 28-29, 2001, allegedly carried out as revenge for the prior murder of Kishan Chand, son of the village Sarpanch Ram Narayan. The prosecution alleged that the accused, harboring deep-rooted suspicion against the five deceased, executed a well-planned attack leading to five deaths, following which 29 persons were tried and 17 convicted—six receiving death sentences and eleven life imprisonment. The connected appeals before the Supreme Court arose from convictions under Sections 147, 148, 302, 342, 427, 435, and 460 read with Section 149 IPC, raising questions regarding the adequacy of evidence, the applicability of common intention/conspiracy, and the confirmation or commutation of capital sentences.
