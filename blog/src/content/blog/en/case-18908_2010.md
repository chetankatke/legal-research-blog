## Summary
This Supreme Court case addresses the interpretation of Article 233(2) of the Constitution, specifically the meaning of "service" and whether a person qualifies as an "advocate" or "pleader" for appointment as a District Judge. The central legal question is whether full-time government law officers—such as District Attorneys, Public Prosecutors, and Assistant Advocate Generals—who are appointed through the Public Service Commission and governed by statutory service rules, are eligible for appointment to the District Judge post under Article 233(2).
