## Summary
This case concerns whether a CBI investigating officer (Respondent No. 5) should face prosecution under Section 340 read with Section 195(1)(b
## Key Holdings
- An application under Section 340 read with Section 195(1)(b) of the Cr.P.C. lies when a party alleges that a false statement or affidavit was made before the court in relation to judicial proceedings.
- Before proceeding under Section 340 Cr.P.C., the court must be prima facie satisfied that the statement or affidavit was made with the deliberate intent to mislead the court and is not merely an error of fact.
- A mere discrepancy between an affidavit filed in court and subsequent investigative actions is not sufficient to initiate prosecution under Section 340 without evidence of deliberate falsehood.
- The scope of Section 195(1)(b) Cr.P.C. is confined to offences committed in or in relation to proceedings in court, and does not extend to acts of investigation carried out thereafter.
- The High Court exercises judicial discretion in considering applications under Section 340 Cr.P.C., and such discretion is not to be lightly interfered with in appeal.
