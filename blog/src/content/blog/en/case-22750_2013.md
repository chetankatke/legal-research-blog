## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Medical College & Ors. v. Union of India & Anr.* (Writ Petition (C) No. 580 of 2013), private unaided medical colleges challenged a corrigendum issued by the Medical Council of India (MCI) that restricted the benefit of the "Enhancement of Annual Intake Capacity in Undergraduate Courses Regulations 2013" exclusively to Government Medical Colleges for the academic year 2013-14. The petitioners argued that the corrigendum effectively overrode the statutory Regulations and was discriminatory, violative of Article 14 of the Constitution, since the Regulations were originally intended to apply uniformly to all medical colleges (government and non-government) to address the doctor-population ratio. The MCI defended the corrigendum as being issued in public interest.
