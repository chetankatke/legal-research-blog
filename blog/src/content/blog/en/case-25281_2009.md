## Summary
In this appeal, the Supreme Court of India considered whether the High Court of Madhya Pradesh was justified in reversing the Sessions Court's acquittal of the appellant, who had been charged with rape (Section 376 IPC) and house trespass (Section 450 IPC) of a minor prosecutrix. The central legal question was whether the prosecution had established that the prosecutrix was below 16 years of age, since proof of minority would render the issue of consent legally irrelevant. The appellant argued (through amicus curiae) that the trial court correctly found the prosecution failed to prove the victim's age and that the sexual act was consensual, while the State contended the High Court rightly determined the prosecutrix was a minor. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court reversed the Sessions Court's acquittal and convicted the appellant under Sections 376 and 450 IPC.
- The prosecutrix was found to be below 16 years of age at the relevant time, based on evidence on record.
- Consent of the prosecutrix loses its significance in law if she is found to be below 16 years of age.
- The trial Court's conclusion that the prosecutrix consented and was not below 16 years of age was held to be erroneous.
- The prosecution established the offenses of rape and house-trespass with intent to commit rape against the appellant.
