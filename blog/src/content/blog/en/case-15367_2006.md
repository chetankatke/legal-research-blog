## Summary
In *Venkatesan v. Rani & Anr.*, the Supreme Court addressed the scope of the High Court's revisional jurisdiction under Sections 397 and 401 of the CrPC when reviewing a trial court's order of acquittal. The appellant, charged under Sections 498A, 304-B, and 302 IPC for allegedly burning his wife to death over dowry demands, was acquitted by the Trial Court, but the Madras High Court reversed the acquittal and remanded the matter for fresh disposal. The appellant contended before the Supreme Court that the Trial Court had thoroughly evaluated the evidence and assigned cogent reasons for acquittal, and that the High Court erroneously found inherent flaws in the order. The central legal question was whether the High Court correctly applied the principles governing its revisional jurisdiction in disturbing the trial court's acquittal.
