## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Manoj Giri v. State of Chhatisgarh* (Criminal Appeal No. 470 of 2012), the Supreme Court addressed an appeal against the High Court of Chhattisgarh's judgment, which had upheld the appellant's conviction under Sections 396 (murder during dacoity) and 376(2)(g) (gang rape) of the Indian Penal Code while acquitting him under Section 395 (dacoity). The case arose from a brutal incident on 25.01.2004, in which the prosecutrix, her husband, and her father-in-law were ambushed on a lonely road near Koshtha pond; the prosecutrix was gang-raped, her husband was beaten
## Key Holdings
- The High Court maintained the appellant's conviction under Section 396 IPC for murder committed in the course of dacoity, along with the life sentence imposed.
- The High Court upheld the conviction under Section 376(2)(g) IPC for gang rape, sentencing the appellant to rigorous imprisonment for ten years.
- The High Court set aside the appellant's conviction under Section 395 IPC for dacoity, which had been wrongly awarded by the trial court for a period of five years.
- The trial court had erroneously convicted the appellant under Sections 395, 396, 397, 398 and 376(2)(g) IPC together, while the High Court corrected this by acquitting under Section 395 but sustaining the rest.
- The appellate court affirmed that commission of dacoity coupled with murder and gang rape on the fateful night of 25.01.2004 was established beyond reasonable doubt against the appellant.
