# 18346_2012

## Citation

- **Date:** 27.10.2005

## Summary
**Summary:**

Mumbai Waste Management Ltd. (MWM) challenged the Bombay High Court's order dismissing its writ petition, which had sought to prevent the curtailment of its territorial jurisdiction for hazardous waste management in favor of another operator, SMS Infrastructure Ltd. The principal legal question was whether the Government of India retained the power to re-fix or re-allocate territorial areas of operation for hazardous waste facilities under the Hazardous Waste (Management, Handling and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2008, or whether that power was denuded by the new Rules. MWM contended that under the 2008 Rules, the Secretary of Environment was only a monitoring authority and lacked jurisdiction to re-allocate territorial areas. The Supreme Court, after hearing extensive arguments at the admission stage, upheld the High Court's view and refused to interfere, effectively affirming the Government's authority to re-allocate territorial jurisdiction and directing the petitioner to operate only within its allotted area.
