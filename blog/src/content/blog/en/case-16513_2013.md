## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court of India heard an appeal by U.P. State Road Transport Corporation challenging the Allahabad High Court's affirmation of an Industrial Tribunal award reinstating a driver (C.P. Goswami) with back wages and continuity of service. The respondent had been terminated following a departmental inquiry based on allegations of abusing staff, creating workplace obstruction, and being under the influence of liquor during duty on 17 January 1996. The central legal question was whether the Tribunal was justified in quashing the termination order and granting back wages, given the appellant's contention that the misconduct warranted dismissal and that the "no work, no pay" principle should apply. The Corporation argued that the Tribunal improperly interfered with the disciplinary action and erred in awarding full back wages for the period the workman did not actually serve.
