## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a case involving the abduction and alleged sexual assault of a young woman, in which the appellant Vikas was not named in the original charge-sheet but was sought to be added as an accused during trial. The key legal question concerns the scope and exercise of power under Section 319 of the Cr.P.C. — specifically, whether the Trial Court was justified in taking cognizance against the appellant for offences under Sections 363, 366(A), 120B, and 376(2)(g) IPC based on evidence produced during
## Key Holdings
- Under Section 319 of the CrPC, a trial court may summon an additional person as accused if evidence produced during trial discloses that such person committed the offence, even if not named in the charge-sheet.
- The trial court must be 'satisfied' based on evidence led during trial that the person sought to be arraigned has committed an offence before exercising power under Section 319 of the CrPC.
- The High Court under Section 482 of the CrPC has discretionary power to quash criminal proceedings, and will not ordinarily interfere with the trial court's order summoning an additional accused if there is prima facie evidence.
- Once the trial court forms a satisfaction that a person not named in the charge-sheet has committed the offence, cognizance can be taken and non-bailable warrant issued against such person.
- An appeal lies to the Supreme Court against the High Court's order dismissing a petition under Section 482 of the CrPC challenging summoning under Section 319 of the CrPC.
