## Summary
This is a criminal appeal in the Supreme Court of India arising from a conviction under Section 302/34 IPC (murder with common intention), where appellants Litta Singh and Kalla Singh, along with their father Boga Singh, were alleged to have attacked Hansraj Singh with lathis and a gandasi on 7th February 2001, following a prior quarrel, causing injuries that led to his death in hospital the next day. The Additional Sessions Judge convicted all three accused, and the Rajasthan High Court dismissed their appeal, prompting the present special leave petition. ## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court considered whether the appellants shared a common intention under Section 34 IPC to commit the murder of Hansraj Singh.
- The conviction under Section 302/34 IPC was based on the concerted attack by the accused using lathis and a gandasi resulting in fatal head injuries.
- The High Court upheld the trial court's conviction relying on eyewitness testimony of the complainant and other witnesses who saw the accused beating the victim.
- The recovery of weapons of offence and forensic evidence corroborated the prosecution's version of the incident.
- Appellant No. 2 Kalla Singh was granted bail by the Supreme Court during the pendency of the appeal.
