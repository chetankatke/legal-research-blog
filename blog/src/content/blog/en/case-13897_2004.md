## Key Holdings
- A donation deed granting property to donees with a stipulation that only their male legal heirs shall inherit, with right of alienation, creates only a life estate in the donees.
- Upon the death of a donee without male issue, the property does not devolve upon the female heirs of the donee but must go to the nearest male reversioner from the donor's family line.
- Where one donee dies issueless, the surviving donee becomes the full usufructuary owner of the property subject to the same conditions of the original donation.
- The Hindu Succession Act, 1956 was extended to the Union Territory of Pondicherry with effect from 1.10.1963, applying its provisions to inheritance disputes in that territory.
- A sale deed executed by a female heir in violation of the conditions of the original donation deed restricting alienation to male legal heirs is not maintainable as against the rightful male reversioner.
