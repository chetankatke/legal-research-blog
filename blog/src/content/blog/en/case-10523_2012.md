# Tax Appellate Tribunal in Jamshri Ranjitsinghji Spinning and Weaving Mills v. Inspecting Assistant Commissioner

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
In *Commissioner of Income Tax v. M/s Excel Industries Ltd.*, the Supreme Court addressed whether the benefit of duty-free import entitlements obtained through advance licences and duty entitlement pass books (issued against export obligations) accrues as income in the year of export or in the year the duty-free imports are actually made. The assessee, maintaining accounts on a mercantile basis, contended that such benefits should only be taxed when the raw materials are imported and consumed, relying on the ITAT's decision in *Jamshri Ranjitsinghji Spinning and Weaving Mills*. The Supreme Court held that the income does not accrue in the year of export but in the year in which the imports are made, aligning with the assessee's position and the *Jamshri* precedent relied upon.
## Key Holdings
- Income from duty free import entitlements obtained through advance licences and duty entitlement pass books accrues in the year the duty free imports are made, not in the year the exports are made.
- Under Section 28(iv) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, the right to receive the benefit of duty free imports becomes vested and absolute only upon fulfillment of the export obligation, but taxability is determined when the raw material is actually imported and consumed.
- The Assessing Officer distinguished the Jamshri Ranjitsinghji decision on the ground that it pertained to assessment year 1985-86 when the export promotion scheme was materially different.
- The assessee, maintaining accounts on a mercantile basis, is entitled to claim deduction of advance licence and duty entitlement pass book benefits receivable as they accrue only upon actual import and consumption of raw materials.
- The taxability of benefits arising from business under Section 28(iv) includes the value of any benefit or perquisite, whether convertible into money or not, arising from carrying on a business or profession.
