## Summary
**Summary:**

These three consolidated criminal appeals before the Supreme Court arose from a Karnataka High Court judgment that acquitted two accused (Smt. Nagavva and Doddappa Ningappa Ugarkhod) but upheld the murder convictions under Section 302/149 IPC and related convictions against six other family members. The dispute stemmed from intra-family land grievances, where the accused harbored grudges over land distribution and prior incidents of assault and theft preceded the fatal occurrence. One appellant (Sataveer Basappa Hariyal) died during the pendency of the appeal, while the remaining appellants challenged their convictions and life sentences. The excerpt provided does not extend to the Court's ultimate holding on the merits of the appeals.
