# 13166_2010

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 3.3.2010
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court addressed an appeal arising from the Delhi High Court's order permitting withdrawal of an appeal (FAO (OS) 349 of 2009), with the appellants objecting not to the withdrawal itself but to certain observations made by the High Court while granting leave to withdraw, along with the extension of interim relief (restraining alienation of the Rights issue) until 10.05.2010. The key legal question was whether such observations and continuation of interim relief were proper when an appeal is withdrawn. The Court held that the High Court's observations regarding the prima facie findings could not be said to be incorrect, particularly because the appellants' own counsel had expressly requested clarification that the observations and decision in the impugned order should not influence other judicial forums. The Court effectively upheld the High Court's order, finding no infirmity in the observations made or the interim relief extended.
## Key Holdings
- The observations made by the High Court while permitting withdrawal of the appeal, that the impugned order partakes of a prima facie finding, cannot be said to be incorrect.
- Interim relief granted during the pendency of an appeal may be extended even after the appeal is withdrawn in order to maintain status quo between the parties.
- A request for clarification made by counsel at the time of withdrawal—that observations in the impugned order should not influence other judicial forums—was properly recorded and addressed by the High Court.
- Interim orders restraining alienation or creation of third-party rights were passed solely to maintain status quo and not on a consideration of the respective merits of the parties' cases.
