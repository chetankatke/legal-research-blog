## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court case addressed whether an equitable mortgage created by deposit of title-deeds requires compulsory registration under Section 17(1)(c) of the Registration Act, 1908. Punjab National Bank had sanctioned loans to M/s. Ultra Tech Private, with title deeds of the borrowers' lands deposited as security in a town notified under Section 58(f) of the Transfer of Property Act. The State of Haryana resisted mutation in revenue records, contending that the instrument of deposit was compulsorily registrable and attracted stamp duty and registration fees.
