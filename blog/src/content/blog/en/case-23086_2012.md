## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) arises from the Andhra Pradesh High Court's dismissal of its petition seeking cancellation of bail granted to V. Vijay Sai Reddy (A-2), a co-accused in the high-profile Y.S. Jaganmohan Reddy corruption and money-laundering case. The case involves allegations that A-2, as founder Director of M/s Jagathi Publications and Financial Advisor to Y.S.
## Key Holdings
- The High Court has the power to cancel bail granted to an accused if circumstances warrant such cancellation.
- Cancellation of bail can be sought when the prosecution demonstrates that the accused has violated bail conditions or that grounds exist for believing the accused may tamper with evidence or influence witnesses.
- The Supreme Court may interfere with the High Court's order on cancellation of bail if the order is perverse or based on incorrect appreciation of facts and law.
- Allegations of money laundering and corruption under the Prevention of Corruption Act are serious offenses that weigh against grant of bail.
- The grant of bail to a person accused of economic offenses involving large-scale laundering of public funds requires strict scrutiny of the circumstances.
