## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court case involves appeals by Aslam and Galli challenging their conviction under Section 376 read with Section 34 IPC (rape with common intention), affirmed by the Allahabad High Court, for the gang rape of a 19-year-old married woman (PW-1) who was working alone in a field on 16.01.1992. The appellants contested the conviction on grounds including delay in filing the FIR, absence of injuries on the prosecutrix, and the non-examination of the doctor who conducted her medical examination. The Trial Court and High Court had relied on the testimony of the prosecutrix (PW-1) — corroborated by PW-2, the village Pradhan — finding her account credible despite the absence of physical injuries,
## Key Holdings
- The testimony of the prosecutrix (PW-1) inspired confidence and was relied upon as the basis for conviction, with corroboration from PW-2.
- Defense contentions regarding delay in lodging the FIR, absence of injuries on the victim, and the victim's character were rejected in their entirety.
- The absence of injuries on the victim was adequately explained by the fact that the incident occurred in a Barsin field.
- Both the Trial Court and the High Court, after re-appreciation of the entire evidence, upheld the conviction of the appellants under Section 376 read with Section 34 of the IPC, warranting rigorous imprisonment for seven years.
