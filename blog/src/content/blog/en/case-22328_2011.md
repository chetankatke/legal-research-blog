# 22328_2011

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 07/01/2011

- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal addressed the jurisdictional question of whether an appeal against an order of acquittal in a complaint case lies to the Sessions Court under Section 378(1) of the CrPC or to the High Court under Section 378(4). The case arose from a food adulteration prosecution under the Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, where the appellant-manufacturer was acquitted by the Magistrate, and the State filed an appeal before the Sessions Court. The High Court held that the Sessions Court lacked jurisdiction and transferred the appeal to itself, prompting the appeal before the Supreme Court. The judgment ultimately resolves the conflict between Sections 378(1) and 378(4) regarding the appropriate forum for appeals against acquittal in complaint cases.
