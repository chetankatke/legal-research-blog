## Key Holdings
- Under Section 13(1)(h) of the Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, the landlord's bona fide requirement may be pleaded for the entire family, not merely for one family member.
- Concurrent findings of fact by the trial court and first appellate court can be disturbed by the High Court in second appeal where the landlord's claim of bona fide need is not established.
- Bona fide need encompassing both commercial and residential requirements of the landlord and his family members is a valid ground for seeking eviction under Section 13(1)(h).
- The death of the landlord's wife during the pendency of proceedings does not defeat the eviction claim where the pleaded need is that of the landlord and his family collectively.
