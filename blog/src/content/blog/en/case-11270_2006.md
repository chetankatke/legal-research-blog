## Summary
**Summary:**

These civil appeals before the Supreme Court of India challenged the Bombay High Court's judgment declaring Section 33A of the Bombay Police Act, 1951 (inserted by the 2005 Amendment) as ultra vires Articles 14 and 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, on the ground that the provision imposed unreasonable restrictions on dance performances in hotels, bars, and restaurants. The provision was enacted by the State of Maharashtra to curb alleged prostitution rackets, obscene dance forms, and criminal activity allegedly conducted through such establishments, following reports of numerous license violations and rescue of minor girls. The core legal question concerns the constitutional validity of the State's regulatory power over places of public amusement, particularly whether the restrictions imposed under Section 33A on performance licences for dance bars are a reasonable restriction in the interest of public order, morality, and decency, or an arbitrary invasion of the right to practice any profession/trade.
