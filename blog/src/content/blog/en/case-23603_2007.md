## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal arises from a divorce petition filed by the appellant-husband under Section 13 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, before the Family Court at Jaipur, seeking dissolution of his 1990 marriage on the ground of cruelty. The appellant alleged that the respondent-wife abused his aged father, pressured him to abandon his family, abruptly left the matrimonial home in March 1991, and subsequently refused to return or even allow him to meet their newly-born son, despite multiple reconciliation efforts. Both the Family Court and the Division Bench of the Rajasthan High Court dismissed the petition, and the matter was carried to the Supreme Court by way of special leave. ## Key Holdings
- Cruelty towards a spouse's family members, particularly verbal abuse and neglect of an aged father-in-law, can constitute mental cruelty as a ground for divorce under Section 13 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955.
- A wife's unilateral decision to leave the matrimonial home without reasonable cause and refusal to return despite repeated reconciliation efforts constitutes willful desertion entitling the husband to divorce.
- The husband's duty to maintain his aged parents cannot be compromised by spousal pressure, and a spouse's persistent demand to abandon such duty aggravates the ground of cruelty.
- Withholding information about the birth of a child and denying the husband access to the newborn amounts to mental cruelty supporting dissolution of marriage.
