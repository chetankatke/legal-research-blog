## Summary
In this case, the appellant challenged the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission's order upholding the rejection of his insurance claim under the 'Janta Gramin Vyaktigat Durghatna Policy,' which was taken in his name by his father for Rs. 7,00,000. The legal question centers on whether the appellant's 100% disability in his right eye and hearing loss resulted from the accidental fall on 22.10.1999, or from pre-existing conditions (Phthisis Bulbi and congenital hearing loss) as contended by the insurance company. ## Key Holdings
- The insurance policy covered loss of one eye directly caused by accident, making the appellant's claim for loss of vision in the right eye potentially compensable.
- The burden lies on the insurance company to prove that the disability was pre-existing or not caused by the accident, and mere assertions without substantive evidence are insufficient to reject a claim.
- Medical certificates from multiple doctors and the District Medical Board establishing 100% disability in the right eye constitute strong evidence supporting the claim arising from the accidental fall.
- The State Commission and National Commission erred in upholding the rejection of the claim where medical evidence clearly established the causal connection between the accident and the disability suffered by the appellant.
- When there is conflicting medical evidence, the consumer fora should prefer contemporaneous medical records and expert medical board opinions over belated claims of pre-existing conditions raised by the insurer.
