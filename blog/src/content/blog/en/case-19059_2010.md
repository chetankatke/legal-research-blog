## Summary
In *Anil Kumar Mahajan v. Union of India*, the Supreme Court considered whether a High Court could permit the withdrawal of a writ petition filed by an alleged "insane person" through a lawyer who received instructions directly from him, without first determining the question of his mental capacity. The appellant, an IAS officer, challenged the Delhi High Court's order dismissing his petition as withdrawn with liberty to refile through a next friend, after respondents raised the issue that he was mentally incapacitated. The two key legal questions were whether the High Court erred in not adjudicating the insanity claim and whether a lawyer could validly act on instructions from a person of unsound mind. The matter arose from the appellant's long-pending service dispute regarding adverse ACR entries and denial of promotion to the IAS selection grade.
## Key Holdings
- The High Court erred in not deciding the threshold question of whether the appellant is an insane person before permitting withdrawal of the petition.
