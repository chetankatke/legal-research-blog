## Summary
**Summary:**

This case arises from Special Leave Petitions filed by trained teachers seeking appointment to vacant primary teacher posts in Bihar, which were withdrawn in 2006 based on the State of Bihar's solemn undertaking to recruit trained teachers on available vacancies in a decentralized manner, with age relaxation as directed by the Patna High Court. When the State failed to honor its commitments, the petitioners filed contempt petitions, which were disposed of with directions to implement the undertaking and give priority to trained teachers in appointments. The present matter, along with numerous connected contempt petitions, writ petitions, and interlocutory applications, comes before the Supreme Court due to the State's continued default in fulfilling its assurances regarding recruitment of trained primary teachers in Bihar. ## Key Holdings
- The State of Bihar gave an undertaking in 2006 to recruit and fill vacant primary school teacher posts with trained teachers, as a basis for withdrawal of the original Special Leave Petition.
- The Bihar Elementary Teachers Appointment Rules, 2003 were quashed by the Patna High Court, necessitating new recruitment rules to be framed for appointment of trained teachers in a decentralized manner with age relaxation.
- Priority in appointment to vacant primary teacher posts is to be given to trained teachers in accordance with the State's categorical commitment before the Supreme Court.
- Where the number of available trained teachers is less than the number of vacancies, no selection test is required to that extent, and reference to the Bihar Public Service Commission for recruitment may be modified accordingly.
- Failure of the State of Bihar to honour its commitments led to contempt proceedings, and the Court directed strict implementation of its earlier undertaking and assurances.
