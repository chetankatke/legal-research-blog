# 1027_2005

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 28.09.2004
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed whether an insurance company could avoid liability for a motor accident claim when the premium cheque was dishonoured before the accident but the intimation of dishonour was communicated to the policy-holder only afterwards. The Court held that, following its earlier decision in *United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Laxmamma*, the insurance company remains liable to satisfy the award because the intimation regarding the dishonour of the cheque and cancellation of the policy was communicated to the policy-holder after the date of the accident (26.04.2000, while the accident occurred on 19.04.2000). Consequently, the Court dismissed the appeal, affirming the liability of the insurance company to pay compensation to the claimants, with the right to recover the same from the vehicle owner.
## Key Holdings
- The insurance company is liable to satisfy the award if intimation regarding the dishonour of the cheque and cancellation of the policy is communicated to the policy-holder after the date of the accident.
- The defence that the policy of insurance was not valid because the cheque was dishonoured prior to the accident does not exonerate the insurance company from paying compensation when intimation of dishonour was given after the accident.
- Where the cheque for the insurance premium was dishonoured on 17.04.2000 but intimation to the policy-holder was given on 26.04.2000, after the accident on 19.04.2000, the insurance company cannot contend that the policy-holder was not holding a valid policy.
- The insurance company and the owner were held jointly and severally liable by the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal to pay compensation to the claimants.
- The insurance company cannot recover the compensation amount paid to the claimants from the owner when the intimation of dishonour of the premium cheque was communicated after the accident.
