## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court is examining whether the penalty of "removal from service" imposed on a Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangthan employee for forcibly entering his Principal's office in a drunken state violates the **doctrine of proportionality**. The respondent, an Upper Division Clerk, had admitted the misconduct and apologized, leading the Disciplinary Authority to impose removal without a regular inquiry (based on his admission under the CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965). While the Central Administrative Tribunal upheld the penalty, the High Court found removal disproportionate and substituted it with reinstatement (continuity of service limited to pensionary benefits), no back wages, and withholding of two annual increments.
