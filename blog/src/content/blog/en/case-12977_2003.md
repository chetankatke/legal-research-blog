## Summary
**Summary:**

These two civil appeals before the Supreme Court arose from a common Delhi High Court judgment concerning the determination of market value of land acquired in village Shahibabad Daulatpur under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894, following notifications under Sections 4, 6, and 17(1) dated 26.03.1983. The core legal question was the appropriate compensation for the acquired lands, which the Acquisition Officer initially fixed at Rs.13,000 per bigha (Block-A) and Rs.6,000 per bigha (Block-B), later enhanced by the reference court to Rs.17,500–Rs.18
## Key Holdings
- The case concerns determination of market value of land acquired under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894, following simultaneous notifications under Sections 4, 17(1), and 6 issued on 26.03.1983.
- The Acquisition Officer initially fixed compensation at Rs.13,000/- per bigha for Block-A land and Rs.6,000/- per bigha for Block-B land.
- The Reference Court enhanced the compensation to Rs.17,500/- per bigha and Rs.18,000/- per bigha for land abutting the road.
- The High Court further enhanced the market value of the acquired land to Rs.42,000/- per bigha in its impugned judgment dated 07.02.2003.
- The appellants being still dissatisfied with the compensation awarded have approached the Supreme Court seeking further enhancement of the market value.
