## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard appeals by the State of U.P. and U.P. State Sugar Corporation against a High Court order directing deletion of the Corporation's name from revenue records in respect of certain agricultural land. The dispute arose under the U.P. Sugar Undertakings (Acquisition) Act, 1971, which vested twelve sick sugar mills in the Corporation, and the central legal question was whether the agricultural land appurtenant to a vested sugar factory passed to the Corporation along with the undertaking. The High Court held that only the sugar manufacturing undertaking — comprising plants, machinery, and equipment specified in Section 2(h) — stood transferred, and that the agricultural land did not vest in the
## Key Holdings
- Agricultural land does not vest in the U.P. State Sugar Corporation under the U.P. Sugar Undertakings (Acquisition) Act, 1971, even when the sugar mill itself vests in the Corporation.
- The expression 'scheduled undertaking' under Section 2(h) of the 1971 Act refers only to plants, machinery, equipment, and assets used in the manufacture of sugar by vacuum pans and mechanical power.
- Under Section 3 of the Act, a scheduled undertaking vests in the Corporation free from all debts, mortgages, charges, encumbrances, and similar obligations except liens for advances on sugar stock or stock-in-trade.
- The respondent-sugar factory, listed at Item-7 of the Second Schedule, stood vested in the Corporation with effect from 28th October 1984 pursuant to the notification dated 27th October 1984.
