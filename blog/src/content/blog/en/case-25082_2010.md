## Summary
**Summary:**

This case arises from civil suits filed by Col. Surat Singh seeking declaration of ownership and permanent injunction regarding suit land that he had sold to Col. Girdhar Singh and his family without specifying khasra numbers, but which the buyers subsequently sold in pieces to other defendants with specific khasra numbers. The central legal question concerns the validity of subsequent sales of specific parcels by purchasers who had themselves acquired undivided land without specified khasra numbers, and the effect of discrepancies between the sale deed and mutation entries. ## Key Holdings
- A sale of undivided share in joint holding land without specifying khasra numbers does not entitle the purchaser to sell specific parcels with specific khasra numbers.
- Where the area in a sale deed differs from the mutation entries, the trial court may order correction of the mutation entries to reflect the actual area sold.
- Corrections to mutation entries do not affect actual possession of the suit property, which continues as before until changed by partition proceedings between co-sharers.
- A co-sharer selling undivided interest in joint property passes only the share and not title to any specific portion of the land.
