# 19172_2007

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** April 03, 2013
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This appeal arose from a labor dispute where the respondent, a Motormate in the Haryana Housing Board, was terminated in 1996 and claimed to have completed more than 240 days of service in the relevant year, entitling him to protection under Section 25-F of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947. The Labour Court and the High Court found non-compliance with Section 25-F and awarded reinstatement without back-wages. The Supreme Court accepted the concurrent factual finding that the respondent had indeed worked more than 240 days, rendering his termination void. However, the Court modified the relief by substituting reinstatement with compensation of Rs. 1 lakh (payable within four weeks), reasoning that reinstatement was not warranted given the short duration of his service.
## Key Holdings
- Non-compliance with Section 25-F of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 renders the termination of an employee who has completed 240 days of service in a year as bad and invalid.
- The Supreme Court will not disturb a concurrent finding of fact that an employee had worked more than 240 days in the relevant year.
- Reinstatement can be substituted with monetary compensation where the court deems it appropriate in the interests of justice.
- The award of compensation in lieu of reinstatement is a permissible modification of the Labour Court's relief under the Industrial Disputes Act.
- Compensation of Rs. 1 lakh was awarded in lieu of reinstatement to a workman whose termination was found to be in violation of Section 25-F.
