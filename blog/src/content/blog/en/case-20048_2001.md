## Summary
The Supreme Court appeals challenged the Delhi High Court's dismissal of writ petitions filed by long-standing licensees of railway shops at Sadar Bazar, Delhi, contesting the Railways' decision to enhance the licence fee from Rs.21 to Rs.270 per sq. yard per annum with retrospective effect from 1.11.1980, and the subsequent termination notices for non-payment. The key legal question was whether the Railway Administration could validly enhance the licence fee to Rs.270 per sq. yard despite the Railway Minister having earlier observed that such revision was excessive and recommended reconsideration with only a 5-10% increase. The matter involved principles of administrative law regarding the binding nature of ministerial directions on subordinate authorities and the legality of retrospective enhancement of licence fees for railway property that was originally evacuee property. ## Key Holdings
- The Railway Administration has the authority to enhance the license fee for railway property from Rs.21 to Rs.270 per sq. yard per annum with retrospective effect from 1.11.1980.
- The High Court was justified in dismissing the writ petitions challenging the notice dated 25.5.1987 for enhanced license fee as the Railway had the power to revise the same.
- Termination of licences and direction to vacate premises for non-payment of outstanding license fee dues is a valid exercise of the Railways' rights as licensor.
- The order of the Hon'ble Railway Minister dated 26.9.1980 staying the auction and directing reconsideration did not create any binding estoppel against the Railway Administration's power to enhance the license fee.
- Long-standing licensees of pre-independence evacuee property do not acquire any protected tenancy or leasehold rights merely by continuous possession and payment of license fees.
