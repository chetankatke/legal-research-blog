## Summary
**Summary:**

This Supreme Court appeal concerns the jurisdiction of the Commissioner appointed under Section 62 of the Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, specifically whether the Commissioner can examine the legality of a dismissal order of an employee with disability. The appellant, a primary teacher with disability, was dismissed from service after prolonged unauthorized absence, and the Commissioner had set aside the dismissal before the Single Judge and Division Bench of the Gujarat High Court reversed that order. The core legal question before the Supreme Court is the scope of the Commissioner's authority under Section 62 to inquire into the deprivation of rights of persons with disabilities, including service-related matters such as dismissal. ## Key Holdings
- The Commissioner appointed under Section 62 of the Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995 has jurisdiction to inquire into the legality of an order of dismissal from service of a disabled person if it comes to his notice that such person has been deprived of his rights.
- The Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995 is a beneficial legislation that protects disabled employees from being deprived of their statutory rights guaranteed under the Act.
- Unauthorized absence from duty and failure to submit any medical certificate or explanation in response to charge-sheet can constitute valid grounds for disciplinary proceedings against an employee, including a disabled employee.
- The procedural safeguards of issuing notice and charge-sheet must be followed before passing any order of dismissal affecting a disabled person, and non-compliance can vitiate the proceedings.
