# 2542_2012

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 13.4.1952
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

The Supreme Court heard an appeal by Gurudwara Sahib, which sought a declaration of ownership over disputed land based on adverse possession since 1952, along with correction of revenue records and a permanent injunction against the Gram Panchayat. The lower courts had granted the injunction but denied the declaration, holding that a suit for declaration of ownership cannot be founded on adverse possession, as it is a "shield and not a sword" available only as a defence. The trial court had found the appellant's adverse possession factually established since 13.4.1952, but framed the legal issue of maintainability of a declaratory suit based on adverse possession, which was answered against the plaintiff. The case thus presents the recurring legal question of whether adverse possession can be the basis of an independent suit for declaration of title, rather than merely a defensive plea.
