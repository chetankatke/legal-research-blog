## Summary
In *Kaliya v. State of Madhya Pradesh*, the Supreme Court heard an appeal challenging the appellant's conviction under Section 302 IPC for setting her daughter-in-law (Guddi) on fire, leading to her death from 100% burn injuries on 18.6.1984, while the co-accused husband and son were acquitted of Section 498-A IPC charges. The central legal issue concerned the reliability and sufficiency of the dying declaration — recorded by Dr. Nirmal Kumar Gupta in the presence of staff nurse Merry Kutti Michael — in which the deceased stated that her mother-in-law poured kerosene and set fire to her saree. The Trial Court and High Court had both relied primarily on this dying declaration to uphold the conviction and life sentence. The matter was before the Supreme Court to assess whether the dying declaration, taken alone, was sufficient to sustain the murder conviction against the appellant.
