## Summary
**Summary:**

This criminal appeal before the Supreme Court of India arose from the appellant's conviction under Sections 302, 376, and 201 of the Indian Penal Code for the murder and rape of a 4-year-old girl (Pooja) and for causing disappearance of evidence, with both the trial court and the Rajasthan High Court affirming the life sentence. The legal question centered on whether the circumstantial evidence presented by the prosecution established a complete and unbroken chain conclusively pointing to the appellant's guilt. The Amicus Curiae argued that the prosecution failed to satisfactorily establish the circumstances and that material contradictions in the depositions of the deceased's family witnesses (P.Ws. 1-4) undermined the conviction. The judgment excerpt cuts off before revealing the Supreme Court's final holding on the appeals.
