## Summary
**Summary:**

This criminal appeal by Ramswaroop and Chintu Mahte challenges the Madhya Pradesh High Court's judgment confirming their conviction by the IInd Additional Session Judge, Shivpuri, under Sections 148, 302 read with 149, 452, and 325 read with 149 of the IPC. The prosecution's case arose from an incident on 09.09.1989, where a dispute over cows damaging crops led to a group of accused—including the appellants—attacking the deceased Badri at his home, inflicting fatal injuries with luhangis and lathis, and also assaulting his mother Gourabai. The deceased succumbed to his injuries on the way to the hospital, prompting registration of FIR No. 12/1989 and leading to the trial court's conviction of all accused. The Supreme Court was called upon to examine whether the High Court was justified in upholding the conviction based on the evidence on record regarding the appellants' participation in the common object of the unlawful assembly.
