## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal by the Bangalore Development Authority (BDA) arises from a land acquisition dispute concerning 22 guntas of land in Survey No. 23/10 of Ejipura, acquired in 1965 for the formation of Koramangala Layout, with possession taken in 1969 and 1978. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court allowed the first appeal and remitted the matter to the trial Court for fresh disposal on grounds arising from the dispute over acquired and allotted land.
- Statutory notice to the Bangalore Development Authority is a mandatory prerequisite before filing a suit against it, and its non-compliance renders the suit unsustainable.
- A landowner whose land is acquired under a Land Acquisition Award and whose possession is taken over by the acquiring authority cannot transfer valid title to subsequent purchasers after possession has been handed over for formation of a layout.
- Possession of acquired land taken by the Land Acquisition Officer and handed over to the engineering section for layout formation constitutes completion of the acquisition process, barring subsequent claims by private parties.
- The Supreme Court granted leave to appeal against the High Court's order remanding the matter, indicating the legal questions regarding acquisition, possession, and subsequent sale deeds warranted examination.
