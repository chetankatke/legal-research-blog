## Summary
**Summary:**

This case arises from appeals against the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission's (NCDRC) judgment concerning 17 insurance claims filed by M/s Garg Sons International against the Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India Ltd. (ECGC). The central legal question is whether the ECGC was justified in rejecting the insured's claims on the ground that the exporter failed to comply with Clause 8(b) of the insurance policy, which mandated that the insurer be informed of any default by the foreign buyer within 45 days of the payment becoming due. ## Key Holdings
- The insurer's liability under the export credit insurance policy is conditional upon the insured's compliance with the mandatory requirement of Clause 8(b) regarding timely intimation of default by the foreign importer.
- The period of 45 days from the date the payment became due is a mandatory stipulation under Clause 8(b) of the insurance agreement, and failure to communicate the default within this period disentitles the insured from claiming under the policy.
- Insurance claims with respect to which information about the default was not furnished to the insurer within the time period stipulated in the agreement cannot be entertained or allowed.
- The Consumer Protection Act, 1986 applies to disputes between the Export Credit Guarantee Corporation and the insured exporter, and the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission has jurisdiction to adjudicate such insurance disputes.
- A government company engaged in the business of insuring exporters is subject to consumer forum jurisdiction for claims arising from alleged breach of mandatory terms in the insurance contract.
