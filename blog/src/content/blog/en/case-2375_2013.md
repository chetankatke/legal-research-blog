# 2375_2013

## Summary
This case arises from a conviction where the trial court had found the appellant guilty under Section 3(1)(xi) of the SC & ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 for allegedly molesting a victim in his shop, but the High Court of Calcutta converted the conviction to Section 354 IPC and reduced the sentence. The key legal question before the Supreme Court concerned whether the High Court was justified in holding itself to be *functus officio* and refusing to entertain the parties' application to compound the offence after it had already delivered its judgment. ## Key Holdings
- The High Court may convert a conviction from Section 3(1)(xi) of the SC & ST Act to Section 354 IPC where there is no evidence the victim was singled out on account of caste.
- Once a judgment has been delivered, the court becomes functus officio and cannot entertain an application to compound the offence in the absence of any pending lis.
- The remedy of parties seeking to compound an offence after judgment lies in moving the Supreme Court, not the court that rendered the judgment.
- The trial court convicted the appellant under Section 3(1)(xi) of the SC & ST Act despite no charge being framed under Section 354 IPC, yet the High Court properly modified the conviction to Section 354 IPC with reduced sentence.
