## Summary
This Supreme Court appeal arose from a land acquisition dispute where the Patna High Court had reduced the compensation awarded by the Reference Court to a landowner whose 3.54-acre land was acquired for the Orni-reservoir in Bihar. The key legal questions were whether the Reference Court's market valuation of Rs. 250 per decimal was excessive, and whether damages for standing crops (Rs. 10,000) were properly awarded. The High Court had reduced the market rate to Rs. 100 per decimal and struck down the standing crops compensation, requiring the claimant to refund part of the amount
