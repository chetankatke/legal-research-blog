# 17336_2006

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 27.7.2007
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
The Supreme Court addressed appeals by the New Delhi Municipal Council (NDMC) challenging interim orders of the Delhi High Court, which had directed NDMC to either reinstate three former employees (whose terminations were declared illegal by the Labour Court) or pay them their last drawn wages. The key legal issue was whether the High Court's interim direction—granted while the NDMC's writ petitions challenging the Labour Court awards were pending—should be disturbed. Holding that the underlying writ petitions had remained pending for far too long, the Court disposed of the appeals and directed the Delhi High Court to hear and decide the writ petitions within three months, with no adjournments to either party, while leaving its own 2007 stay order undisturbed since it had already been operative for years.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court disposed of the appeals and directed the Delhi High Court to hear and decide the pending writ petitions within three months from the date of receipt of the order.
- The Supreme Court declined to alter the 2007 interim order solely on the ground that it had been in operation for several years.
- The parties to the writ petitions were directed not to seek any adjournments before the High Court.
- The Court clarified that if the writ petitions are not decided within the stipulated three-month period, it would be open to the respondents to pursue appropriate relief.
- Leave was granted in SLP(C) No. 2110 of 2011, and all three connected appeals were disposed of with a request for expeditious disposal by the High Court.
