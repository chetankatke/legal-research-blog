# 14087_2007

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 22.01.2007
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
This civil appeal before the Supreme Court challenged a judgment and order of the Madras High Court (Madurai Bench) in A.S. No. 237 of 1993 dated 22.01.2007. During the pendency of the appeal, the Court referred the matter to a Mediator in view of the parties' mutual agreement to seek an amicable settlement. The Mediator facilitated a settlement between the parties, and his report dated 31.03.2012 was placed on record, with both parties having arrived at an amicable resolution. The Court disposed of the appeal in terms of the settlement suggested by the Mediator, making the report part of the decree.
## Key Holdings
- The Supreme Court may refer a pending dispute to a Mediator when both parties' counsel agree to amicable settlement of their disputes.
- The report of a court-appointed Mediator, upon the parties arriving at a settlement, is taken on record and forms part of the decree.
- A civil appeal may be disposed of in terms of the settlement arrived at between the parties before the learned Mediator.

Court vide earlier orders dated 09.03.2011 had thought it fit to 
refer the matter to a Mediator for adjudication and decision in view 
of the fact that both the learned counsel for the parties had agreed 
for an amicable settlement of the dispute between themselves.
3.
In compliance of the aforesaid order passed by this Court, 
the learned Mediator has mediated the dispute between the parties 
and  thereafter  has  sent  his  report,  dated  31.03.2012  which  was 
received by this Court on 09.04.2012.  Both the parties have arrived 
at a settlement amicably before the learned Mediator. The report of 
the learned Mediator is taken on record and the same would form part 
of the decree.
