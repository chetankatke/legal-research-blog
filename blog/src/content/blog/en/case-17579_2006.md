## Summary
**Summary:**

This appeal before the Supreme Court of India (Dipak Misra, J.) challenged two orders of the Delhi High Court concerning the refund of Rs. 22 lakhs deposited by late Yash Pal Anand and his three brothers with the Engineering Export Promotion Council (EEPC) as a condition of bail in CBI cases registered against them under Sections 420, 468/471 IPC. ## Key Holdings
- When an accused is acquitted, the amount deposited with the agency as a condition of bail is refundable, especially where the deposit was made without prejudice to the accused's rights.
- The trial abates upon the death of the accused before its conclusion, as in the case of the original accused Yash Pal Anand.
- The right to claim refund of the bail deposit enures to the legal heirs/estate of the deceased accused who died during trial, as the amount was deposited without prejudice.
- The High Court's order directing refund of the bail amount deposited pursuant to the bail condition is a valid and enforceable direction against the Engineering Export Promotion Council.
- Once the trial against an accused ends in acquittal or abates due to death, the prosecuting/holding agency has no continuing right to retain the bail deposit.
