## Summary
**Summary:**

In *Nagappan v. State by Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu* (Criminal Appeal No. 1533 of 2009), the Supreme Court heard an appeal against the High Court's confirmation of the conviction of four accused (A-1 to A-4) for the murder of Pasupathy, who was attacked with knives, a stick, and an iron pipe on 08.05.2000 due to a pre-existing enmity. The trial court had convicted the accused under Section 302 read with Section 34 of the IPC, sentencing them to life imprisonment, and the High Court had upheld this conviction. The appeal before the Supreme Court arose from these concurrent findings of guilt, with the key legal question concerning the application of Section 34 (common intention) to the appellants' participation in the premeditated murder. The judgment excerpt reflects the appellate court's consideration of the evidence and the scope of joint liability for the killing.
