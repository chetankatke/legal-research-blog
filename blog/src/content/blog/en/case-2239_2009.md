## Summary
This is a criminal appeal in the Supreme Court of India challenging the Madhya Pradesh High Court's judgment upholding the appellant's conviction under Sections 376 and 506 Part II IPC (rape and criminal intimidation) for forcibly having intercourse with the prosecutrix at knife point in a sugarcane field on 28.9.1992. The prosecution's case rested on the prosecutrix's testimony (P.W.2), corroborated by medical evidence from Dr. Manju Saxena (P.W.1), who found the hymen torn in an irregular manner with two fingers easily insertable into the vagina, along with seized exhibits including slides and the peticoat. The key legal issue centers on whether the prosecution proved the appellant's guilt beyond reasonable doubt, particularly given certain inconsistencies in the prosecutrix's cross-examination testimony regarding injuries and the timing of washing blood stains.
