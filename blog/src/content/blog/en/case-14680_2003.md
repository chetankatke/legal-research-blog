# 14680_2003

## Citation
- **Court:** Supreme Court of India
- **Date:** 2.5.2003
- **Judges:** _Unknown_
- **Citation:** 

## Summary
**Summary:**

This case involves M/s KCP Ltd., a manufacturer of sugar and cement plant machinery, challenging the CESTAT's order denying MODVAT credit on certain machinery parts, electric cables, and equipment purchased from local dealers and exported to Vietnam for use in setting up a sugar plant for M/s Vina Sugars.
## Key Holdings
- MODVAT credit under Rule 57Q of the Central Excise Rules, 1944 cannot be availed on goods purchased from local market that are not used by the assessee in its own factory premises in relation to the manufacture of its final product.
- Goods purchased by the assessee and transported abroad as part of a turnkey plant installation in Vietnam do not qualify as 'capital goods' under Rule 57Q merely because they form part of the machinery exported.
- The definition of 'capital goods' under Rule 57Q requires that the goods be used in the factory of the manufacturer in connection with the production of excisable goods, and exported components do not satisfy this requirement.
- The Central Board of Excise and Customs is competent to direct a review of orders dropping show cause notices where the original adjudication is found to be inconsistent with the statutory provisions governing MODVAT credit.
- Mere assembly of purchased components with self-manufactured machinery in a container for export does not transform the purchased goods into capital goods eligible for MODVAT credit in the hands of the assessee.
