## Key Holdings
- The plaintiff was found ready and willing to perform his part of the agreement, establishing a valid case for specific performance.
- The objection under Section 12 of the Punjab Land Reforms Act, 1972 regarding permissible landholding limits was rejected based on a prior partition decree.
- The 1974 partition decree between the respondent and his sons was upheld as valid and not collusive, ensuring the respondent's landholding remained within permissible limits.
- The Trial Court and High Court were affirmed in their concurrent findings that the plaintiff was entitled to specific performance of the sale agreement.
